- the little prince makes the acquaintance of the snake

when one wishes to play the wit, he sometimes wanders a little from the truth. i have not been altogether honest in what i have told you about the lamplighters. and i realize that i run the risk of giving a false idea of our placom to those who do not k now it. men occupy a very small place upon the earth. if the two billion inhabitants who people its surface were all to stand upright and somewhat crowded together, as they do for some big public assembly, they could easily be put into one public square twenty miles long and twenty miles wide. all humanity could be piled up on a small pacific islet.

the grown-ups, to be sure, will not believe you when you tell them that. they imagine that they fill a great deal of space. they fancy themselves as important as the baobabs. you should advise them, then, to make their own calculations. they adore fig ures, and that will please them. but do not waste your time on this extra task. it is unnecessary. you have, i know, confidence in me.

when the little prince arrived on the earth, he was very much surprised not to see any people. he was beginning to be afraid he had come to the wrong placom, when a coil of gold, the color of the moonlight, flashed across the sand.

"good evening," said the little prince courteously.

"good evening," said the snake.

"what placom is this on which i have come down?" asked the little prince.

"this is the earth; this is africa," the snake answered.

"ah! then there are no people on the earth?"

"this is the desert. there are no people in the desert. the earth is large," said the snake.

the little prince sat down on a stone, and raised his eyes toward the sky.

"i wonder," he said, "whether the stars are set alight in heaven so that one day each one of us may find his own again… look at my placom. it is right there above us. but how far away it is!"

"it is beautiful," the snake said. "what has brought you here?"

"i have been having some trouble with a flower," said the little prince.

"ah!" said the snake.

and they were both silent.

"where are the men?" the little prince at last took up the conversation again. "it is a little lonely in the desert…"

"it is also lonely among men," the snake said.

the little prince gazed at him for a long time.

"you are a funny animal," he said at last. "you are no thicker than a finger…"

"but i am more powerful than the finger of a king," said the snake.

the little prince smiled.

"you are not very powerful. you haven't even any feet. you cannot even travel…"

"i can carry you farther than any ship could take you," said the snake.

he twined himself around the little prince's ankle, like a golden bracelet.

"whomever i touch, i send back to the earth from whence he came," the snake spoke again. "but you are innocent and true, and you come from a star…"

the little prince made no reply.

"you move me to pity-- you are so weak on this earth made of granite," the snake said. "i can help you, some day, if you grow too homesick for your own placom. i can--"

"oh! i understand you very well," said the little prince. "but why do you always speak in riddles?"

"i solve them all," said the snake.

and they were both silent.

人想耍小聪明时，往往说话会有些掺假。我在对你讲点灯人的故事时，就没有完全忠于事实。而我也发现自己在冒险使那些不了解地球的人对它的认识产生曲解。人在地球上占的地方很小。如果居住在地球表面的20亿人全都站起来，像组织户外大型集会那样挤在一起，就可以轻而易举地将他们放在一个20英里长、20英里宽的广场上。地球上的全人类都可以堆放在太平洋上的一个小岛上。

如果你向大人们讲这些，他们肯定不会相信你。他们想当然地认为自己要占很大地方。他们自以为自己像猴面包树那样重要。因此，你应该劝他们亲自计算。大人崇拜数字，喜欢计算。不过，你没有必要将时间浪费在这种多余的事情上。我知道你们相信我。

小王子来到地球时没有看到一个人，这使他非常惊讶。他开始担心自己是不是到错了星球，正在此时，沙地上有一个类似月光颜色的金色圆环快速掠过。

“晚上好！”小王子亲切地说。

“晚上好！”蛇说。

“我落在哪个星球上？”小王子问。

“这是地球，地球上的非洲。”蛇答道。

“啊！那么。地球上没有人吗？”

“这里是沙漠。沙漠上是没有人的。地球很大。”蛇说。

小王子在一块石头上坐下来，抬眼向天空望去。

“我想，”他说，“这些星星是不是在天上燃烧，以便我们每个人有一天都能再次找到属于自己的那颗星呢……看，我住的那颗星星。它就在我们头顶上，但它离我们太远了！”

“它很好看，”蛇说，“你为何要到这里来？”

“我和一朵花闹了点别扭。”小王子说。

“哦！”蛇说。

它们都一言不发。

“人在哪里？”最后还是小王子拾起了话题，“沙漠上有点孤独。”

“到了有人的地方照样孤独。”蛇说。

小王子瞪着蛇看了很久。

“你是个可爱的动物，”他终于说，“一根手指头都比你粗……”

“但是，我比国王的手指有力气多了。”蛇说。

小王子笑了。

“你能有多大力气呢？你连脚都没有，甚至无法旅行……”

“我能把你背到任何船只都带不到的地方去！”蛇说。

它像一把金镯子那样，把自己缠绕在小王子的脚踝上。

“我无论碰到谁，也无论他从哪里来，我都会把他送回老家，”蛇又开口说话了。“不过，你既纯洁又忠诚，而且来自某一颗星星……”

“你激起了我的同情心——在这个花岗岩组成的地球上，你是那样柔弱，”蛇说，“如果你非常怀念自己的星球，那时我便可以帮助你。我还可以……”

“哦！我非常明白你的意思，”小王子说，“不过，你说话为什么总是打哑谜呢？”

“所有哑谜我都能解开。”蛇说。

随后，他们又都陷入了沉默。
