Chapter 1

第一章

MANY YEARS LATER as he faced the firing squad, Colonel Aureliano Buendía was to remember that distant afternoon when his father took him to discover ice.

多年以后，面对行刑队，奥雷里亚诺·布恩迪亚上校将会回想起父亲带他去见识冰块的那个遥远的下午。

At that time Macondo was a village of twenty adobe houses, built on the bank of a river of clear water that ran along a bed of polished stones, which were white and enormous, like prehistoric eggs.

当时，马孔多是一个二十户人家的村落，泥巴和芦苇盖成的屋子沿河岸排开，湍急的河水清澈见底，河床里卵石洁白光滑宛如史前巨蛋。

The world was so recent that many things lacked names, and in order to indicate them it was necessary to point.

世界新生伊始，许多事物还没有名字，提到的时候尚需用手指指点点。

Every year during the month of March a family of ragged gypsies would set up their tents near the village, and with a great uproar of pipes and kettledrums they would display new inventions.

每年到了三月份，一家衣衫褴褛的吉卜赛人就会来到村边搭起帐篷，在铜管乐器和铙钹的喧闹声中，展示新近的发明。

First they brought the magnet.

首先他们带来的是磁铁。

A heavy gypsy with an untamed beard and sparrow hands, who introduced himself as Melquíades, put on a bold public demonstration of what he himself called the eighth wonder of the learned al-chemists of Macedonia.

一个身形庞大的吉卜赛人，胡须蓬乱，手如鸟爪，自称梅尔基亚德斯，向大家进行了一次骇人的展示，他称之为马其顿炼金大师创造的第八奇迹。

He went from house to house dragging two metal ingots and everybody was amazed to see pots, pans, tongs, and braziers tumble down from their places and beams creak from the desperation of nails and screws trying to emerge, and even objects that had been lost for a long time appeared from where they had been searched for most and went dragging along in turbulent confusion behind Melquíades' magical irons.

他拖着两块金属锭挨家挨户走着，大家惊奇地看到，铁锅、铁盆、铁钳、小铁炉纷纷跌落，木板因钉子和螺丝没命地挣脱出来而嘎吱作响，甚至连那些丢失多日的物件也在久寻不得的地方出现，一窝蜂似的跟在梅尔基亚德斯的魔铁后面乱滚。

“Things have a life of their own,” the gypsy proclaimed with a harsh accent.

“万物皆有灵，”吉卜赛人用刺耳的声调宣告，

“It's simply a matter of waking up their souls.” José Arcadio Buendía, whose unbridled imagination always went beyond the genius of nature and even beyond miracles and magic, thought that it would be possible to make use of that useless invention to extract gold from the bowels of the earth.

“只需唤起它们的灵性。”何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚的想象力经常超越大自然的创造，甚至超越了奇迹和魔法的境地，他认为可以利用这项无用的发明来挖掘地下的金子。

Melquíades, who was an honest man, warned him: “It won't work for that.” But José Arcadio Buendía at that time did not believe in the honesty of gypsies, so he traded his mule and a pair of goats for the two magnetized ingots.

梅尔基亚德斯是个诚实的人，他警告过：“这东西干那个不行。”但当时的何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚还不相信吉卜赛人的诚实，于是用自己的骡子和两只山羊换下了两块磁铁。

Úrsula Iguarán, his wife, who relied on those animals to increase their poor domestic holdings, was unable to dissuade him.

他的妻子乌尔苏拉·伊瓜兰本指望靠那些牲口扩展微薄的家业，却未能劝阻他。

“Very soon well have gold enough and more to pave the floors of the house,” her husband replied.

“用不了多久，我们就会有足够的金子铺房子的地面了。”她的丈夫答道。

For several months he worked hard to demonstrate the truth of his idea.

几个月来他费尽心机，试图证明自己的猜想。

He explored every inch of the region, even the riverbed, dragging the two iron ingots along and reciting Melquíades' incantation aloud.

他拖着两块铁锭，大声念起梅尔基亚德斯的咒语，踏遍了那一带的每一寸土地，连河床都没放过。

The only thing he succeeded in doing was to unearth a suit of fifteenth-century armor which had all of its pieces soldered together with rust and inside of which there was the hollow resonance of an enormous stone-filled gourd.

他唯一的收获是一套十五世纪的盔甲，所有的部件都因锈蚀而粘结在一起，敲上去里面发出空洞的回声，仿佛一个塞满石头的大葫芦。

When José Arcadio Buendía and the four men of his expedition managed to take the armor apart, they found inside a calcified skeleton with a copper locket containing a woman's hair around its neck.

何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚和他的四名探险队员费尽九牛二虎之力拆开盔甲，发现里面有一具钙化的骷髅，骷髅的脖子上挂着一个铜圣物匣，里面有一绺女人的头发。

In March the gypsies returned.

到了三月，吉卜赛人又来了。

This time they brought a telescope and a magnifying glass the size of a drum, which they exhibited as the latest discovery of the Jews of Amsterdam.

这次他们带来了一架望远镜和一面像鼓一样大的放大镜，展出时声称是阿姆斯特丹犹太人的最新发明。

They placed a gypsy woman at one end of the village and set up the telescope at the entrance to the tent.

他们让一个吉卜赛女人坐在村子的一头，把望远镜安在帐篷入口。

For the price of five reales, people could look into the telescope and see the gypsy woman an arm's length away.

花上五个里亚尔，人们就能凑近望远镜，看到那个吉卜赛女人仿佛近在咫尺。

“Science has eliminated distance,” Melquíades proclaimed.

“科学消除了距离，”梅尔基亚德斯宣告，

“In a short time, man will be able to see what is happening in any place in the world without leaving his own house.” A burning noonday sun brought out a startling demonstration with the gigantic magnifying glass: they put a pile of dry hay in the middle of the street and set it on fire by concentrating the sun's rays.

“用不了多久，人们就能足不出户，看到世界上任何地方发生的事情。”正午时分，骄阳似火，他们用那面巨大的放大镜做了一次惊人的展示：他们在街道中央放了一堆干草，利用阳光聚焦，竟将干草点燃。

José Arcadio Buendía, who had still not been consoled for the failure of big magnets, conceived the idea of using that invention as a weapon of war.

何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚尚未从磁铁的失败中恢复过来，此时又产生了一个主意，要将这项发明用作战争武器。

Again Melquíades tried to dissuade him, but he finally accepted the two magnetized ingots and three colonial coins in exchange for the magnifying glass.

梅尔基亚德斯再次试图劝阻，但最后还是同意用放大镜换取两块磁铁和三枚殖民地时期的金币。

Úrsula wept in consternation.

乌尔苏拉难过得哭了。

That money was from a chest of gold coins that her father had put together ova an entire life of privation and that she had buried underneath her bed in hopes of a proper occasion to make use of it.

那点钱是她父亲一生省吃俭用攒下的一箱金币中的一部分，她一直将其埋在床下，期盼着有个好机会投资。

José Arcadio Buendía made no attempt to console her, completely absorbed in his tactical experiments with the abnegation of a scientist and even at the risk of his own life.

何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚没有试图安慰她，他以科学家的忘我精神，甚至冒着生命危险，全身心地投入战术实验。

In an attempt to show the effects of the glass on enemy troops, he exposed himself to the concentration of the sun's rays and suffered burns which turned into sores that took a long time to heal.

为了证明放大镜对敌军的效果，他将自己暴露在阳光焦点下，结果严重烧伤，伤口变成了溃疡，很久才愈合。

Over the protests of his wife, who was alarmed at such a dangerous invention, at one point he was ready to set the house on fire.

妻子对这种危险的发明感到惊恐，抗议不断，但他不顾一切，甚至差点把自家的房子点燃。

He would spend hours on end in his room, calculating the strategic possibilities of his novel weapon until he succeeded in putting together a manual of startling instructional clarity and an irresistible power of conviction.

他连续数小时把自己关在房间里，计算这种新武器的战略潜力，终于编写出一本说明手册，其清晰的指导性和不可抗拒的说服力令人惊叹。

He sent it to the government, accompanied by numerous descriptions of his experiments and several pages of explanatory sketches; by a messenger who crossed the mountains, got lost in measureless swamps, forded stormy rivers, and was on the point of perishing under the lash of despair, plague, and wild beasts until he found a route that joined the one used by the mules that carried the mail.

他将其连同实验的详细描述和几页附图，托付给一名信使送交政府。那名信使翻山越岭，在无边无际的沼泽中迷失方向，涉过湍急的河流，在绝望、瘟疫和野兽的折磨下险些丧命，最后终于找到一条与运邮骡队汇合的路线。

In spite of the fact that a trip to the capital was little less than impossible at that time, José Arcadio Buendía promised to undertake it as soon as the government ordered him to so that he could put on some practical demonstrations of his invention for the military authorities and could train them himself in the complicated art of solar war.

尽管当时去一趟首都几乎是不可能的，但何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚承诺，只要政府下令，他立刻启程去向军方展示发明的实际效果，并亲自传授太阳战的复杂战术。

For several years he waited for an answer.

他等了好几年。

Finally, tired of waiting, he bemoaned to Melquíades the failure of his project and the gypsy then gave him a convincing proof of his honesty: he gave him back the doubloons in exchange for the magnifying glass, and he left him in addition some Portuguese maps and several instruments of navigation.

最后等得不耐烦了，他向梅尔基亚德斯抱怨这项事业的挫败。吉卜赛人随即拿出了令人信服的证据证明了自己的诚实：他退还了金币，收回了放大镜，并留下了几幅葡萄牙地图和几件航海仪器。

In his own handwriting he set down a concise synthesis of the studies by Monk Hermann. which he left José Arcadio so that he would be able to make use of the astrolabe, the compass, and the sextant.

他亲笔写下赫尔曼修士研究成果的简明提要留给何塞·阿卡迪奥，以便他能学会使用星盘、罗盘和六分仪。

José Arcadio Buendía spent the long months of the rainy season shut up in a small room that he had built in the rear of the house so that no one would disturb his experiments.

在漫长的雨季里，何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚将自己关在他在房子深处搭建的一个小房间里，以免有人打扰他的实验。

Having completely abandoned his domestic obligations, he spent entire nights in the courtyard watching the course of the stars and he almost contracted sunstroke from trying to establish an exact method to ascertain noon.

他完全抛弃了家庭责任，整夜待在院子里观察星象运行，为了找出一个精准测定正午的方法，他差点中暑。

When he became an expert in the use and manipulation of his instruments, he conceived a notion of space that allowed him to navigate across unknown seas, to visit uninhabited territories, and to establish relations with splendid beings without having to leave his study.

当他精通了这些仪器的使用后，他对空间有了新的认识，这让他无需离开书房，就能在未知的大海中航行，探访荒凉的土地，并与非凡的生灵建立联系。

That was the period in which he acquired the habit of talking to himself, of walking through the house without paying attention to anyone, as Úrsula and the children broke their backs in the garden, growing banana and caladium, cassava and yams, ahuyama roots and eggplants.

正是那段时期，他养成了自言自语的习惯，在房子里走来走去，对谁都不理睬；而乌尔苏拉和孩子们却在菜园里累弯了腰，照料着香蕉和海芋、木薯和山药、南瓜和茄子。

Suddenly, with-out warning, his feverish activity was interrupted and was replaced by a kind of fascination.

突然，毫无征兆地，他狂热的活动中断了，取而代之的是一种迷惘。

He spent several days as if he were bewitched, softly repeating to himself a string of fearful conjectures without giving credit to his own understanding.

好几天他仿佛中了魔一样，低声对自己重复着一连串可怕的猜测，连他自己都不敢相信。

Finally, one Tuesday in December, at lunchtime, all at once he released the whole weight of his torment.

最后，在十二月的一个星期二的午餐时分，他将心中的痛苦全盘托出。

The children would remember for the rest of their lives the august solemnity with which their father, devastated by his prolonged vigil and by the wrath of his imagination, revealed his discovery to them:

孩子们将终生记得父亲当时庄严的模样，他被长时间的熬夜和过度发挥的想象力折磨得精疲力竭，向他们透露了自己的发现：

“The earth is round, like an orange.”

“地球是圆的，就像一个橘子。”

Úrsula lost her patience.

乌尔苏拉失去了耐心。

“If you have to go crazy, please go crazy all by yourself!” she shouted.

“你要发疯，就自己去疯！”她大喊，

“But don't try to put your gypsy ideas into the heads of the children.” José Arcadio Buendía, impassive, did not let himself be frightened by the desperation of his wife, who, in a seizure of rage, mashed the astrolabe against the floor.

“别把那些吉卜赛人的鬼点子塞进孩子们的脑子里。”何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚无动于衷，没被妻子绝望的神情吓倒。妻子一怒之下把星盘摔碎在地上。

He built another one, he gathered the men of the village in his little room, and he demonstrated to them, with theories that none of them could understand, the possibility of returning to where one had set out by consistently sailing east.

他又造了一个，把村里的男人们召集在小房间里，用他们谁也听不懂的理论，向他们证明一直往东航行就有可能回到出发点。

The whole village was convinced that José Arcadio Buendía had lost his reason, when Melquíades returned to set things straight.

全村人都确信何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚失去了理智，就在这时，梅尔基亚德斯回来澄清了事实。

He gave public praise to the intelligence of a man who from pure astronomical speculation had evolved a theory that had already been proved in practice, although unknown in Macondo until then, and as a proof of his admiration he made him a gift that was to have a profound influence on the future of the village: the laboratory of an alchemist.

他公开赞扬了这个人的智慧，竟然仅凭天文学推测就得出了一个已经在实践中得到证明的理论，尽管当时在马孔多尚无人知晓；为了表示敬意，他送给他一件对村子未来产生深远影响的礼物：一个炼金室。

By then Melquíades had aged with surprising rapidity.

那时的梅尔基亚德斯衰老得惊人。

On his first trips he seemed to be the same age as José Arcadio Buendía.

初次见面时，他看起来似乎和何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚年纪相仿。

But while the latter had preserved his extraordinary strength, which permitted him to pull down a horse by grabbing its ears, the gypsy seemed to have been worn dowse by some tenacious illness.

但后者还保持着非凡的力量，能揪住马耳朵把马拉倒；而吉卜赛人却仿佛被某种顽疾折磨得心力交瘁。

It was, in reality, the result of multiple and rare diseases contracted on his innumerable trips around the world.

实际上，这是他在世界各地无数次游历中染上的各种罕见疾病的结果。

According to what he himself said as he spoke to José Arcadio Buendía while helping him set up the laboratory, death followed him everywhere, sniffing at the cuffs of his pants, but never deciding to give him the final clutch of its claws.

据他本人在帮何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚建立炼金室时所说，死神到处追随着他，闻着他裤脚的气味，但一直没有决定向他伸出最后的利爪。

He was a fugitive from all the plagues and catastrophes that had ever lashed mankind.

他是人类所遭受的一切瘟疫和灾难的幸存者。

He had survived pellagra in Persia, scurvy inthe Malayan archipelago , leprosy in Alexandria, beriberi in Japan, bubonic plague in Madagascar, an earthquake in Sicily, and a disastrous shipwreck in the Strait of Magel-lan.

他在波斯躲过了糙皮病，在马来群岛逃过了坏血病，在亚历山大逃过了麻风病，在日本逃过了脚气病，在马达加斯加逃过了腺鼠疫，在西西里逃过了地震，在麦哲伦海峡逃过了毁灭性的海难。

That prodigious creature, said to possess the keys of Nostradamus, was a gloomy man, enveloped in a sad aura, with an Asiatic look that seemed to know what there was on the other side of things.

这个据说掌握着诺查丹玛斯密码的奇人，却是个性格阴郁的人，浑身笼罩着一种悲凉的气息，长着一副亚洲人的面孔，仿佛能看透事物的另一面。

He wore a large black hat that looked like a raven with widespread wings, and a velvet vest across which the patina of the centuries had skated.

他戴着一顶大黑帽，像是一只展翅的乌鸦，穿着一件仿佛历经几个世纪风霜的天鹅绒背心。

But in spite of his immense wisdom and his mysterious breadth, he had a human burden, an earthly condition that kept him involved in the small problems of daily life.

尽管他拥有无尽的智慧和神秘的背景，但他仍有着普通人的重负，世俗的境遇使他无法摆脱日常生活琐事的羁绊。

He would complain of the ailments of old age, he suffered from the most insignificant economic difficulties, and he had stopped laughing a long time back because scurvy had made his teeth drop out.

他会抱怨衰老带来的病痛，会为微不足道的经济困难而发愁，而且他早就停止了欢笑，因为坏血病让他的牙齿掉光了。

On that suffocating noontime when the gypsy revealed his secrets, José Arcadio Buendía had the certainty that it was the beginning of a great friendship.

在那个令人窒息的正午，当吉卜赛人向他吐露这些秘密时，何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚确信，这是一段伟大友谊的开端。

The children were startled by his fantastic stories.

孩子们被他奇妙的故事惊呆了。

Aureliano, who could not have been more than five at the time, would remember him for the rest of his life as he saw him that afternoon, sitting against the metallic and quivering light from the window, lighting up with his deep organ voice the darkest reaches of the imagination, while down over his temples there flowed the grease that was being melted by the heat.

当时还不到五岁的奥雷里亚诺，一辈子也忘不了那天下午见他的情景：他背对着窗户里射入的闪烁着金属光泽的光线坐着，用低沉的管风琴般的声音，照亮了想象力最深邃的角落，汗水融化的油脂顺着他的鬓角流下。

José Arcadio, his older brother, would pass on that wonderful image as a hereditary memory to all of his descendants.

他的哥哥何塞·阿卡迪奥，将把这奇妙的画面当作家族记忆传给所有的后代。

Úrsula on the other hand, held a bad memory of that visit, for she had entered the room just as Melquíades had carelessly broken a flask of bichloride of mercury.

而乌尔苏拉对那次拜访的印象却糟透了，因为她走进房间时，梅尔基亚德斯正好不小心打碎了一瓶二氯化汞。

“It's the smell of the devil,” she said.

“这是魔鬼的气味，”她说。

“Not at all,” Melquíades corrected her.

“一点也不，”梅尔基亚德斯纠正她。

“It has been proven that the devil has sulphuric properties and this is just a little corrosive sublimate.”

“事实证明，魔鬼具有硫磺的特性，这只不过是一点升汞。”

Always didactic, he went into a learned exposition of the diabolical properties of cinnabar, but Úrsula paid no attention to him, although she took the children off to pray.

他总是喜欢说教，滔滔不绝地讲起朱砂的邪恶属性，但乌尔苏拉根本不理他，只是把孩子们带走去祈祷。

That biting odor would stay forever in her mind linked to the memory of Melquíades.

那刺鼻的气味将永远留在她的脑海里，与梅尔基亚德斯的记忆交织在一起。

The rudimentary laboratory–in addition to a profusion of pots, funnels, retorts, filters, and sieves–was made up of a primitive water pipe, a glass beaker with a long, thin neck, a reproduction of the philosopher's egg, and a still the gypsies themselves had built in accordance with modern descriptions of the three-armed alembic of Mary the Jew.

那个简陋的炼金室——除了一堆坩埚、漏斗、曲颈瓶、过滤器和筛子之外——还包括一根原始的水管，一个长细颈玻璃烧杯，一个“哲人卵”复制品，以及吉卜赛人自己根据现代有关犹太人玛利亚的三臂蒸馏器的描述建造的一套蒸馏装置。

Along with those items, Melquíades left samples of the seven metals that corresponded to the seven planets, the formulas of Moses and Zosimus for doubling the quantity of gold, and a set of notes and sketches concerning the processes of the Great Teaching that would permit those who could interpret them to undertake the manufacture of the philosopher's stone.

伴随着这些物件，梅尔基亚德斯还留下了与七大行星相对应的七种金属样本，摩西和佐西姆斯关于金子翻倍的公式，以及一套关于大秘宝炼制工序的笔记和草图，只要能看懂，就能着手炼制点金石。

Seduced by the simplicity of the formulas to double the quantity of gold, José Arcadio Buendía paid court to Úrsula for several weeks so that she would let him dig up her colonial coins and increase them by as many times as it was possible to subdivide mercury.

金子翻倍的公式看起来极其简单，何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚受其诱惑，连续几周对乌尔苏拉大献殷勤，好让她允许自己把埋藏的殖民地金币挖出来，让它们的数量随着水银的细分翻上几番。

Úrsula gave in, as always, to her husband's unyielding obstinacy.

像往常一样，乌尔苏拉在丈夫的不屈不挠面前让步了。

Then José Arcadio Buendía threw three doubloons into a pan and fused them with copper filings, orpiment, brimstone, and lead.

于是，何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚把三枚金币扔进平底锅，将它们与铜屑、雌黄、硫磺和铅熔合在一起。

He put it all to boil in a pot of castor oil until he got a thick and pestilential syrup which was more like common caramel than valuable gold.

他把这一切放在一锅蓖麻油里熬煮，直到熬成一种浓稠而恶臭的糖浆，与其说是贵重的金子，倒不如说更像普通的焦糖。

In risky and desperate processes of distillation, melted with the seven planetary metals, mixed with hermetic mercury and vitriol of Cyprus, and put back to cook in hog fat for lack of any radish oil, Úrsula's precious inheritance was reduced to a large piece of burnt hog cracklings that was firmly stuck to the bottom of the pot.

经过一系列危险而绝望的蒸馏，与七种行星金属熔合，加入赫尔墨斯水银和塞浦路斯硫酸，因为缺少萝卜油，他又用猪油重新熬煮，最后乌尔苏拉宝贵的遗产变成了一大块烧焦的油渣，牢牢地粘在锅底。

When the gypsies came back, Úrsula had turned the whole population of the village against them.

吉卜赛人再来的时候，乌尔苏拉已经煽动全村人反对他们。

But curiosity was greater than fear, for that time the gypsies went about the town making a deafening noise with all manner of musical instruments while a hawkeran nounced the exhibition of the most fabulous discovery of the Naciancenes.

但好奇心战胜了恐惧，因为这次吉卜赛人用各种乐器制造出震耳欲聋的喧闹在村里游走，同时一名小贩高声宣告，要展示纳西安人最不可思议的发现。

So that everyone went to the tent and by paying one cent they saw a youthful Mel-quíades, recovered, unwrinkled, with a new and flashing set of teeth.

于是大家都涌向帐篷，花上一分钱，就能看到一个年轻的梅尔基亚德斯，他恢复了健康，脸上没有皱纹，还换上了一副闪闪发光的新牙。

Those who remembered his gums that had been destroyed by scurvy, his flaccid cheeks, and his withered lips trembled with fear at the final proof of the gypsy's supernatural power.

那些还记得他被坏血病毁掉的牙龈、松弛的脸颊和干瘪的嘴唇的人，面对这吉卜赛人超自然力量的确凿证据，都吓得浑身发抖。

The fear turned into panic when Melquíades took out his teeth, intact, encased in their gums, and showed them to the audience for an instant–a fleeting instant in which he went back to being the same decrepit man of years past–and put them back again and smiled once more with the full control of his restored youth.

当梅尔基亚德斯把完好的牙齿连同牙龈一起掏出来，向观众展示了片刻——在这短暂的片刻里，他又变回了多年前那个衰老的老头——然后又把它们安回去，再度展露出重获青春的自信笑容时，恐惧变成了恐慌。

Even José Arcadio Buen-día himself considered that Melquíades' knowledge had reached unbearable extremes, but he felt a healthy excitement when the gypsy explained to him atone the workings of his false teeth.

就连何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚本人也觉得梅尔基亚德斯的知识已经达到了令人无法忍受的极端，但当吉卜赛人单独向他解释假牙的原理时，他感到了一阵释然的兴奋。

It seemed so simple and so prodigious at the same time that overnight he lost all interest in his experiments in alchemy.

这原理显得如此简单又如此奇妙，一夜之间他便对炼金术实验完全失去了兴趣。

He underwent a new crisis of bad humor.

他又陷入了一场新的烦躁危机。

He did not go back to eating regularly, and he would spend the day walking through the house.

他不按时吃饭，整天在屋子里踱步。

“Incredible things are happening in the world,” he said to Úrsula.

“这世界上正发生着不可思议的事情，”他对乌尔苏拉说，

“Right there across the river there are all kinds of magical instruments while we keep on living like donkeys.” Those who had known him since the foundation of Macondo were startled at how much he had changed under Melquíades' influence.

“就在河对岸，有各种神奇的机器，而我们却像驴子一样生活。”那些从建村起就认识他的人，都对他受梅尔基亚德斯影响后发生的变化感到震惊。

At first José Arcadio Buendía had been a kind of youthful patriarch who would give instructions for plant-ing and advice for the raising of children and animals, and who collaborated with everyone, even in the physical work, for the welfare of the community.

起初，何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚是个年轻的大家长式的领袖，他指导播种，为抚养孩子和饲养家畜出谋划策，为了社区的利益他与所有人合作，甚至亲自参与体力劳动。

Since his house from the very first had been the best in the village, the others had been built in its image and likeness.

因为他的房子从一开始就是村里最好的，其他房子都是仿照它建造的。

It had a small, well-lighted living roost, a dining room in the shape of a terrace with gaily colored flowers, two bedrooms, a courtyard with a gigantic chestnut tree, a well kept garden, and a corral where goats, pigs, and hens lived in peaceful communion.

它有一个明亮的小客厅，一个带花卉盆栽的露台式餐厅，两间卧室，一个长着一棵巨大栗树的院子，一个精心打理的菜园，还有一个养着山羊、猪和母鸡和睦相处的围栏。

The only animals that were prohibited, not just in his house but in the entire settlement, were fighting cocks.

唯一被禁止饲养的动物，不仅在他家，在整个村落都是如此，那就是斗鸡。

Úrsula's capacity for work was the same as that of her husband.

乌尔苏拉的干劲与她丈夫不相上下。

Active, small, severe, that woman of un-breakable nerves who at no moment in her life had been heard to sing seemed to be everywhere, from dawn until quite late at night, always pursued by the soft whispering of her stiff, starched petticoats.

她精明能干，身材娇小，不苟言笑，这个神经坚如钢铁的女人，一生中从未被人听见唱过歌；从黎明到深夜，她似乎无处不在，身后总是伴随着她那上了浆的硬挺衬裙发出的沙沙声。

Thanks to her the floors of tamped earth, the unwhitewashed mud walls, the rustic, wooden furniture they had built them-selves were always dean, and the old chests where they kept their clothes exhaled the warm smell of basil.

多亏了她，夯土的地面，未粉刷的泥墙，他们自己打造的粗糙木家具，总是干干净净；存放衣物的旧箱子散发着罗勒的温和气息。

José Arcadio Buendía, who was the most enterprising man ever to be seen in the village, had set up the placement of the houses in such a way that from all of them one could reach the river and draw water with the same effort, and he had lined up the streets with such good sense that no house got more sun than another during the hot time of day.

何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚是村里罕见的最具进取心的人，他规划了房屋的布局，确保每家每户到河边打水的路程都一样远；他巧妙地规划了街道，使得在一天中最热的时候，没有哪栋房子比其他房子受到更多日照。

Within a few years Macondo was a village that was more orderly and hard working than any known until then by its three hundred inhabitants.

短短几年内，马孔多就成了其三百名居民所见过的最井然有序、最勤奋的村庄。

It was a truly happy village where no one was over thirty years of age and where no one had died.

这是一个真正幸福的村落，那里没有人超过三十岁，也从未有人死去。

Since the time of its founding, José Arcadio Buendía had built traps and cages.

从建村之初，何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚就制作了捕鸟器和鸟笼。

In a short time he filled not only his own house but all of those in the village with troupials, canaries, bee eaters, and redbreasts.

没过多久，他不仅在自己家，而且在村里所有的房子里都挂满了装着拟黄鹂、金丝雀、食蜂鸟和知更鸟的笼子。

The concert of so many different birds became so disturbing that Úrsula would plug her ears with beeswax so as not to lose her sense of reality.

如此多不同鸟类的合唱变得如此喧闹，以至于乌尔苏拉不得不用蜂蜡塞住耳朵，以免失去现实感。

The first time that Melquíades' tribe arrived, selling glass balls for headaches, everyone was surprised that they had been able to find that village lost in the drowsiness of the swamp, and the gypsies confessed that they had found their way by the song of the birds.

梅尔基亚德斯的部落第一次来到这里兜售治疗头痛的玻璃球时，大家都很惊讶他们是怎么找到这个迷失在昏沉沼泽中的村庄的，吉卜赛人坦白说，他们是循着鸟鸣声找来的。

That spirit of social initiative disappearedin a short time, pulled away by the fever of the magnets, the astronomical calculations, the dreams of transmutation, and the urge to discover the wonders of the world.

那种造福社会的进取精神没过多久便消逝了，被磁铁的狂热、天文的演算、点金的梦想和探索世界奇迹的渴望所取代。

From a clean and active man,José Arcadio Buendía changed into a man lazy in appearance, careless in his dress, with a wild beard that Úrsula managed to trim with great effort and a kitchen knife.

何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚从一个整洁勤奋的人，变成了一个外表邋遢、衣衫不整的人；他留着野草般的胡须，乌尔苏拉费了九牛二虎之力才用菜刀帮他修剪了一番。

There were many who considered him the victim of some strange spell.

许多人认为他是中了某种怪咒。

But even those most convinced of his madness left work and family to follow him when he brought out his tools to clear the land and asked the assembled group to open a way that would put Macondo in contact with the great inventions.

但是，当他拿出开荒工具，号召大家开辟一条让马孔多与伟大发明接轨的道路时，即使是那些最确信他疯了的人，也抛下工作和家人追随他。

José Arcadio Buendía was completely ignorant of the geography of the region.

何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚对当地的地形一无所知。

He knew that to the east there lay an impenetrable mountain chain and that on the other side of the mountains there was the ardent city of Riohacha, where in times past–according to what he had been told by the first Aureliano Buendía, his grandfather–Sir Francis Drake had gone crocodile hunting with cannons and that he repaired hem and stuffed them with straw to bring to Queen Elizabeth.

他知道东边是一条难以逾越的山脉，山的那边是炎热的里奥阿查城；据他的祖父，第一位奥雷里亚诺·布恩迪亚讲，过去弗朗西斯·德雷克爵士曾在那儿用大炮猎杀鳄鱼，他将鳄鱼修补好，填满干草，带回去献给伊丽莎白女王。

In his youth, José Arcadio Buendía and his men, with wives and children, animals and all kinds of domestic implements, had crossed the mountains in search of an outlet to the sea, and after twenty-six months they gave up the expedition and founded Macondo, so they would not have to go back.

年轻时，何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚和他的同伴们带着妻儿、牲口和各种家什，翻山越岭寻找出海口；二十六个月后，他们放弃了探险，建立了马孔多，这样就不用再回去了。

It was, therefore, a route that did not interest him, for it could lead only to the past.

因此，这是一条他不感兴趣的路线，因为它只会通向过去。

To the south lay the swamps, covered with an eternal vegetable scum and the whole vast universe of the great swamp, which, according to what the gypsies said, had no limits.

南边是沼泽，覆盖着一层永远也除不尽的植物黏液；还有广袤无垠的巨大水域，据吉卜赛人说，那里没有尽头。

The great swamp in the west mingled with a boundless extension of water where there were soft-skinned ceta-ceans that had the head and torso of a woman, causing the ruination of sailors with the charm of their extraordinary breasts.

西边的大沼泽与一片无边无际的水域相连，那里有一种皮肤柔软的鲸类动物，长着女人的头和身体，它们用迷人的乳房让水手们走向毁灭。

The gypsies sailed along that route for six months before they reached the strip of land over which the mules that carried the mail passed.

吉卜赛人沿着那条路线航行了六个月，才到达运邮骡队经过的那片狭长陆地。

According to José ArcadioBuendía's calculations, the only possibility of contact with civilization lay along the northern route.

根据何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚的计算，与文明接触的唯一可能性在北方。

So he handed out clearing tools and hunting weapons to the same men who had been with him during the founding of Macondo.

于是他将开荒工具和狩猎武器分发给那些当年和他一起建立马孔多的男人们。

He threw his directional instruments and his maps into a knapsack, and he undertook the reckless adventure.

他把定向仪器和地图塞进背包，踏上了这次鲁莽的冒险之旅。

During the first days they did not come across any appreciable obstacle.

最初的几天，他们没有遇到任何明显的障碍。

They went down along the stony bank of the river to the place where years before they had found the soldier's armor, and from there they went into the woods along a path between wild orange trees.

他们顺着布满石块的河岸往下走，来到了几年前发现士兵盔甲的地方，然后沿着野橘树间的一条小路走进了森林。

At the end of the first week they killed and roasted a deer, but they agreed to eat only half of it and salt the rest for the days that lay ahead.

第一周结束时，他们猎杀并烤熟了一只鹿，但大家同意只吃一半，把剩下的一半用盐腌起来，以备不时之需。

With that precaution they tried to postpone the necessity of having to eat macaws, whose blue flesh had a harsh and musky taste.

采取这一预防措施，是为了尽量推迟吃金刚鹦鹉的时间，那种鸟蓝色的肉带着一股刺鼻的麝香味。

Then, for more than ten days, they did not see the sun again.

随后，有十多天他们再也没见过太阳。

The ground became soft and damp, like volcanic ash, and the vegetation was thicker and thicker, and the cries of the birds and the uproar of the monkeys became more and more remote, and the world became eternally sad.

地面变得柔软潮湿，像火山灰一样；植被越来越茂密，鸟鸣声和猴子的喧闹声渐行渐远，世界变得永远沉寂悲凉。

The men on the expedition felt overwhelmed by their most ancient memories in that paradise of dampness and silence, going back to before original sin, as their boots sank into pools of steaming oil and their machetes destroyed bloody lilies and golden salamanders.

在这个潮湿寂静的乐园里，探险队的人们被他们最古老的记忆所吞没，仿佛回到了原罪发生之前；他们的靴子陷进冒着热气的油池里，大砍刀无情地砍碎了血红色的百合和金色的蝾螈。

For a week, almost without speaking, they went ahead like sleepwalkers through a universe of grief, lighted only by the tenuous reflection of luminous in-sects, and their lungs were overwhelmed by a suffocating smell of blood.

整整一个星期，他们几乎一言不发，像梦游者一样在一个充满悲怆的宇宙中穿行，只有发光昆虫的微弱光芒照亮前路，一股令人窒息的血腥味充斥着他们的肺腑。

They could not return because the strip that they were opening as they went along would soon close up with a new vegetation that. almost seemed to grow before their eyes.

他们无法回头，因为他们边走边开辟的小径很快就被新长出的植被覆盖了，那些植物简直像是在他们眼皮底下疯长。

“It's all right,” José Arcadio Buendía would say.

“没关系，”何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚说，

“The main thing is not to lose our bearings.” Always following his compass, he kept on guiding his men toward the invisible north so that they would be able to get out of that enchanted region.

“关键是不要迷失方向。”他始终盯着罗盘，不断指引同伴们向看不见的北方前进，试图带领他们走出这片被施了魔法的区域。

It was a thick night, starless, but the darkness was becoming impregnated with a fresh and clear air.

那是一个漆黑无星的夜晚，但黑暗中开始弥漫着一股清新澄澈的空气。

Exhausted by the long crossing, they hung up their hammocks and slept deeply for the first time in two weeks.

经历了漫长的跋涉，他们筋疲力尽地挂好吊床，两周来第一次沉沉睡去。

When they woke up, with the sun already high in the sky, they were speechless with fascination.

当他们醒来时，太阳已经高挂在空中，眼前的景象令他们着迷得说不出话来。

Before them, surrounded by ferns and palm trees, white and powdery in the silent morning light, was an enormous Spanish galleon.

在他们面前，被蕨类植物和棕榈树包围着，在静谧的晨光中泛着苍白粉末状光泽的，是一艘巨大的西班牙大帆船。

Tilted slightly to the starboard, it had hanging from its intact masts the dirty rags of its sails in the midst of its rigging, which was adorned with orchids.

它向右舷微微倾斜，完好无损的桅杆上挂着破布条般的船帆，索具上开满了兰花。

The hull, covered with an armor of petrified barnacles and soft moss, was firmly fastened into a surface of stones.

船身覆盖着一层石化的藤壶和柔软的青苔，牢牢地嵌在一片石地上。

The whole structure seemed to occupy its own space, one of solitude and oblivion, protected from the vices of time and the habits of the birds.

这庞然大物似乎拥有自己的空间，一个被孤独和遗忘所包围的空间，免受了时光的侵蚀和鸟类的袭扰。

Inside, where the expeditionaries explored with careful intent, there was nothing but a thick forest of flowers.

探险队员们小心翼翼地进去探查，发现里面除了密密麻麻的花丛，什么也没有。

The discovery of the galleon, an indication of the proximity of the sea, broke José Arcadio Buendía's drive.

发现大帆船意味着大海就在附近，这一下子打破了何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚的动力。

He considered it a trick of his whimsical fate to have searched for the sea without finding it, at the cost of countless sacrifices and suffering, and to have found it all of a sudden without looking for it, as if it lay across his path like an insurmountable object.

他觉得这是命运在跟他开无常的玩笑：他付出了无数的牺牲和苦难去寻找大海却一无所获；而在他不再寻找时，它却突然出现，像一个无法逾越的障碍横亘在他的路上。

Many years later Colonel Aureliano Buendía crossed the region again, when it was already a regular mail route, and the only part of the ship he found was its burned-out frame in the midst of a field of poppies.

许多年后，奥雷里亚诺·布恩迪亚上校再次穿越该地区，当时那里已经是一条定期的邮政路线；他唯一找到的，是这艘船在一片罂粟花海中被烧毁的骨架。

Only then, convinced that the story had not been some product of his father's imagination, did he wonder how the galleon had been able to get inland to that spot.

直到那时，他才确信这个故事绝非他父亲的臆想，他不禁疑惑，这艘大帆船是如何深入内陆到达那个地方的。

But José Arcadio Buendía did not concern himself with that when he found the sea after another four days' journey from the galleon.

但是，当何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚离开大帆船又走了四天，终于看到大海时，他根本没有考虑这个问题。

His dreams ended as he faced that ashen, foamy, dirty sea, which had not merited the risks and sacrifices of the adventure.

面对这片灰白、泛着泡沫、浑浊不堪的大海，他的梦想破灭了；它根本不值得他冒那么大的风险，付出那么多的牺牲。

“God damn it!” he shouted.

“该死！”他大喊，

“Macondo is surrounded by water on all sides.”

“马孔多四面都是水。”

The idea of a peninsular Macondo prevailed for a long time, inspired by the arbitrary map that José Arcadio Buendía sketched on his return from the expedition.

马孔多是个半岛的观念流传了很长一段时间，这源于何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚探险归来后随意绘制的一张地图。

He drew it in rage, evilly, exaggerating the difficulties of communication, as if to punish himself for the absolute lack of sense with which he had chosen the place.

他满怀愤怒，不怀好意地画了这张图，夸大了交通的困难，仿佛在惩罚自己当初毫无常识地选择了这个地方。

“We'll never get anywhere,” he lamented to Úrsula.

“我们哪儿也去不了，”他向乌尔苏拉哀叹，

“We're going to rot our lives away here without receiving the benefits of science.” That certainty, mulled over for several months in the small room he used as his laboratory, brought him to the conception of the plan to move Maeondo to a better place.

“我们会在这里烂掉，永远享受不到科学的好处。”这个念头被他在当作实验室的小房间里反复盘算了几个月，最终使他萌生了将马孔多迁往一个更好地方的计划。

But that time Úrsula had anticipated his feverish designs.

但那一次，乌尔苏拉预料到了他狂热的企图。

With the secret and implacable labor of a small ant she predisposed the women of the village against the flightiness of their husbands, who were already preparing for the move.

她像一只小蚂蚁一样进行着隐秘而毫不留情的努力，煽动村里的妇女们反对丈夫们的轻率举动，那些男人已经准备好要搬家了。

José Arcadio Buendía did not know at what moment or because of what adverse forces his plan had become enveloped in a web of pretexts, disappointments, and evasions until it turned into nothing but an illusion.

何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚不知道是在哪个时刻，或是因为何种阻力，他的计划陷入了一张充满借口、失望和逃避的网中，直到最终沦为一场泡影。

Úrsula watched him with innocent attention and even felt some pity for him on the morning when she found him in the back room muttering about his plans for moving as he placed his laboratory pieces in their original boxes.

一天早上，乌尔苏拉发现他在后面的房间里，一边把实验室的器具放回原装的盒子里，一边嘀咕着搬家的计划；她以无辜的神情注视着他，甚至对他产生了一丝怜悯。

She let him finish.

她由着他收拾。

She let him nail up the boxes and put his initials on them with an inked brush, without reproaching him, but knowing now that he knew (because she had heard him say so in his soft monologues) that the men of the village would not back him up in his undertaking.

她让他把箱子钉好，用蘸墨水的刷子写上名字的缩写；她没有责备他，因为她此刻明白他已经知道（她听到他在轻声自语中说过）村里的男人不会支持他的行动。

Only when he began to take down the door of the room did Úrsula dare ask him what he was doing, and he answered with a certain bitterness.

直到他开始拆房间的门时，乌尔苏拉才敢问他在干什么，他带着些许苦涩回答。

“Since no one wants to leave, we'll leave all by ourselves.” Úrsula did not become upset.

“既然没人想走，那我们自己走。”乌尔苏拉并没有生气。

“We will not leave,” she said.

“我们不走，”她说。

“We will stay here, because we have had a son here.”

“我们就留在这里，因为我们在这里生了一个儿子。”

“We have still not had a death,” he said.

“我们这里还没死过人，”他说。

“A person does not belong to a place until there is someone dead under the ground.”

“只要没有死人埋在地下，你就不属于这个地方。”

Úrsula replied with a soft firmness:

乌尔苏拉温柔而坚定地回答：

“If I have to die for the rest of you to stay here, I will die.”

“如果必须我死才能让你们留下来，那我就去死。”

José Arcadio Buendía had not thought that his wife's will was so firm.

何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚没想到妻子的意志如此坚定。

He tried to seduce her with the charm of his fantasy, with the promise of a prodigious world where all one had to do was sprinkle some magic liquid on the ground and the plants would bear fruit whenever a man wished, and where all manner of instruments against pain were sold at bargain prices.

他试图用他幻想的魅力来诱惑她，向她许诺一个奇妙的世界：只要在地上洒点神奇的液体，植物就会随心所欲地结果实；各种止痛的仪器都能以低廉的价格买到。

But Úrsula was insensible to his clairvoyance.

但乌尔苏拉对他的远见毫不动心。

“Instead of going around thinking about your crazy inventions, you should be worrying about your sons,” she replied.

“你与其成天琢磨那些疯疯癫癫的发明，还不如多操心操心你的儿子们，”她回答说，

“Look at the state they're in, running wild just like donkeys.”

“看看他们那副样子，简直就像脱缰的野驴。”

José Arcadio Buendía took his wife's words literally.

何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚从字面上理解了妻子的话。

He looked out the window and saw the barefoot children in the sunny garden and he had the impression that only at that instant had they begun to exist, conceived by Úrsula's spell, Something occurred inside of him then, something mysterious and definitive that uprooted him from his own time and carried him adrift through an unexplored region of his memory.

他望向窗外，看到光着脚的孩子们在阳光明媚的花园里玩耍，他恍惚觉得仿佛直到这一刻，他们才在乌尔苏拉的咒语下开始存在；他内心涌起一种神秘而决定性的感受，将他从自身的时间中连根拔起，载着他在记忆中未被探索的区域漂流。

While Úrsula continued sweeping the house, which was safe now from being abandoned for the rest of her life, he stood there with an absorbed look, contemplating the children until his eyes became moist and he dried them with the back of his hand, exhaling a deep sigh of resignation.

乌尔苏拉继续打扫着屋子，这屋子现在安全了，她余生都不会再被抛弃；而他神情专注地站在那儿，久久凝视着孩子们，直到眼眶湿润，他用手背擦去眼泪，发出一声深深的无可奈何的叹息。

“All right,” he said.

“好吧，”他说。

“Tell them to come help me take the things out of the boxes.”

“叫他们来帮我把东西从箱子里拿出来。”

José Arcadio, the older of the children, was fourteen.

大儿子何塞·阿卡迪奥十四岁了。

He had a square head, thick hair, and his father' s character.

他长着方头，浓密的头发，性格像他父亲。

Although he had the same impulse for growth and physical strength, it was early evident that he lacked imagination.

虽然他有着同样的成长冲动和强壮体魄，但很早就显露出缺乏想象力。

He had been conceived and born during the difficult crossing of the mountains, before the founding of Macondo, and his parents gave thanks to heaven when they saw he had no animal features.

他是在建村前那次艰难的翻山越岭中孕育并出生的；当父母看到他身上没有动物的特征时，不由得感谢上苍。

Aureliano, the first human being to be born in Macondo, would be six years old in March.

奥雷里亚诺，马孔多诞生的第一个人类，到三月份就满六岁了。

He was silent and withdrawn.

他沉默寡言，性格孤僻。

He had wept in his mother's womb and had been born with his eyes open.

他在娘胎里就曾哭泣过，出生时睁着双眼。

As they were cutting the umbilical cord, he moved his head from side to side, taking in the things in the room and examining the faces of the people with a fearless curiosity.

当他们剪脐带时，他左右转动着脑袋，打量着房间里的陈设，带着毫无畏惧的好奇心端详着人们的脸庞。

Then, indifferent to those who came close to look at him, he kept his attention concentrated on the palm roof, which looked as if it were about to collapse under the tremendous pressure of the rain.

然后，他对靠近来看他的人毫不理会，将注意力集中在棕榈叶搭成的屋顶上，那屋顶在滂沱大雨的重压下似乎随时都会坍塌。

Úrsula did not remember the intensity of that look again until one day when little Aureliano, at the age of three, went into the kitchen at the moment she was taking a pot of boiling soup from the stove and putting it on the table.

乌尔苏拉直到小奥雷里亚诺三岁时，才再次回想起那目光中的专注；那天，她刚把一锅滚烫的汤从炉子上端下来放到桌上，孩子走进了厨房。

The child, Perplexed, said from the doorway, “It's going to spill.” The pot was firmly placed in the center of the table, but just as soon as the child made his announcement, it began an unmistakable movement toward the edge, as if impelled by some inner dynamism, and it fell and broke on the floor.

孩子站在门口，困惑地说：“要洒了。”锅明明稳稳地放在桌子正中，但就在孩子说完这番话时，它竟然不可思议地向边缘移动，仿佛被某种内在动力驱使着，随后掉在地上摔得粉碎。

Úrsula, alarmed, told her husband about the episode, but he interpreted it as a natural phenomenon.

乌尔苏拉惊恐万分，把这事告诉了丈夫，但他却将其解释为自然现象。

That was the way he always was alien to the existence of his sons, partly because he considered childhood as a period of mental insufficiency, and partly because he was always too absorbed in his fantastic speculations.

他总是这样对儿子们的存在漠不关心，部分原因是觉得童年时代智力尚未健全，部分原因是他总是太过沉迷于自己那些奇思妙想。

But since the afternoon when he called the children in to help him unpack the things in the laboratory, he gave them his best hours.

但是，自从那天下午他把孩子们叫来帮忙拆箱起，他便把最好的时光都给了他们。

In the small separate room, where the walls were gradually being covered by strange maps and fabulous drawings, he taught them to read and write and do sums, and he spoke to them about the wonders of the world, not only where his learning had extended, but forcing the limits of his imagination to extremes.

在那个独立的小房间里，墙上渐渐贴满了奇怪的地图和奇妙的图纸；他教他们读书写字和算术，给他们讲世界的奇迹，不仅局限于他已知的学识，还将想象力的极限发挥到了极致。

It was in that way that the boys ended up learning that in the southern extremes of Africa there were men so intelligent and peaceful that their only pastime was to sit and think, and that it was possible to cross the Aegean Sea on foot by jumping from island to island all the way to the port of Salonika.

男孩们正是以这种方式得知，在非洲大陆的最南端，生活着一群既聪明又平和的人，他们唯一的消遣就是静坐思考；而只要从一个岛跳到另一个岛，就能步行穿越爱琴海，一直走到萨洛尼卡港。

Those hallucinating sessions remained printed on the memories of the boys in such a way that many years later, a second before the regular army officer gave the firing squad the command to fire, Colonel Aureliano Buendía saw once more that warm March afternoon on which his father had interrupted the lesson in physics and stood fascinated, with his hand in the air and his eyes motionless, listening to the distant pipes, drums, and jingles of the gypsies, who were coming to the village once more, announcing the latest and most startling discovery of the sages of Memphis.

这些充满幻觉的课程深深印在男孩们的脑海中；许多年后，在正规军军官向行刑队下达开火命令的前一秒，奥雷里亚诺·布恩迪亚上校再次浮现出那个温暖的三月下午：父亲中断了物理课，如痴如醉地站在那里，一只手悬在半空，目光呆滞，倾听着远处吉卜赛人传来的笛声、鼓声和铃鼓声；他们又一次来到村里，宣告孟菲斯智者最新、最惊人的发现。

They were new gypsies, young men and women who knew only their own language, handsome specimens with oily skins and intelligent hands, whose dances and music sowed a panic of uproarious joy through the streets, with parrots painted all colors reciting Italian arias, and a hen who laid a hundred golden eggs to the sound of a tambourine, and a trained monkey who read minds, and the multi-use machine that could be used at the same time to sew on buttons and reduce fevers, and the apparatus to make a person forget his bad memories, and a poultice to lose time, and a thousand more inventions so ingenious and unusual that José Arcadio Buendía must have wanted to invent a memory machine so that he could remember them all.

这批是新来的吉卜赛人，是只懂自己语言的年轻男女；他们容貌俊美，皮肤油亮，双手灵巧，他们的舞蹈和音乐在街头播撒下一种狂乱欢乐的恐慌：有五颜六色的鹦鹉背诵意大利咏叹调，有伴随铃鼓声下出一百个金蛋的母鸡，有能看透人心的受训猴子，有同时能缝扣子和退烧的多功能机器，有让人忘记烦恼的仪器，有能打发时间的药膏，还有成千上万种极其巧妙和新奇的发明，简直让何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚恨不得发明一台记忆机，好把它们全都记住。

In an instant they transformed the village.

转眼间，他们就让村子变了样。

The inhabitants of Macondo found themselves lost is their own streets, confused by the crowded fair.

马孔多的居民发现自己在熟悉的街道上迷失了方向，被拥挤喧闹的集市搞得晕头转向。

Holding a child by each hand so as not to lose them in the tumult, bumping into acrobats with gold-capped teeth and jugglers with six arms, suffocated by the mingled breath of manure and sandals that the crowd exhaled, José Arcadio Buendía went about everywhere like a madman, looking for Melquíades so that he could reveal to him the infinite secrets of that fabulous nightmare.

何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚一手牵着一个孩子，生怕在人群中走散；他撞上镶金牙的杂技演员和长着六只手的杂耍艺人，在人群呼出的粪便和凉鞋的混合气味中几乎窒息；他像疯子一样到处寻找梅尔基亚德斯，想让他向自己揭开这场奇妙梦魇的无尽奥秘。

He asked several gypsies, who did not understand his language.

他问了几个吉卜赛人，他们听不懂他的话。

Finally he reached the place where Melquíades used to set up his tent and he found a taciturn Armenian who in Spanish was hawking a syrup to make oneself invisible.

最后，他找到了梅尔基亚德斯过去搭帐篷的地方，发现一个沉默寡言的亚美尼亚人正用西班牙语兜售一种隐身糖浆。

He had drunk down a glass of the amber substance in one gulp as José Arcadio Buendía elbowed his way through the absorbed group that was witnessing the spectacle, and was able to ask his question.

当何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚从全神贯注看热闹的人群中挤过去时，那人刚把一杯琥珀色液体一饮而尽，何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚趁机问出了问题。

The gypsy wrapped him in the frightful climate of his look before he turned into a puddle of pestilential and smoking pitch over which the echo of his reply still floated: “Melquíades is dead.”

吉卜赛人用那令人胆寒的目光打量了他一番，随后化作一摊恶臭冒烟的沥青，上面依然回荡着他的答复：“梅尔基亚德斯死了。”

Upset by the news, José Arcadio Buendía stood motionless, trying to rise above his affliction, until the group dis -persed, called away by other artifices, and the puddle of the taciturn Armenian evaporated completely.

何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚被这个消息惊得呆立当场，努力平复心中的悲痛；直到人群被其他戏法吸引走，那个沉默的亚美尼亚人化作的沥青潭也彻底蒸发。

Other gypsies confirmed later on that Melquíades had in fact succumbed to the fever on the beach at Singapore and that his body had been thrown into the deepest part of the Java Sea.

后来其他吉卜赛人证实，梅尔基亚德斯确实在新加坡的海滩上死于热病，遗体被抛入了爪哇海最深处。

The children had no interest in the news.

孩子们对这个消息毫无兴趣。

They insisted that their father take them to see the overwhelming novelty of the sages of Memphis that was being advertised at the entrance of a tent that, according to what was said, had belonged to King Solomon.

他们执意要父亲带他们去看孟菲斯智者那令人惊叹的新奇玩意儿，那玩意儿正在一顶据说是所罗门王遗留的帐篷入口处做广告。

They insisted so much that José Arcadio Buendía paid the thirty reales and led them into the center of the tent, where there was a giant with a hairy torso and a shaved head, with a copper ring in his nose and a heavy iron chain on his ankle, watching over a pirate chest.

他们纠缠不休，何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚只得掏出三十个里亚尔，领着他们走到帐篷中央；那里有一个剃着光头、毛茸茸半裸着身子的巨人，鼻子上穿个铜环，脚踝上拴着沉重的铁链，正看守着一个海盗式的箱子。

When it was opened by the giant, the chest gave off a glacial exhalation.

巨人一打开箱子，便冒出一股寒气。

Inside there was only an enor-mous, transparent block with infinite internal needles in which the light of the sunset was broken up into colored stars.

里面只有一个巨大的透明冰块，内部布满了无数针状气泡，落日的余晖在其中折射出五颜六色的星光。

Disconcerted, knowing that the children were wait-ing for an immediate explanation, José Arcadio Buendía ventured a murmur:

何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚感到有些不知所措，知道孩子们在等着他马上给出解释，便试探性地嘀咕道：

“It's the largest diamond in the world.”

“这是世上最大的钻石。”

“No,” the gypsy countered.

“不，”吉卜赛人纠正道，

“It's ice.”

“是冰块。”

José Arcadio Buendía, without understanding, stretched out his hand toward the cake, but the giant moved it away.

何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚懵懂地向那块东西伸出手，但巨人一把将其挪开。

“Five reales more to touch it,” he said.

“再加五个里亚尔才能摸。”他说。

José Arcadio Buendía paid them and put his hand on the ice and held it there for several minutes as his heart filled with fear and jubilation at the contact with mys-tery.

何塞·阿卡迪奥·布恩迪亚付了钱，将手放在冰块上，停放了足有几分钟，心中充满了接触到神秘事物时的恐惧和喜悦。

Without knowing what to say, he paid ten reales more so that his sons could have that prodigious experience.

他不知道该说什么，又付了十个里亚尔，好让两个儿子也体验一把这奇妙的感觉。

Little José Arcadio refused to touch it.

小何塞·阿卡迪奥拒绝去摸。

Aureliano, on the other hand, took a step forward and put his hand on it, withdrawing it immediately.

奥雷里亚诺倒是往前迈了一步，把手放在上面，立刻又缩了回来。

“It's boiling,” he exclaimed, startled.

“它在烧！”他吃惊地喊道。

But his father paid no attention to him.

但他父亲并没有理会他。

Intoxicated by the evidence of the miracle, he forgot at that moment about the frustration of his delirious undertakings and Melquíades' body, abandoned to the appetite of the squids.

他陶醉在奇迹的明证中，那一刻，他忘了自己狂热事业的挫败，也忘了被抛弃给乌贼当点心的梅尔基亚德斯的遗体。

He paid another five reales and with his hand on the cake, as if giving testimony on the holy scriptures, he exclaimed:

他又付了五个里亚尔，手抚在冰块上，仿佛按着《圣经》宣誓般高声呼喊：

“This is the great invention of our time.”

“这是我们这个时代最伟大的发明。”
