Chapter 3

第三章

Pilar Ternera's son was brought to his grandparents' house two weeks after he was born.

皮拉尔·特尔内拉的儿子出生两个星期后，被送到祖父母家里。

Úrsula admitted him grudgingly, conquered once more by the obstinacy of her husband, who could not tolerate the idea that an offshoot of his blood should be adrift, but he imposed the condition that the child should never know his true identity.

乌苏拉勉强同意了，她再次被丈夫的固执所征服；他无法忍受自己血脉的枝条流落在外，但他提出了一个条件：孩子永远不能知道自己的真实身份。

Although he was given the name José Arcadio, they ended up calling him simply Arcadio so as to avoid confusion.

虽然给他取名叫何塞·阿尔卡迪奥，但为了避免混淆，大家最终只叫他阿尔卡迪奥。

At that time there was so much activity in the town and so much bustle in the house that the care of the children was relegated to a secondary level.

那时镇上活动频繁，家里也忙乱不堪，照看孩子的事就降到了次要地位。

They were put in the care of Visitación, a Guajiro Indian woman who had arrived in town with a brother in flight from a plague of insomnia that had been scourging their tribe for several years.

他们被交给比西塔西翁照看，这是一位瓜希拉印第安女人，她和弟弟一起逃到镇上，因为他们部落几年来一直遭受失眠症的折磨。

They were both so docile and willing to help that Úrsula took them on to help her with her household chores.

他们俩都很温顺，乐于助人，于是乌苏拉收留了他们，帮助她做家务。

That was how Arcadio and Amaranta came to speak the Guajiro language before Spanish, and they learned to drink lizard broth and eat spider eggs without Úrsula's knowing it, for she was too busy with a promising business in candy animals.

就这样，阿尔卡迪奥和阿玛兰塔先学会说瓜希拉语，然后才学会西班牙语，他们还学会喝蜥蜴汤、吃蜘蛛蛋，而乌苏拉对此一无所知，因为她正忙于一项前景看好的糖果动物生意。

Macondo had changed.

马孔多变了样。

The people who had come with Úrsula spread the news of the good quality of its soil and its privileged position with respect to the swamp, so that from the narrow village of past times it changed into an active town with stores and workshops and a permanent commercial route over which the first Arabs arrived with their baggy pants and rings in their ears, swapping glass beads for macaws.

随乌苏拉来的人传播了这里土壤肥沃、位置优越的消息，于是从过去狭小的村落，变成了一个活跃的镇子，有商店和作坊，还有一条固定的商路，第一批阿拉伯人穿着宽大的裤子、戴着耳环，沿着这条路到来，用玻璃珠换取金刚鹦鹉。

José Arcadio Buendía did not have a moment's rest.

何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚一刻也不得休息。

Fascinated by an immediate reality that came to be more fantastic than the vast universe of his imagination, he lost all interest in the alchemist's laboratory, put to rest the material that had become attenuated with months of manipulation, and went back to being the enterprising man of earlier days when he had decided upon the layout of the streets and the location of the new houses so that no one would enjoy privileges that everyone did not have.

他被眼前的现实迷住了，这种现实比他想象中的广阔宇宙还要奇妙，于是他对炼金实验室失去了兴趣，把那些经月累月摆弄而变得稀薄的材料搁置一旁，重新成为早年的创业者，决定街道的布局和新房的位置，让谁也不能享有别人没有的特权。

He acquired such authority among the new arrivals that foundations were not laid or walls built without his being consulted, and it was decided that he should be the one in charge of the distribution of the land.

他在新来的人中获得了如此权威，以至于不经他咨询，地基就不会打，墙就不会砌，大家还决定由他负责分配土地。

When the acrobat gypsies returned, with their vagabond carnival transformed now into a gigantic organization of games of luck and chance, they were received with great joy, for it was thought that José Arcadio would be coming back with them.

当那些杂技吉卜赛人回来时，他们那流浪的狂欢节已变成一个庞大的赌博和碰运气组织，大家兴高采烈地迎接他们，因为人们以为何塞·阿尔卡迪奥会随他们归来。

But José Arcadio did not return, nor did they come with the snake-man, who, according to what Úrsula thought, was the only one who could tell them about their son, so the gypsies were not allowed to camp in town or set foot in it in the future, for they were considered the bearers of concupiscence and perversion.

但何塞·阿尔卡迪奥没有回来，他们也没有带来蛇人——据乌苏拉认为，只有蛇人能告诉他们儿子的消息——因此吉卜赛人以后不准在镇上扎营或踏足，因为他们被视为淫欲和堕落的传播者。

José Arcadio Buendía, however, was explicit in maintaining that the old tribe of Melquíades, who had contributed so much to the growth of the village with his age-old wisdom and his fabulous inventions, would always find the gates open.

然而，何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚明确表示，梅尔基亚德斯那个古老的部落——他们以古老的智慧和奇妙的发明为村子的成长做出了巨大贡献——将永远受到欢迎。

But Melquíades' tribe, according to what the wanderers said, had been wiped off the face of the earth because they had gone beyond the limits of human knowledge.

但据流浪者说，梅尔基亚德斯的部落已从地球上消失，因为他们超越了人类知识的界限。

Emancipated for the moment at least from the torment of fantasy, José Arcadio Buendía in a short time set up a system of order and work which allowed for only one bit of license: the freeing of the birds, which, since the time of the founding, had made time merry with their flutes, and installing in their place musical clocks in every house.

至少暂时摆脱了幻想的折磨，何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚很快建立了一套秩序与工作的制度，只允许一项自由：放飞鸟儿——自建村以来，这些鸟儿就用笛声让时光欢快——并在每家每户安装音乐钟来取代它们。

They were wondrous clocks made of carved wood, which the Arabs had traded for macaws and which José Arcadio Buendía had synchronized with such precision that every half hour the town grew merry with the progressive chords of the same song until it reached the climax of a noontime that was as exact and unanimous as a complete waltz.

这些是用雕刻木头制成的奇妙时钟，是阿拉伯人用金刚鹦鹉换来的，何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚把它们校准得非常精确，每隔半小时，全镇就随着同一首歌逐渐升高的和弦而欢腾，直到正午达到高潮，那一刻精确而一致，就像一曲完整的华尔兹。

It was also José Arcadio Buendía who decided during those years that they should plant almond trees instead of acacias on the streets, and who discovered, without ever revealing it, a way to make them live forever.

也是何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚在那几年决定在街道上种植杏树而不是金合欢树，并且发现了一种让杏树永生的方法，但他从未透露。

Many years later, when Macondo was a field of wooden houses with zinc roofs, the broken and dusty almond trees still stood on the oldest streets, although no one knew who had planted them.

许多年后，当马孔多变成一片锌皮屋顶的木屋时，那些残破而布满灰尘的杏树依然屹立在最古老的街道上，尽管没有人知道是谁种下的。

While his father was putting the town in order and his mother was increasing their wealth with her marvelous business of candied little roosters and fish, which left the house twice a day strung along sticks of balsa wood, Aureliano spent interminable hours in the abandoned laboratory, learning the art of silverwork by his own experimentation.

父亲在整顿镇子，母亲用她那美妙的糖果小公鸡和小鱼生意增加财富——这些糖果每天两次用轻木棍串着离开家——而奥雷里亚诺则在废弃的实验室里度过漫长的时光，通过自己的实验学习银匠手艺。

He had shot up so fast that in a short time the clothing left behind by his brother no longer fit him and he began to wear his father's, but Visitación had to sew pleats in the shirt and darts in the pants, because Aureliano had not sequined the corpulence of the others.

他长得飞快，很快哥哥留下的衣服就不合身了，他开始穿父亲的衣服，但比西塔西翁不得不在衬衫上缝褶、在裤子上打褶，因为奥雷里亚诺没有继承其他人的魁梧身材。

Adolescence had taken away the softness of his voice and had made him silent and definitely solitary, but, on the other hand, it had restored the intense expression that he had had in his eyes when he was born.

青春期夺走了他声音的柔和，让他变得沉默而彻底孤独，但另一方面，也恢复了他出生时眼睛里那种强烈的表情。

He concentrated so much on his experiments in silverwork that he scarcely left the laboratory to eat.

他全神贯注于银匠实验，几乎不离开实验室去吃饭。

Worried ever his inner withdrawal, José Arcadio Buendía gave him the keys to the house and a little money, thinking that perhaps he needed a woman.

何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚担心他内心的退缩，给了他房子的钥匙和一点钱，心想他也许需要一个女人。

But Aureliano spent the money on muriatic acid to prepare some aqua regia and he beautified the keys by plating them with gold.

但奥雷里亚诺把钱花在盐酸上配制王水，还给钥匙镀金使它们变得漂亮。

His excesses were hardly comparable to those of Arcadio and Amaranta, who had already begun to get their second teeth and still went about all day clutching at the Indians' cloaks, stubborn in their decision not to speak Spanish but the Guajiro language.

他的过分行为几乎无法与阿尔卡迪奥和阿玛兰塔相比，他们已经开始长第二颗牙，却整天抓着印第安人的斗篷，固执地决定不说西班牙语而说瓜希拉语。

“You shouldn't complain.”

“你不该抱怨。”

Úrsula told her husband.

乌苏拉对丈夫说。

“Children inherit their parents' madness.” And as she was lamenting her misfortune, convinced that the wild behavior of her children was something as fearful as a pig's tail, Aureliano gave her a look that wrapped her in an atmosphere of uncertainty.

“孩子们继承了父母的疯狂。”她一边哀叹自己的不幸，一边确信孩子们的狂野行为像猪尾巴一样可怕，这时奥雷里亚诺看了她一眼，让她陷入一种不确定的氛围。

“Somebody is coming,” he told her.

“有人要来了，”他对她说。

Úrsula, as she did whenever he made a prediction, tried to break it down with her housewifely logic.

乌苏拉每次听到他的预言，都会用主妇的逻辑去拆解。

It was normal for someone to be coming.

有人要来是很正常的。

Dozens of strangers came through Macondo every day without arousing suspicion or secret ideas.

每天都有几十个陌生人经过马孔多，并没有引起怀疑或秘密的想法。

Nevertheless, beyond all logic, Aureliano was sure of his prediction.

然而，超越一切逻辑，奥雷里亚诺对自己的预言确信无疑。

“I don't know who it will be,” he insisted, “but whoever it is is already on the way.”

“我不知道是谁，”他坚持说，“但不管是谁，已经在路上了。”

That Sunday, in fact, Rebeca arrived.

那个星期天，蕾贝卡果然来了。

She was only eleven years old.

她只有十一岁。

She had made the difficult trip from Manaure with some hide dealers who had taken on the task of delivering her along with a letter to José Arcadio Buendía, but they could not explain precisely who the person was who had asked the favor.

她随一些皮革商从马瑙雷艰难地来到这里，他们承担了把她连同一封信交给何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚的任务，但他们无法准确说明委托此事的人是谁。

Her entire baggage consisted of a small trunk, a little rocking chair with small hand-painted flowers, and a canvas sack which kept making a cloc-cloc-cloc sound, where she carried her parents' bones.

她的全部行李是一个小箱子、一把绘有小花的手绘摇椅，以及一个不断发出咯咯咯声音的帆布袋，里面装着她父母的骨头。

The letter addressed to José Arcadio Buendía was written in very warm terms by someone who still loved him very much in spite of time and distance, and who felt obliged by a basic humanitarian feeling to do the charitable thing and send him that poor unsheltered orphan, who was a second cousin of Úrsula's and consequently also a relative of José Arcadio Buendía, although farther removed, because she was the daughter of that unforgettable friend Nicanor Ulloa and his very worthy wife Rebeca Montiel, may God keep them in His holy kingdom, whose remains the girl was carrying so that they might be given Christian burial.

写给何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚的信语气非常热情，写信人尽管时过境迁仍深爱着他，并且出于基本的人道主义情感，觉得有义务做这件善事，把那个可怜的无依孤儿送给他。她是乌苏拉的远房表妹，因此也是何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚的远亲，因为她是那位难忘的朋友尼卡诺尔·乌略亚和他值得尊敬的妻子蕾贝卡·蒙铁尔的女儿，愿上帝保佑他们在圣洁的天国，那女孩带着他们的遗骨，以便让他们得到基督徒的安葬。

The names mentioned, as well as the signature on the letter, were perfectly legible, but neither José Arcadio Buendía nor Úrsula remembered having any relatives with those names, nor did they know anyone by the name of the sender of the letter, much less the remote village of Manaure.

信中提到的名字以及签名都清晰可辨，但何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚和乌苏拉都不记得有任何叫这些名字的亲戚，也不知道写信人的名字，更不用说那个遥远的马瑙雷村了。

It was impossible to obtain any further information from the girl.

从女孩那里无法获得更多信息。

From the moment she arrived she had been sitting in the rocker, sucking her finger and observing everyone with her large, startled eyes without giving any sign of understanding what they were asking her.

从到达的那一刻起，她就坐在摇椅上，吮着手指，用惊恐的大眼睛观察着每个人，没有任何迹象表明她理解他们在问什么。

She wore a diagonally striped dress that had been dyed black, worn by use, and a pair of scaly patent leather boots.

她穿着一件斜条纹的黑色连衣裙，因长期穿着而破旧，还有一双鳞状漆皮靴。

Her hair was held behind her ears with bows of black ribbon.

她的头发用黑色丝带在耳后扎成蝴蝶结。

She wore a scapular with the images worn away by sweat, and on her right wrist the fang of a carnivorous animal mounted on a backing of copper as an amulet against the evil eye.

她戴着一个因汗水而磨损的圣牌，右手腕上戴着一枚食肉动物的牙齿，镶在铜底座上，作为抵御邪眼的护身符。

Her greenish skin, her stomach, round and tense as a drum, revealed poor health and hunger that were older than she was, but when they gave her something to eat she kept the plate on her knees without tasting anything.

她发绿的皮肤、像鼓一样圆而紧绷的肚子，显露出比她年龄更大的病弱和饥饿，但当他们给她食物时，她把盘子放在膝盖上，一口也不尝。

They even began to think that she was a deaf-mute until the Indians asked her in their language if she wanted some water and she moved her eyes as if she recognized them and said yes with her head.

他们甚至开始以为她是聋哑人，直到印第安人用他们的语言问她是否要水，她才动了动眼睛，好像认出了他们，并点头说是。

They kept her, because there was nothing else they could do.

他们留下了她，因为别无选择。

They decided to call her Rebeca, which according to the letter was her mother's name, because Aureliano had the patience to read to her the names of all the saints and he did not get a reaction from any one of them.

他们决定叫她蕾贝卡，因为信中说那是她母亲的名字——奥雷里亚诺耐心地给她读了所有圣徒的名字，却没有一个引起她的反应。

Since there was no cemetery in Macondo at that time, for no one had died up till then, they kept the bag of bones to wait for a worthy place of burial, and for a long time it got in the way everywhere and would be found where least expected, always with its clucking of a broody hen.

那时马孔多还没有墓地，因为至今还没有人死去，他们就把那袋骨头留着，等待一个合适的安葬之地，很长一段时间里它到处碍事，总在最意想不到的地方被发现，总是发出孵蛋母鸡般的咯咯声。

A long time passed before Rebeca became incorporated into the life of the family.

过了很长时间，蕾贝卡才融入家庭生活。

She would sit in her small rocker sucking her finger in the most remote corner of the house.

她会坐在小摇椅上吮手指，待在房子最偏远的角落。

Nothing attracted her attention except the music of the clocks, which she would look for every half hour with her frightened eyes as if she hoped to find it someplace in the air.

除了时钟的音乐，什么都吸引不了她的注意，她每半小时就用惊恐的眼睛寻找，仿佛希望在空气中的某个地方找到它。

They could not get her to eat for several days.

他们好几天都无法让她吃东西。

No one understood why she had not died of hunger until the Indians, who were aware of everything, for they went ceaselessly about the house on their stealthy feet, discovered that Rebeca only liked to eat the damp earth of the courtyard and the cake of whitewash that she picked off the walls with her nails.

没人明白她为什么没有饿死，直到那些印第安人——他们悄无声息地在房子里走来走去，什么都知道——发现蕾贝卡只喜欢吃院子里潮湿的泥土和她用指甲从墙上抠下来的白灰块。

It was obvious that her parents, or whoever had raised her, had scolded her for that habit because she did it secretively and with a feeling of guilt, trying to put away supplies so that she could eat when no one was looking.

显然，她的父母或抚养她的人曾为这个习惯责骂过她，因为她偷偷地做，带着愧疚感，试图存些存货，以便在没人看见时吃。

From then on they put her under an implacable watch.

从那时起，他们对她进行了无情的监视。

They threw cow gall onto the courtyard and rubbed hot chili on the walls, thinking they could defeat her pernicious vice with those methods, but she showed such signs of astuteness and ingenuity to find some earth that Úrsula found herself forced to use more drastic methods.

他们在院子里泼牛胆汁，在墙上抹辣椒，以为这样就能战胜她那有害的恶习，但她表现出如此机敏和 ingenuity 来找泥土，以至于乌苏拉被迫采用更激烈的办法。

She put some orange juice and rhubarb into a pan that she left in the dew all night and she gave her the dose the following day on an empty stomach.

她把橙汁和大黄放进锅里，在露水中放了一夜，第二天空腹给她服下。

Although no one had told her that it was the specific remedy for the vice of eating earth, she thought that any bitter substance in an empty stomach would have to make the liver react.

虽然没人告诉她这是吃土恶习的特效药，但她认为空腹服用任何苦的东西都会让肝脏产生反应。

Rebeca was so rebellious and strong in spite of her frailness that they had to tie her up like a calf to make her swallow the medicine, and they could barely keep back her kicks or bear up under the strange hieroglyphics that she alternated with her bites and spitting, and that, according to what the scandalized Indians said, were the vilest obscenities that one could ever imagine in their language.

尽管身体虚弱，蕾贝卡却非常叛逆和强壮，他们不得不像绑小牛一样把她绑起来让她吞药，他们几乎挡不住她的踢腿，也忍受不了她交替着咬人和吐口水时发出的奇怪象形文字，据惊骇的印第安人说，那是他们语言中能想象到的最下流的脏话。

When Úrsula discovered that, she added whipping to the treatment.

当乌苏拉发现这一点时，她在治疗中增加了鞭打。

It was never established whether it was the rhubarb or the beatings that had effect, or both of them together, but the truth was that in a few weeks Rebeca began to show signs of recovery.

从未确定是大黄还是鞭打起了作用，还是两者共同作用，但事实是，几周后蕾贝卡开始显示出康复的迹象。

She took part in the games of Arcadio and Amaranta, who treated her like an older sister, and she ate heartily, using the utensils properly.

她参加了阿尔卡迪奥和阿玛兰塔的游戏，他们把她当作姐姐对待，她吃得津津有味，使用餐具也很得体。

It was soon revealed that she spoke Spanish with as much fluency as the Indian language, that she had a remarkable ability for manual work, and that she could sing the waltz of the clocks with some very funny words that she herself had invented.

很快发现，她说西班牙语和印第安语一样流利，她有出色的手工能力，还能用自己编的非常有趣的歌词唱时钟的华尔兹。

It did not take long for them to consider her another member of the family.

没过多久，他们就把她视为家庭的另一员。

She was more affectionate to Úrsula than any of her own children had been, and she called Arcadio and Amaranta brother and sister, Aureliano uncle, and José Arcadio Buendía grandpa.

她对乌苏拉比自己的任何孩子都更亲热，她叫阿尔卡迪奥和阿玛兰塔哥哥姐姐，叫奥雷里亚诺叔叔，叫何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚爷爷。

So that she finally deserved, as much as the others, the name of Rebeca Buendía, the only one that she ever had and that she bore with dignity until her death.

因此她终于和其他人一样，配得上蕾贝卡·布恩迪亚这个名字，这是她唯一的名字，她带着尊严一直用到去世。

One night about the time that Rebeca was cured of the vice of eating earth and was brought to sleep in the other children's room, the Indian woman, who slept with them awoke by chance and heard a strange, intermittent sound in the corner.

大约在蕾贝卡治好吃土恶习、被安排和其他孩子睡一个房间的时候，一天晚上，和他们睡在一起的印第安女人偶然醒来，听到角落里传来一种奇怪的间歇性声音。

She got up in alarm, thinking that an animal had come into the room, and then she saw Rebeca in the rocker, sucking her finger and with her eyes lighted up in the darkness like those of a cat.

她惊慌地起身，以为有动物进了房间，然后看到蕾贝卡坐在摇椅上，吮着手指，眼睛在黑暗中像猫眼一样发光。

Terrified, exhausted by her fate, Visitación recognized in those eyes the symptoms of the sickness whose threat had obliged her and her brother to exile themselves forever from an age-old kingdom where they had been prince and princess.

比西塔西翁吓坏了，对自己的命运感到疲惫，她在那双眼睛里认出了那种疾病的症状，正是这种疾病的威胁迫使她和弟弟永远流亡，离开了他们曾是王子和公主的古老王国。

It was the insomnia plague.

那是失眠症瘟疫。

Cataure, the Indian, was gone from the house by morning.

早上，印第安人卡塔乌雷已经离开了房子。

His sister stayed because her fatalistic heart told her that the lethal sickness would follow her, no matter what, to the farthest corner of the earth.

他的姐姐留了下来，因为她宿命的心告诉她，无论如何，这种致命的疾病都会跟着她到地球最遥远的角落。

No one understood Visitación's alarm.

没人理解比西塔西翁的惊恐。

“If we don't ever sleep again, so much the better,” José Arcadio Buendía said in good humor.

“如果我们再也不睡觉，那更好，”何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚幽默地说。

“That way we can get more out of life.” But the Indian woman explained that the most fearsome part of the sickness of insomnia was not the impossibility of sleeping, for the body did not feel any fatigue at all, but its inexorable evolution toward a more critical manifestation: a loss of memory.

“这样我们就能从生活中得到更多。”但印第安女人解释说，失眠症最可怕的部分不是无法睡觉，因为身体根本不感到疲劳，而是它不可阻挡地演变为更严重的表现：记忆丧失。

She meant that when the sick person became used to his state of vigil, the recollection of his childhood began to be erased from his memory, then the name and notion of things, and finally the identity of people and even the awareness of his own being, until he sank into a kind of idiocy that had no past.

她的意思是，当病人习惯了清醒状态后，童年的回忆就开始从记忆中抹去，然后是事物的名称和概念，最后是人们的身份甚至对自己存在的意识，直到陷入一种没有过去的痴呆状态。

José Arcadio Buendía, dying with laughter, thought that it was just a question of one of the many illnesses invented by the Indians' superstitions.

何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚笑得要死，认为这只是印第安人迷信发明的众多疾病之一。

But Úrsula, just to be safe, took the precaution of isolating Rebeca from the other children.

但乌苏拉为了保险起见，采取预防措施，把蕾贝卡和其他孩子隔离开来。

After several weeks, when Visitación's terror seemed to have died down, José Arcadio Buendía found himself rolling over in bed, unable to fall asleep.

几周后，当比西塔西翁的恐惧似乎消退时，何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚发现自己在床上翻来覆去，无法入睡。

Úrsula, who had also awakened, asked him what was wrong, and he answered: “I'm thinking about Prudencio Aguilar again.”

同样醒来的乌苏拉问他怎么了，他回答：“我又在想普鲁邓希奥·阿基拉尔了。”

They did not sleep a minute, but the following day they felt so rested that they forgot about the bad night.

他们一分钟也没睡，但第二天他们感到非常休息好，以至于忘了那个糟糕的夜晚。

Aureliano commented with surprise at lunchtime that he felt very well in spite of the fact that he had spent the whole night in the laboratory gilding a brooch that he planned to give to Úrsula for her birthday.

午饭时，奥雷里亚诺惊讶地评论说，尽管他整夜在实验室给一个准备送给乌苏拉做生日礼物的小饰物镀金，他却感觉非常好。

They did not become alarmed until the third day, when no one felt sleepy at bedtime and they realized that they had gone more than fifty hours without sleeping.

直到第三天，他们才感到惊慌，那时睡觉时没人感到困倦，他们意识到已经五十多个小时没有睡觉了。

“The children are awake too,” the Indian said with her fatalistic conviction.

“孩子们也醒着，”印第安女人带着宿命的信念说。

“Once it gets into a house no one can escape the plague.”

“一旦它进入一所房子，谁也逃不掉这场瘟疫。”

They had indeed contracted the illness of insomnia.

他们确实感染了失眠症。

Úrsula, who had learned from her mother the medicinal value of plants, prepared and made them all drink a brew of monkshood, but they could not get to sleep and spent the whole day dreaming on their feet.

乌苏拉从母亲那里学到植物的药用价值，准备好并让他们都喝下乌头汤，但他们睡不着，整天站着做梦。

In that state of hallucinated lucidity, not only did they see the images of their own dreams, but some saw the images dreamed by others.

在那种幻觉般的清醒状态中，他们不仅看到自己梦中的图像，有些人还看到别人梦中的图像。

It was as if the house were full of visitors.

仿佛房子里挤满了访客。

Sitting in her rocker in a corner of the kitchen, Rebeca dreamed that a man who looked very much like her, dressed in white linen and with his shirt collar closed by a gold button, was bringing her a bouquet of roses.

蕾贝卡坐在厨房角落的摇椅上，梦见一个长得非常像她的男人，穿着白色亚麻衣服，衬衫领子用金扣子扣着，带给她一束玫瑰。

He was accompanied by a woman with delicate hands who took out one rose and put it in the child's hair.

陪伴他的是一个双手纤细的女人，她拿出一朵玫瑰插在孩子的头发上。

Úrsula understood that the man and woman were Rebeca's parents, but even though she made a great effort to recognize them, she confirmed her certainty that she had never seen them.

乌苏拉明白那男人和女人是蕾贝卡的父母，但尽管她费了很大劲去认出他们，她还是确认自己从未见过他们。

In the meantime, through an oversight that José Arcadio Buendía never forgave himself for, the candy animals made in the house were still being sold in the town.

与此同时，由于何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚永远无法原谅自己的疏忽，家里做的糖果动物仍在镇上销售。

Children and adults sucked with delight on the delicious little green roosters of insomnia, the exquisite pink fish of insomnia, and the tender yellow ponies of insomnia, so that dawn on Monday found the whole town awake.

孩子们和大人津津有味地吮吸着美味的失眠绿色小公鸡、精致的失眠粉红色小鱼和柔嫩的失眠黄色小马，于是星期一的黎明发现全镇都醒着。

No one was alarmed at first.

起初没人惊慌。

On the contrary, they were happy at not sleeping because there was so much to do in Macondo in those days that there was barely enough time.

相反，他们为不睡觉而高兴，因为那时马孔多有那么多事要做，几乎没有足够的时间。

They worked so hard that soon they had nothing else to do and they could be found at three o'clock in the morning with their arms crossed, counting the notes in the waltz of the clock.

他们工作如此努力，很快就没事可做了，凌晨三点就能看到他们交叉着胳膊，数着时钟华尔兹的音符。

Those who wanted to sleep, not from fatigue but because of the nostalgia for dreams, tried all kinds of methods of exhausting themselves.

那些想睡觉的人，不是因为疲劳，而是因为对梦的怀念，尝试了各种让自己筋疲力尽的方法。

They would gather together to converse endlessly, to tell over and over for hours on end the same jokes, to complicate to the limits of exasperation the story about the capon, which was an endless game in which the narrator asked if they wanted him to tell them the story about the capon, and when they answered yes, the narrator would say that he had not asked them to say yes, but whether they wanted him to tell them the story about the capon, and when they answered no, the narrator told them that he had not asked them to say no, but whether they wanted him to tell them the story about the capon, and when they remained silent the narrator told them that he had not asked them to remain silent but whether they wanted him to tell them the story about the capon, and no one could leave because the narrator would say that he had not asked them to leave but whether they wanted him to tell them the story about the capon, and so on and on in a vicious circle that lasted entire nights.

他们聚在一起无休止地交谈，一连几个小时反复讲同一个笑话，把阉鸡的故事复杂化到令人恼火的极限，那是一个无休止的游戏，讲故事的人问大家要不要听阉鸡的故事，如果大家说“要”，他就说没让大家说“要”，而是问大家要不要听阉鸡的故事；如果大家说“不要”，他就说没让大家说“不要”，而是问大家要不要听阉鸡的故事；如果大家都不说话，他就说没让大家不说话，而是问大家要不要听阉鸡的故事；而且谁也不许走，因为他没让人走，而是问大家要不要听阉鸡的故事。就这样继续下去，整夜整夜重复这一恶性循环。

When José Arcadio Buendía realized that the plague had invaded the town, he gathered together the heads of families to explain to them what he knew about the sickness of insomnia, and they agreed on methods to prevent the scourge from spreading to other towns in the swamp.

当何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚意识到瘟疫已经侵入镇子时，他召集各家家长，向他们解释他所知道的失眠症情况，他们商定了一些方法，防止这场灾难蔓延到沼泽地的其他城镇。

That was why they took the bells off the goats, bells that the Arabs had swapped them for macaws, and put them at the entrance to town at the disposal of those who would not listen to the advice and entreaties of the sentinels and insisted on visiting the town.

因此他们从山羊身上取下铃铛——这些铃铛是阿拉伯人用金刚鹦鹉换给他们的——放在镇口，供那些不听哨兵劝告和恳求、坚持要进镇的人使用。

All strangers who passed through the streets of Macondo at that time had to ring their bells so that the sick people would know that they were healthy.

那时所有经过马孔多街道的陌生人都必须摇铃，以便病人知道他们是健康的。

They were not allowed to eat or drink anything during their stay, for there was no doubt but that the illness was transmitted by mouth, and all food and drink had been contaminated by insomnia.

他们在逗留期间不准吃喝任何东西，因为毫无疑问，疾病是通过嘴巴传播的，所有食物和饮料都已被失眠污染。

In that way they kept the plague restricted to the perimeter of the town.

就这样，他们把瘟疫限制在镇子的范围内。

So effective was the quarantine that the day came when the emergency situation was accepted as a natural thing and life was organized in such a way that work picked up its rhythm again and no one worried any more about the useless habit of sleeping.

隔离如此有效，以至于有一天，紧急情况被视为自然的事，生活被组织起来，工作又恢复了节奏，没人再担心睡觉这个无用的习惯。

It was Aureliano who conceived the formula that was to protect them against loss of memory for several months.

是奥雷里亚诺想出了那个公式，让他们几个月内免于记忆丧失。

He discovered it by chance.

他偶然发现了它。

An expert insomniac, having been one of the first, he had learned the art of silverwork to perfection.

作为最早的失眠专家之一，他已把银匠手艺学得完美无缺。

One day he was looking for the small anvil that he used for laminating metals and he could not remember its name.

有一天他在找用来层压金属的小铁砧，却想不起它的名字。

His father told him: “Stake.” Aureliano wrote the name on a piece of paper that he pasted to the base of the small anvil stake.

父亲告诉他：“铁砧。”奥雷里亚诺把名字写在一张纸上，贴在小铁砧的底座上。

In that way he was sure of not forgetting it in the future.

这样他就确信将来不会忘记它。

It did not occur to him that this was the first manifestation of a loss of memory, because the object had a difficult name to remember.

他没有想到这是记忆丧失的第一个表现，因为这个东西的名字很难记住。

But a few days later he discovered that he had trouble remembering almost every object in the laboratory.

但几天后，他发现几乎记不起实验室里的每一个物品。

Then he marked them with their respective names so that all he had to do was read the inscription in order to identify them.

于是他给它们标上各自的名字，这样他只需读铭文就能辨认它们。

When his father told him about his alarm at having forgotten even the most impressive happenings of his childhood, Aureliano explained his method to him, and José Arcadio Buendía put it into practice all through the house and later on imposed it on the whole village.

当父亲告诉他自己甚至忘记了童年最令人印象深刻的事件而感到惊慌时，奥雷里亚诺向他解释了自己的方法，何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚在全家推行，后来又强加给整个村子。

With an inked brush he marked everything with its name: table, chair, clock, door, wall, bed, pan.

他用蘸墨水的刷子给每样东西标上名字：桌子、椅子、时钟、门、墙、床、锅。

He went to the corral and marked the animals and plants: cow, goat, pig, hen, cassava, caladium, banana.

他到畜栏给动物和植物标名：母牛、山羊、猪、母鸡、木薯、海芋、香蕉。

Little by little, studying the infinite possibilities of a loss of memory, he realized that the day might come when things would be recognized by their inscriptions but that no one would remember their use.

渐渐地，研究记忆丧失的无限可能性，他意识到有一天东西可能会通过铭文被认出，但没人会记得它们的用途。

Then he was more explicit.

于是他更明确了。

The sign that he hung on the neck of the cow was an exemplary proof of the way in which the inhabitants of Macondo were prepared to fight against loss of memory: This is the cow. She must be milked every morning so that she will produce milk, and the milk must be boiled in order to be mixed with coffee to make coffee and milk.

他挂在母牛脖子上的牌子，是马孔多居民准备对抗记忆丧失的典范证明：这是母牛。必须每天早晨挤奶，以便产奶，牛奶必须煮沸，以便与咖啡混合做成牛奶咖啡。

Thus they went on living in a reality that was slipping away, momentarily captured by words, but which would escape irremediably when they forgot the values of the written letters.

就这样，他们继续生活在一种正在溜走的现实中，暂时被文字捕捉，但当他们忘记书写字母的含义时，它就会无可挽回地逃脱。

At the beginning of the road into the swamp they put up a sign that said MACONDO and another larger one on the main street that said GOD EXISTS.

在通往沼泽的路口，他们竖起一块写着“马孔多”的牌子，在主街上又竖起一块更大的，写着“上帝存在”。

In all the houses keys to memorizing objects and feelings had been written.

每家每户都写下了记忆物品和感觉的钥匙。

But the system demanded so much vigilance and moral strength that many succumbed to the spell of an imaginary reality, one invented by themselves, which was less practical for them but more comforting.

但这个系统需要极大的警惕和道德力量，以至于许多人屈服于一种想象现实的魔力——一种他们自己发明的现实，对他们来说不那么实用，但更令人安慰。

Pilar Ternera was the one who contributed most to popularize that mystification when she conceived the trick of reading the past in cards as she had read the future before.

皮拉尔·特尔内拉是最致力于普及这种神秘化的人，她想出了用纸牌解读过去的把戏，就像她以前解读未来一样。

By means of that recourse the insomniacs began to live in a world built on the uncertain alternatives of the cards, where a father was remembered faintly as the dark man who had arrived at the beginning of April and a mother was remembered only as the dark woman who wore a gold ring on her left hand, and where a birth date was reduced to the last Tuesday on which a lark sang in the laurel tree.

通过这种手段，失眠症患者开始生活在一个建立在纸牌不确定选择的世界里，父亲模糊地被记成四月初到来的黑衣男人，母亲只被记成左手戴金戒指的黑衣女人，而出生日期则简化为云雀在月桂树上歌唱的最后一个星期二。

Defeated by those practices of consolation, José Arcadio Buendía then decided to build the memory machine that he had desired once in order to remember the marvelous inventions of the gypsies.

被这些安慰的做法击败后，何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚决定建造他曾经渴望的记忆机器，以便记住吉卜赛人的奇妙发明。

The artifact was based on the possibility of reviewing every morning, from beginning to end, the totality of knowledge acquired during one's life.

这个装置基于每天早晨从头到尾回顾一生所获得全部知识的可能性。

He conceived of it as a spinning dictionary that a person placed on the axis could operate by means of a lever, so that in a very few hours there would pass before his eyes the notions most necessary for life.

他把它设想成一个旋转的字典，人坐在轴上通过杠杆操作，这样在短短几个小时内，生活中最必要的概念就会从眼前掠过。

He had succeeded in writing almost fourteen thousand entries when along the road from the swamp a strange-looking old man with the sad sleepers' bell appeared, carrying a bulging suitcase tied with a rope and pulling a cart covered with black cloth.

他已经成功写下将近一万四千个条目，这时从沼泽路走来一个模样奇怪的老人，带着忧伤的失眠者铃铛，提着一个用绳子捆着的鼓鼓囊囊的手提箱，拉着一辆盖着黑布的手推车。

He went straight to the house of José Arcadio Buendía.

他径直走向何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚的家。

Visitación did not recognize him when she opened the door and she thought he had come with the idea of selling something, unaware that nothing could be sold in a town that was sinking irrevocably into the quicksand of forgetfulness.

比西塔西翁开门时没有认出他，以为他是来卖东西的，不知道在一个不可逆转地陷入遗忘流沙的镇子上，什么也卖不出去。

He was a decrepit man.

他是个衰朽的老人。

Although his voice was also broken by uncertainty and his hands seemed to doubt the existence of things, it was evident that he came from the world where men could still sleep and remember.

虽然他的声音也因不确定而断断续续，双手似乎怀疑事物的存在，但显然他来自人们还能睡觉和记忆的世界。

José Arcadio Buendía found him sitting in the living room fanning himself with a patched black hat as he read with compassionate attention the signs pasted to the walls.

何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚发现他坐在客厅，用一块打补丁的黑帽子扇风，同时带着同情的专注阅读贴在墙上的标牌。

He greeted him with a broad show of affection, afraid that he had known him at another time and that he did not remember him now.

他热情地招呼他，担心自己以前认识他，而现在记不起来了。

But the visitor was aware of his falseness.

但来访者察觉到了他的虚伪。

He felt himself forgotten, not with the irremediable forgetfulness of the heart, but with a different kind of forgetfulness, which was more cruel and irrevocable and which he knew very well because it was the forgetfulness of death.

他感到自己被遗忘了，不是心灵那种不可挽回的遗忘，而是另一种更残酷、更不可逆的遗忘，他非常清楚，因为那是死亡的遗忘。

Then he understood.

于是他明白了。

He opened the suitcase crammed with indecipherable objects and from among them he took out a little case with many flasks.

他打开塞满难以辨认物品的手提箱，从中取出一个装着许多小瓶的小盒子。

He gave José Arcadio Buendía a drink of a gentle color and the light went on in his memory.

他给何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚喝了一种柔和颜色的饮料，他的记忆之光亮了起来。

His eyes became moist from weeping even before he noticed himself in an absurd living room where objects were labeled and before he was ashamed of the solemn nonsense written on the walls, and even before he recognized the newcomer with a dazzling glow of joy.

他的眼睛因哭泣而湿润，甚至在他注意到自己身处一个荒谬的、物品都贴了标签的客厅之前，在他对墙上写的庄严胡话感到羞耻之前，甚至在他以耀眼的喜悦认出来访者之前。

It was Melquíades.

那是梅尔基亚德斯。

While Macondo was celebrating the recovery of its memory, José Arcadio Buendía and Melquíades dusted off their old friendship.

当马孔多庆祝记忆恢复时，何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚和梅尔基亚德斯重温了他们的旧日友情。

The gypsy was inclined to stay in the town.

这位吉卜赛人倾向于留在镇上。

He really had been through death, but he had returned because he could not bear the solitude.

他确实经历过死亡，但他回来了，因为他无法忍受孤独。

Repudiated by his tribe, having lost all of his supernatural faculties because of his faithfulness to life, he decided to take refuge in that corner of the world which had still not been discovered by death, dedicated to the operation of a daguerreotype laboratory.

被部落抛弃，由于忠于生命而失去了所有超自然能力，他决定在这个死亡尚未发现的世界的角落避难，致力于经营一个银版照相实验室。

José Arcadio Buendía had never heard of that invention.

何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚从未听说过这种发明。

But when he saw himself and his whole family fastened onto a sheet of iridescent metal for an eternity, he was mute with stupefaction.

但当他看到自己和全家被永远固定在一张彩虹色的金属板上时，他惊得说不出话来。

That was the date of the oxidized daguerreotype in which José Arcadio Buendía appeared with his bristly and graying hair, his cardboard collar attached to his shirt by a copper button, and an expression of startled solemnity, whom Úrsula described, dying with laughter, as a “frightened general.”

那就是那张氧化的银版照片的日期，何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚出现在上面，头发硬而花白，纸板领子用铜扣子扣在衬衫上，表情惊恐而庄严，乌苏拉笑得要死，把他描述成一个“受惊的将军”。

José Arcadio Buendía was, in fact, frightened on that clear December morning when the daguerreotype was made, for he was thinking that people were slowly wearing away while his image would endure on a metallic plaque.

事实上，何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚在拍摄银版照片的那个晴朗的十二月早晨确实很害怕，因为他想着人们在慢慢消磨，而他的形象却会永远留在金属板上。

Through a curious reversal of custom, it was Úrsula who got that idea out of his head, as it was also she who forgot her ancient bitterness and decided that Melquíades would stay on in the house, although she never permitted them to make a daguerreotype of her because (according to her very words) she did not want to survive as a laughingstock for her grandchildren.

通过一种奇特的习俗逆转，是乌苏拉把这个想法从他脑子里赶了出去，也是她忘记了古老的怨恨，决定让梅尔基亚德斯留在家里，尽管她从未允许他们给自己拍银版照片，因为（用她的话说）她不想作为孙辈的笑柄而存活下来。

That morning she dressed the children in their best clothes, powdered their faces, and gave a spoonful of marrow syrup to each one so that they would all remain absolutely motionless during the nearly two minutes in front of Melquíades' fantastic camera.

那天早晨，她给孩子们穿上最好的衣服，给他们脸上扑粉，每人喂一勺骨髓糖浆，以便他们在梅尔基亚德斯奇妙的相机前近两分钟内完全一动不动。

In the family daguerreotype, the only one that ever existed, Aureliano appeared dressed in black velvet between Amaranta and Rebeca.

在那张家族银版照片——唯一存在的一张——中，奥雷里亚诺穿着黑色天鹅绒，站在阿玛兰塔和蕾贝卡之间。

He had the same languor and the same clairvoyant look that he would have years later as he faced the firing squad.

他有着同样的倦怠和同样预言般的眼神，就像多年后面对行刑队时那样。

But he still had not sensed the premonition of his fate.

但他还没有感觉到自己命运的预兆。

He was an expert silversmith, praised all over the swampland for the delicacy of his work.

他是个熟练的银匠，因手艺精巧而在整个沼泽地受到赞扬。

In the workshop, which he shared with Melquíades' mad laboratory, he could barely be heard breathing.

在他与梅尔基亚德斯疯狂实验室共用的作坊里，几乎听不到他的呼吸声。

He seemed to be taking refuge in some other time, while his father and the gypsy with shouts interpreted the predictions of Nostradamus amidst a noise of flasks and trays and the disaster of spilled acids and silver bromide that was lost in the twists and turns it gave at every instant.

他似乎躲在另一个时间里，而他父亲和吉卜赛人大声解释诺斯特拉达穆斯的预言，伴随着瓶子和托盘的噪音，以及泼洒的酸和溴化银的灾难，这些东西每时每刻都在扭曲中消失。

That dedication to his work, the good judgment with which he directed his attention, had allowed Aureliano to earn in a short time more money than Úrsula had with her delicious candy fauna, but everybody thought it strange that he was now a full-grown man and had not known a woman.

他对工作的专注，以及他引导注意力的良好判断力，让奥雷里亚诺在短时间内赚的钱超过了乌苏拉用美味糖果动物赚的，但大家都觉得奇怪，他现在已是个成年男子，却从未认识过女人。

It was true that he had never had one.

事实是他从未有过。

Several months later saw the return of Francisco the Man, an ancient vagabond who was almost two hundred years old and who frequently passed through Macondo distributing songs that he composed himself.

几个月后，弗朗西斯科·埃尔·奥姆布雷回来了，这位几乎两百岁的古老流浪汉经常经过马孔多，分发他自己创作的歌曲。

In them Francisco the Man told in great detail the things that had happened in the towns along his route, from Manaure to the edge of the swamp, so that if anyone had a message to send or an event to make public, he would pay him two cents to include it in his repertory.

在这些歌中，弗朗西斯科·埃尔·奥姆布雷详细讲述了他沿途城镇发生的事，从马瑙雷到沼泽边缘，因此如果有人有消息要传递或事件要公布，他会付两生太伏让他收进曲目。

That was how Úrsula learned of the death of her mother, as a simple consequence of listening to the songs in the hope that they would say something about her son José Arcadio.

乌苏拉就是这样得知母亲去世的消息的，她听歌只是希望听到关于儿子何塞·阿尔卡迪奥的消息。

Francisco the Man, called that because he had once defeated the devil in a duel of improvisation, and whose real name no one knew, disappeared from Macondo during the insomnia plague and one night he appeared suddenly in Catarino's store.

弗朗西斯科·埃尔·奥姆布雷因曾在即兴对决中击败魔鬼而得此名，没人知道他的真名，他在失眠症瘟疫期间从马孔多消失，一天晚上突然出现在卡塔里诺的店里。

The whole town went to listen to him to find out what had happened in the world.

全镇的人都去听他唱歌，想知道世界上发生了什么。

On that occasion there arrived with him a woman who was so fat that four Indians had to carry her in a rocking chair, and an adolescent mulatto girl with a forlorn look who protected her from the sun with an umbrella.

那次和他一起来的有一个胖女人，胖到四个印第安人必须用摇椅抬她，还有一个神情凄凉的混血少女，用伞为她遮阳。

Aureliano went to Catarino's store that night.

那天晚上奥雷里亚诺去了卡塔里诺的店。

He found Francisco the Man, like a monolithic chameleon, sitting in the midst of a circle of bystanders.

他发现弗朗西斯科·埃尔·奥姆布雷像一只单块变色龙，坐在一群旁观者中间。

He was singing the news with his old, out-of-tune voice, accompanying himself with the same archaic accordion that Sir Walter Raleigh had given him in the Guianas and keeping time with his great walking feet that were cracked from saltpeter.

他用苍老、走调的声音唱着新闻，用沃尔特·雷利爵士在圭亚那给他的那架古老手风琴伴奏，用因硝石而裂开的大脚打着节拍。

In front of a door at the rear through which men were going and coming, the matron of the rocking chair was sitting and fanning herself in silence.

在后面一扇男人进进出出的门前，摇椅上的胖女人静静地坐着扇扇子。

Catarino, with a felt rose behind his ear, was selling the gathering mugs of fermented cane juice, and he took advantage of the occasion to go over to the men and put his hand on them where he should not have.

卡塔里诺耳朵后面别着一朵毡玫瑰，在卖发酵甘蔗汁，他趁机走到男人身边，把手放在不该放的地方。

Toward midnight the heat was unbearable.

接近午夜时，热得无法忍受。

Aureliano listened to the news to the end without hearing anything that was of interest to his family.

奥雷里亚诺从头听到尾，却没听到任何与自己家族有关的事。

He was getting ready to go home when the matron signaled him with her hand.

他正准备回家时，胖女人用手示意他。

“You go in too.” she told him.

“你也进去吧。”她对他说。

“It only costs twenty cents.”

“只要二十生太伏。”

Aureliano threw a coin into the hopper that the matron had in her lap and went into the room without knowing why.

奥雷里亚诺把一枚硬币扔进胖女人膝盖上的漏斗里，走进房间，却不知道为什么。

The adolescent mulatto girl, with her small bitch's teats, was naked on the bed.

那个混血少女，乳房像小母狗的奶头一样小，赤裸着躺在床上。

Before Aureliano sixty-three men had passed through the room that night.

在那天晚上奥雷里亚诺之前，已经有六十三个男人经过这个房间。

From being used so much, kneaded with sweat and sighs, the air in the room had begun to turn to mud.

因为使用过度，被汗水和叹息揉捏，房间里的空气开始变成泥浆。

The girl took off the soaked sheet and asked Aureliano to hold it by one side.

女孩掀开浸透的床单，让奥雷里亚诺抓住一边。

It was as heavy as a piece of canvas.

它像一块帆布一样沉重。

They squeezed it, twisting it at the ends until it regained its natural weight.

他们挤压它，从两端拧，直到它恢复自然重量。

They turned over the mat and the sweat came out of the other side.

他们翻过席子，汗水从另一边流出来。

Aureliano was anxious for that operation never to end.

奥雷里亚诺希望这个操作永远不要结束。

He knew the theoretical mechanics of love, but he could not stay on his feet because of the weakness of his knees, and although he had goose pimples on his burning skin he could not resist the urgent need to expel the weight of his bowels.

他知道爱的理论力学，但因膝盖无力站不住，尽管灼热的皮肤上起了鸡皮疙瘩，他还是无法抵抗排泄肠内重物的迫切需要。

When the girl finished fixing up the bed and told him to get undressed, he gave her a confused explanation: “They made me come in. They told me to throw twenty cents into the hopper and hurry up.”

当女孩整理好床铺让他脱衣服时，他给她一个困惑的解释：“他们让我进来。他们告诉我扔二十生太伏进漏斗，然后快点。”

The girl understood his confusion.

女孩理解他的困惑。

“If you throw in twenty cents more when you go out, you can stay a little longer,” she said softly.

“你出去时再扔二十生太伏，就可以多待一会儿。”她轻声说。

Aureliano got undressed, tormented by shame, unable to get rid of the idea that his nakedness could not stand comparison with that of his brother.

奥雷里亚诺脱掉衣服，被羞耻折磨，无法摆脱自己的裸体无法与哥哥相比的想法。

In spite of the girl's efforts he felt more and more indifferent and terribly alone.

尽管女孩努力，他还是感到越来越冷漠，极其孤独。

“I'll throw in another twenty cents,” he said with a desolate voice.

“我再扔二十生太伏。”他用绝望的声音说。

The girl thanked him in silence.

女孩默默感谢他。

Her back was raw.

她的背已经磨破。

Her skin was stuck to her ribs and her breathing was forced because of an immeasurable exhaustion.

她的皮肤紧贴着肋骨，呼吸因无法估量的疲惫而吃力。

Two years before, far away from there, she had fallen asleep without putting out the candle and had awakened surrounded by flames.

两年前，在离这里很远的地方，她睡前没有熄灭蜡烛，醒来时已被火焰包围。

The house where she lived with the grandmother who had raised her was reduced to ashes.

她和抚养她的祖母住的房子化为灰烬。

Since then her grandmother carried her from town to town, putting her to bed for twenty cents in order to make up the value of the burned house.

从那以后，祖母带着她从一个镇到另一个镇，让她以二十生太伏的价格上床，以弥补烧毁房子的价值。

According to the girl's calculations, she still had ten years of seventy men per night, because she also had to pay the expenses of the trip and food for both of them as well as the pay of the Indians who carried the rocking chair.

根据女孩的计算，她还需要十年，每晚七十个男人，因为她还要支付旅费、两人食物以及抬摇椅的印第安人的工资。

When the matron knocked on the door the second time, Aureliano left the room without having done anything, troubled by a desire to weep.

当胖女人第二次敲门时，奥雷里亚诺什么也没做就离开了房间，内心充满想哭的困扰。

That night he could not sleep, thinking about the girl, with a mixture of desire and pity.

那天晚上他睡不着，想着那个女孩，混合着欲望和怜悯。

He felt an irresistible need to love her and protect her.

他感到一种无法抗拒的需要，要爱她、保护她。

At dawn, worn out by insomnia and fever, he made the calm decision to marry her in order to free her from the despotism of her grandmother and to enjoy all the nights of satisfaction that she would give the seventy men.

黎明时分，被失眠和发烧折磨得筋疲力尽，他平静地决定娶她，以便把她从祖母的专制中解放出来，并享受她本该给七十个男人的所有满足之夜。

But at ten o'clock in the morning, when he reached Catarino's store, the girl had left town.

但早上十点，当他到达卡塔里诺的店时，女孩已经离开镇子了。

Time mitigated his mad proposal, but it aggravated his feelings of frustration.

时间缓和了他疯狂的提议，但加剧了他的挫败感。

He took refuge in work.

他躲进工作中。

He resigned himself to being a womanless man for all his life in order to hide the shame of his uselessness.

他 resigned 自己一生无女人，以掩盖自己无用的羞耻。

In the meantime, Melquíades had printed on his plates everything that was printable in Macondo, and he left the daguerreotype laboratory to the fantasies of José Arcadio Buendía who had resolved to use it to obtain scientific proof of the existence of God.

与此同时，梅尔基亚德斯已把马孔多所有可印的东西都印在板上，他把银版照相实验室留给何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚的幻想，后者决心用它获得上帝存在的科学证明。

Through a complicated process of superimposed exposures taken in different parts of the house, he was sure that sooner or later he would get a daguerreotype of God, if He existed, or put an end once and for all to the supposition of His existence.

通过在房子不同部位进行复杂叠加曝光的过程，他确信迟早会拍到上帝的银版照片——如果上帝存在的话——或者一劳永逸地结束对上帝存在的假设。

Melquíades got deeper into his interpretations of Nostradamus.

梅尔基亚德斯更深入地解读诺斯特拉达穆斯。

He would stay up until very late, suffocating in his faded velvet vest, scribbling with his tiny sparrow hands, whose rings had lost the glow of former times.

他会熬到很晚，闷在他褪色的天鹅绒背心里，用麻雀般的小手涂写，那些戒指已失去昔日的光辉。

One night he thought he had found a prediction of the future of Macondo.

一天晚上，他以为自己找到了马孔多未来的预言。

It was to be a luminous city with great glass houses where there was no trace remaining of the race of the Buendía.

那将是一座光明的城市，有巨大的玻璃房屋，布恩迪亚家族的痕迹荡然无存。

“It's a mistake,” José Arcadio Buendía thundered.

“这是个错误，”何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚咆哮道。

“They won't be houses of glass but of ice, as I dreamed, and there will always be a Buendía, per omnia secula seculorum.”

“它们不会是玻璃房子，而是冰房子，就像我梦见的那样，而且永远会有一个布恩迪亚，世世代代。”

Úrsula fought to preserve common sense in that extravagant house, having broadened her business of little candy animals with an oven that went all night turning out baskets and more baskets of bread and a prodigious variety of puddings, meringues, and cookies, which disappeared in a few hours on the roads winding through the swamp.

乌苏拉努力在那座奢华的房子里保持常识，她扩大了糖果动物生意，增加了一个通宵运转的烤箱，生产出一篮又一篮面包，以及种类繁多的布丁、蛋白酥和饼干，这些东西在几小时内就消失在蜿蜒穿过沼泽的道路上。

She had reached an age where she had a right to rest, but she was nonetheless more and more active.

她已到了有权休息的年龄，但她却越来越活跃。

So busy was she in her prosperous enterprises that one afternoon she looked distractedly toward the courtyard while the Indian woman helped her sweeten the dough and she saw two unknown and beautiful adolescent girls doing frame embroidery in the light of the sunset.

她在繁荣的事业中如此忙碌，以至于一天下午，当印第安女人帮她给面团加糖时，她心不在焉地望向院子，看到两个陌生而美丽的少女在夕阳下做框架刺绣。

They were Rebeca and Amaranta.

她们是蕾贝卡和阿玛兰塔。

As soon as they had taken off the mourning clothes for their grandmother, which they wore with inflexible rigor for three years, their bright clothes seemed to have given them a new place in the world.

她们刚脱下为祖母穿了三年、严格不变的丧服，鲜亮的衣服似乎给了她们在世界上的新位置。

Rebeca, contrary to what might have been expected, was the more beautiful.

与预期相反，蕾贝卡更漂亮。

She had a light complexion, large and peaceful eyes, and magical hands that seemed to work out the design of the embroidery with invisible threads.

她肤色白皙，眼睛大而平静，有一双魔术般的手，似乎用隐形的线绣出图案。

Amaranta, the younger, was somewhat graceless, but she had the natural distinction, the inner tightness of her dead grandmother.

年轻的阿玛兰塔有些笨拙，但她有天生的优雅，有已故祖母内在的紧致。

Next to them, although he was already revealing the physical drive of his father, Arcadio looked like a child.

在她们旁边，虽然已经显露出父亲的身体冲动，阿尔卡迪奥看起来像个孩子。

He set about learning the art of silverwork with Aureliano, who had also taught him how to read and write.

他开始跟奥雷里亚诺学习银匠手艺，后者还教他读书写字。

Úrsula suddenly realized that the house had become full of people, that her children were on the point of marrying and having children, and that they would be obliged to scatter for lack of space.

乌苏拉突然意识到房子已经挤满了人，她的孩子即将结婚生子，由于空间不足，他们将被迫分散。

Then she took out the money she had accumulated over long years of hard labor, made some arrangements with her customers, and undertook the enlargement of the house.

于是她取出多年来辛勤劳动积累的钱，与顾客做了些安排，开始扩建房子。

She had a formal parlor for visits built, another one that was more comfortable and cool for daily use, a dining room with a table with twelve places where the family could sit with all of their guests, nine bedrooms with windows on the courtyard and a long porch protected from the heat of noon by a rose garden with a railing on which to place pots of ferns and begonias.

她建了一个正式的会客客厅，另一个更舒适凉爽的日常用客厅，一个有十二个座位的餐厅，家人可以和所有客人一起就坐，九间面向院子的卧室，以及一条长廊，用玫瑰园遮挡正午的炎热，栏杆上可以摆放蕨类和秋海棠盆栽。

She had the kitchen enlarged to hold two ovens.

她扩大了厨房，容纳两个烤箱。

The granary where Pilar Ternera had read José Arcadio's future was torn down and another twice as large built so that there would never be a lack of food in the house.

皮拉尔·特尔内拉曾为何塞·阿尔卡迪奥算命的粮仓被拆掉，建了一个两倍大的新粮仓，确保家里永远不缺食物。

She had baths built in the courtyard in the shade of the chestnut tree, one for the women and another for the men, and in the rear a large stable, a fenced-in chicken yard, a shed for the milk cows, and an aviary open to the four winds so that wandering birds could roost there at their pleasure.

她在院子里栗树荫下建了浴室，一个给女人，一个给男人，后面有一个大马厩、一个围起来的鸡舍、一个奶牛棚，以及一个四面透风的鸟舍，让流浪的鸟儿随意栖息。

Followed by dozens of masons and carpenters, as if she had contracted her husband's hallucinating fever, Úrsula fixed the position of light and heat and distributed space without the least sense of its limitations.

在几十个泥瓦匠和木匠的跟随下，仿佛感染了丈夫的幻觉热病，乌苏拉确定了光和热的位置，分配空间，完全没有局限感。

The primitive building of the founders became filled with tools and materials, of workmen exhausted by sweat, who asked everybody please not to molest them, exasperated by the sack of bones that followed them everywhere with its dull rattle.

创始人的原始建筑里堆满了工具和材料，工人们汗流浃背、筋疲力尽，他们恳请大家不要打扰他们，被那个到处跟着他们、发出沉闷咯咯声的骨头袋激怒。

In that discomfort, breathing quicklime and tar, no one could see very well how from the bowels of the earth there was rising not only the largest house in the town, but the most hospitable and cool house that had ever existed in the region of the swamp.

在那种不适中，呼吸着生石灰和焦油，没人能看清从大地深处升起的不仅是镇上最大的房子，还是沼泽地区有史以来最热情好客、最凉爽的房子。

José Arcadio Buendía, trying to surprise Divine Providence in the midst of the cataclysm, was the one who least understood it.

何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚试图在灾难中捕捉神圣天意，却是最不理解它的人。

The new house was almost finished when Úrsula drew him out of his chimerical world in order to inform him that she had an order to paint the front blue and not white as they had wanted.

新房子快完工时，乌苏拉把他从幻想世界中拉出来，告诉他有一项命令要把正面刷成蓝色，而不是他们想要的白色。

She showed him the official document.

她给他看了官方文件。

José Arcadio Buendía, without understanding what his wife was talking about, deciphered the signature.

何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚不明白妻子在说什么，他辨认了签名。

“Who is this fellow?” he asked.

“这是谁？”他问。

“The magistrate,” Úrsula answered disconsolately.

“镇长，”乌苏拉沮丧地回答。

“They say he's an authority sent by the government.”

“他们说他是政府派来的权威。”

Don Apolinar Moscote, the magistrate, had arrived in Macondo very quietly.

镇长堂阿波利纳尔·莫斯科特悄无声息地来到马孔多。

He put up at the Hotel Jacob—built by one of the first Arabs who came to swap knickknacks for macaws—and on the following day he rented a small room with a door on the street two blocks away from the Buendía house.

他住在雅各布旅馆——由最早用小玩意换金刚鹦鹉的阿拉伯人之一建造——第二天，他在离布恩迪亚家两个街区的一条街上租了一间有街门的房间。

He set up a table and a chair that he had bought from Jacob, nailed up on the wall the shield of the republic that he had brought with him, and on the door he painted the sign: Magistrate.

他摆上从雅各布那里买的桌子和椅子，在墙上钉上他带来的共和国徽章，在门上刷了“镇长”字样。

His first order was for all the houses to be painted blue in celebration of the anniversary of national independence.

他的第一项命令是所有房屋刷成蓝色，以庆祝国家独立纪念日。

José Arcadio Buendía, with the copy of the order in his hand, found him taking his nap in a hammock he had set up in the narrow office.

何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚手里拿着命令的副本，发现他在狭窄的办公室里挂的吊床上打盹。

“Did you write this paper?” he asked him.

“这文件是你写的吗？”他问他。

Don Apolinar Moscote, a mature man, timid, with a ruddy complexion, said yes.

堂阿波利纳尔·莫斯科特，一个成熟、胆小、面色红润的男人，说是的。

“By what right?” José Arcadio Buendía asked again.

“凭什么权利？”何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚又问。

Don Apolinar Moscote picked up a paper from the drawer of the table and showed it to him.

堂阿波利纳尔·莫斯科特从桌子抽屉里拿出一张纸给他看。

“I have been named magistrate of this town.”

“我被任命为这个镇的镇长。”

José Arcadio Buendía did not even look at the appointment.

何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚甚至没看任命书。

“In this town we do not give orders with pieces of paper,” he said without losing his calm.

“在这个镇上，我们不用纸片发号施令，”他平静地说。

“And so that you know it once and for all, we don't need any judge here because there's nothing that needs judging.”

“让你一次性明白，我们这里不需要任何法官，因为没什么需要审判的。”

Facing Don Apolinar Moscote, still without raising his voice, he gave a detailed account of how they had founded the village, of how they had distributed the land, opened the roads, and introduced the improvements that necessity required without having bothered the government and without anyone having bothered them.

面对堂阿波利纳尔·莫斯科特，他仍然没有提高声音，详细讲述了他们如何建立村子，如何分配土地、开辟道路，以及根据需要引入改进，而没有麻烦政府，也没有人麻烦他们。

“We are so peaceful that none of us has died even of a natural death,” he said.

“我们如此和平，以至于我们中没有人甚至自然死亡，”他说。

“You can see that we still don't have any cemetery.”

“你可以看到我们还没有墓地。”

No one was upset that the government had not helped them.

没人因为政府没有帮助他们而生气。

On the contrary, they were happy that up until then it had let them grow in peace, and he hoped that it would continue leaving them that way, because they had not founded a town so that the first upstart who came along would tell them what to do.

相反，他们很高兴政府直到那时都让他们和平成长，他希望政府继续这样，因为他们建立镇子不是为了让第一个暴发户来告诉他们该做什么。

Don Apolinar had put on his denim jacket, white like his trousers, without losing at any moment the elegance of his gestures.

堂阿波利纳尔穿上他的斜纹布夹克，和裤子一样白，举止始终保持优雅。

“So that if you want to stay here like any other ordinary citizen, you're quite welcome,” José Arcadio Buendía concluded.

“所以如果你想像其他普通公民一样留在这里，非常欢迎，”何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚总结道。

“But if you've come to cause disorder by making the people paint their houses blue, you can pick up your junk and go back where you came from.

“但如果你来制造混乱，让人们把房子刷成蓝色，你可以收拾你的东西回你来的地方。

Because my house is going to be white, white, like a dove.”

因为我的房子要刷成白色，白色，像鸽子一样。”

Don Apolinar Moscote turned pale.

堂阿波利纳尔·莫斯科特脸色发白。

He took a step backward and tightened his jaws as he said with a certain affliction:

他后退一步，咬紧牙关，带着某种痛苦说：

“I must warn you that I'm armed.”

“我必须警告你，我有武器。”

José Arcadio Buendía did not know exactly when his hands regained the useful strength with which he used to pull down horses.

何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚不知道自己的双手什么时候恢复了以前用来拉倒马匹的有用力量。

He grabbed Don Apolinar Moscote by the lapels and lifted him up to the level of his eyes.

他抓住堂阿波利纳尔·莫斯科特的领子，把他举到与自己眼睛平齐的高度。

“I'm doing this,” he said, “because I would rather carry you around alive and not have to keep carrying you around dead for the rest of my life.”

“我这样做，”他说，“是因为我宁愿活着扛着你走，也不愿余生一直扛着你死的尸体。”

In that way he carried him through the middle of the street, suspended by the lapels, until he put him down on his two feet on the swamp road.

就这样，他抓住领子把他悬空扛着穿过街道中央，直到把他放在沼泽路上的双脚上。

A week later he was back with six barefoot and ragged soldiers, armed with shotguns, and an oxcart in which his wife and seven daughters were traveling.

一周后，他带着六个赤脚、衣衫褴褛、扛着猎枪的士兵回来了，还有一辆牛车，上面坐着他的妻子和七个女儿。

Two other carts arrived later with the furniture, the baggage, and the household utensils.

后来又来了两辆车，装着家具、行李和家用器具。

He settled his family in the Hotel Jacob, while he looked for a house, and he went back to open his office under the protection of the soldiers.

他把家人安顿在雅各布旅馆，自己找房子，并在士兵的保护下重新打开办公室。

The founders of Macondo, resolving to expel the invaders, went with their older sons to put themselves at the disposal of José Arcadio Buendía.

马孔多的创始人决定驱逐入侵者，带着大儿子们去找何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚听候调遣。

But he was against it, as he explained, because it was not manly to make trouble for someone in front of his family, and Don Apolinar had returned with his wife and daughters.

但他反对，正如他解释的，因为当着人家的家人面找麻烦不是男子汉的行为，而且堂阿波利纳尔是带着妻子和女儿回来的。

So he decided to resolve the situation in a pleasant way.

于是他决定以愉快的方式解决情况。

Aureliano went with him.

奥雷里亚诺和他一起去。

About that time he had begun to cultivate the black mustache with waxed tips and the somewhat stentorian voice that would characterize him in the war.

大约那时他开始蓄黑胡子，胡尖打蜡，还有在战争中将标志他的那种略带洪亮的声音。

Unarmed, without paying any attention to the guards, they went into the magistrate's office.

他们没有武器，不理会卫兵，走进镇长办公室。

Don Apolinar Moscote did not lose his calm.

堂阿波利纳尔·莫斯科特没有失去冷静。

He introduced them to two of his daughters who happened to be there: Amparo, sixteen, dark like her mother, and Remedios, only nine, a pretty little girl with lily-colored skin and green eyes.

他把他们介绍给恰好在那里的两个女儿：十六岁的安帕罗，像母亲一样肤色黝黑，还有只有九岁的蕾梅黛丝，一个漂亮的小女孩，皮肤像百合一样白，眼睛是绿色的。

They were gracious and well-mannered.

她们优雅而有礼貌。

As soon as the men came in, before being introduced, they gave them chairs to sit on.

男人们一进来，还没介绍，她们就给他们椅子坐。

But they both remained standing.

但两人都站着。

“Very well, my friend,” José Arcadio Buendía said, “you may stay here, not because you have those bandits with shotguns at the door, but out of consideration for your wife and daughters.”

“很好，我的朋友，”何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚说，“你可以留在这里，不是因为你门口有那些扛猎枪的强盗，而是出于对你妻子和女儿的考虑。”

Don Apolinar Moscote was upset, but José Arcadio Buendía did not give him time to reply.

堂阿波利纳尔·莫斯科特很不安，但何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚没给他时间回答。

“We only make two conditions,” he went on.

“我们只提两个条件，”他继续说。

“The first: that everyone can paint his house the color he feels like.

“第一：每个人可以按自己喜欢的颜色刷房子。

The second: that the soldiers leave at once.

第二：士兵立刻离开。

We will guarantee order for you.”

我们会为你保证秩序。”

The magistrate raised his right hand with all the fingers extended.

镇长举起右手，五指张开。

“Your word of honor?”

“以你的名誉担保？”

“The word of your enemy,” José Arcadio Buendía said.

“以你敌人的名义，”何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚说。

And he added in a bitter tone: “Because I must tell you one thing: you and I are still enemies.”

他又用苦涩的语气补充：“因为我必须告诉你一件事：你和我仍然是敌人。”

The soldiers left that same afternoon.

士兵们当天下午就离开了。

A few days later José Arcadio Buendía found a house for the magistrate's family.

几天后，何塞·阿尔卡迪奥·布恩迪亚为镇长一家找到了一所房子。

Everybody was at peace except Aureliano.

除了奥雷里亚诺，大家都平静了。

The image of Remedios, the magistrate's younger daughter, who, because of her age, could have been his daughter, kept paining him in some part of his body.

镇长小女儿蕾梅黛丝的形象——由于年龄，她本可以是他的女儿——不断在他身体的某个部位刺痛他。

It was a physical sensation that almost bothered him when he walked, like a pebble in his shoe.

这是一种身体感觉，走路时几乎让他烦恼，就像鞋里有一颗石子。
