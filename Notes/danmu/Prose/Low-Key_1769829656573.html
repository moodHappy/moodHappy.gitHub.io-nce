Low-Key

Everyone at the office thought Mark was boring.

He arrived at 8:57 every morning, left at 5:30 sharp, and never stayed late. No overtime. No speeches. No opinions in meetings unless someone asked him directly.

When people complained about work, Mark nodded. When people bragged, Mark smiled and stayed quiet.

“Does he even care?” someone once whispered.

Mark cared. He just didn’t advertise it.

---

One Thursday afternoon, the system crashed.

Emails froze. Files vanished. The server light blinked red like a warning sign in a movie.

Panic spread fast.

“Who touched the database?” “Did we lose everything?” “Call IT—now!”

Phones rang. Voices rose.

Mark didn’t say a word.

He walked to his desk, opened his laptop, and plugged in a small black USB drive.

Ten minutes later, the files reappeared.

Emails refreshed. The red light turned green.

Silence filled the room.

“What… just happened?” the manager asked.

Mark looked up. “Backup,” he said. “I made one last week. Just in case.”

“That saved us hours,” the manager said. “Why didn’t you tell anyone?”

Mark shrugged. “No one asked.”

---

Later that day, someone said, “Man, you should show off more.”

Mark packed his bag. “Nah,” he said. “Low-key works fine.”

He stepped outside into the evening air, put on his headphones, and walked home like it was just another day.

And for him, it was.
