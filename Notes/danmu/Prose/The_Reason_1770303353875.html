The Reason

起因

1. The Verdict

判决

The voice on the phone was her oncologist.

电话里的那个声音是她的肿瘤科医生。

The test results had come back confirming the diagnosis.

检查结果回来了，确诊了。

The treatment plan was aggressive and immediate.

治疗方案很激进，而且要立刻开始。

It was not a suggestion; it was a schedule.

那不是建议，是一个时间表。

Her body was no longer just hers; it belonged to the protocol.

她的身体不再只属于她自己；它属于那套治疗方案了。

2. The Warning

预警

"You will likely lose your hair within the first three weeks," the doctor said.

“你很可能会在前三周内脱发，”医生说。

He spoke of follicles and toxicity.

他谈到了毛囊和毒性。

He described it as a side effect.

他把这描述为一种副作用。

To her, it sounded like an erasure.

对她来说，这听起来像是一种抹除。

He advised her to buy a wig before she needed one.

他建议她在需要之前先买好假发。

He said it helps with the transition.

他说这有助于过渡。

3. The Nightmare

噩梦

She imagined waking up to a pillow covered in dark webs.

她想象着醒来时，看到枕头上布满了黑色的网。

She imagined washing her hair and having clumps come away in her hands.

她想象着洗头时，成团的头发脱落在手里。

She imagined the slow, messy defeat of watching it fall.

她想象着看着它掉落时那种缓慢而狼狈的溃败。

That was a horror she refused to wait for.

那是她拒绝等待的恐惧。

She did not want the disease to take her hair.

她不想让疾病夺走她的头发。

4. The Logic

逻辑

If her hair was going to go, she would be the one to send it away.

如果头发终究要走，她要是那个送走它的人。

It was the only thing left she could control.

这是她仅剩的能掌控的事。

Cutting it was not an act of beauty.

剪发不是为了美。

It was a preemptive strike.

这是一种先发制人的打击。

She would rather look like a boy than a victim.

她宁愿看起来像个男孩，也不愿像个受害者。

By the time the medicine entered her veins, the hair would already be gone.

等到药物进入血管时，头发已经不在了。

She would not give the cancer the satisfaction of taking it from her.

她不会让癌症拥有从她身上夺走它的快感。
