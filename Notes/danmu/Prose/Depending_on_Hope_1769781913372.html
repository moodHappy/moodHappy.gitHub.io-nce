Depending on Hope

Every morning, I wake up and check my phone. I don’t know exactly what I’m expecting, but I’m definitely expecting something.

Sometimes I expect a message. Sometimes I expect good news. And sometimes, I just expect the day to be a little easier than yesterday.

I rely on small things more than I’d like to admit. A warm cup of coffee. A quiet bus ride. Someone saying, “It’s going to be okay,” even if they’re not sure.

There are days when I realize I’m really just depending on hope. Not a big dream. Not a clear plan. Just the hope that things won’t get worse.

I used to expect too much from people. I thought if I waited long enough, someone would understand me completely. But life doesn’t work that way. People are busy. People are tired. People have their own problems.

So now, I try to expect less from others and rely more on myself. I still hope. I still wait. But I don’t put all my weight on one person or one moment.

Depending on something is not a bad thing. We all do it. The trick is knowing what to depend on — and when to let go.
