Chapter 50: The Audit of Nostalgia

发布时间：2026/6/20 21:29:05

第五十章：记忆的审计局

The crowd’s fury didn’t dissipate; it crystallized into a jagged, collective sense of humiliation. Those who had spent their savings to inhale sewage gas felt their very identities being dismantled by Miyetsuko’s scorn. They clutched their melting perfume bottles like defiled IDs, their faces contorted with the realization that they had paid to be insulted.

人群的怒火并未消散，反而凝结成了一种尖锐的集体羞辱感。那些花光积蓄去吸入污水气味的人，此刻感到连同自己的身份都被美悦子的轻蔑彻底瓦解了。他们攥着半融化的香水瓶，那神情仿佛握着一张被作废的身份证明，脸上的表情扭曲着——他们终于意识到，自己花钱买的竟是一场针对尊严的羞辱。

A man in a crisp municipal uniform shouldered his way to the front, brandishing a temporary lockdown order. "Under the Public Odor Safety Act, this establishment is subject to immediate asset freeze." But before he could even finish, he was shoved aside. A second, more ominous group strode forward. Their badges weren't for standard regulation; they bore the heavy, gold-embossed seal of the 'Memory Audit Commission.'

一名身着制服的市政人员挤到前排，出示了临时封控令：“根据《公共气味安全条例》，该店即刻资产冻结。”话音未落，他便被推开了——另一批更具威慑力的人接管了现场。他们胸口的徽章并非普通的监管标志，而是沉甸甸的、金箔压制的“记忆审查委员会”。

Miyetsuko, still perched on the counter, didn't flinch. She watched the bureaucrats with a predatory curiosity, her smile widening. "Finally," she purred, holding out the megaphone like a peace offering. "Someone who actually understands the rules of the game. You want to judge reality? You want to regulate the past? Fine."

美悦子依旧稳坐收银台，毫无惧色。她带着掠食者般的好奇注视着这些官僚，嘴角勾起：“终于来了，懂规则的人。你们想裁决现实？想监管过去？很好。”

She thrust the megaphone into the hands of the lead auditor. "Go ahead. Tell them. Are their first loves legally binding? Are their memories compliant with the current regulatory standards? Make your ruling."

她将扩音器递给首席审查官，示意他开口：“来吧，告诉他们。这些人的初恋是合法的吗？他们的记忆符合当前的监管标准吗？做个判决吧。”

The Auditor didn't look at Miyetsuko. He looked at the trembling crowd with the hollow, clinical stare of a man who viewed human history as a series of spreadsheets.

审查官甚至没看美悦子，只是用一种看待报表般的空洞眼神扫视着人群。

"The Commission is not here to provide retroactive emotional validation," he stated, his voice flat, devoid of empathy. "The quality of your past is subject to audit. Any citizen harboring 'unverified nostalgia' or 'non-compliant emotional residues' is hereby required to report to the Memory Verification Bureau within 24 hours."

“委员会不负责提供追溯性的情感认可，”他的声音平铺直叙，毫无温度，“你们过去回忆的质量，正处于审计范围内。任何持有‘未经核准的怀旧’或‘非合规情绪残留’的公民，必须在24小时内前往记忆审查检查处报到。”

"Your memories," he concluded, adjusting his glasses, "are not your own. They are assets. And every asset is subject to seizure if it is found to be fraudulent."

“你们的记忆，”他调整了一下眼镜，冷冷补充道，“不是你们自己的。它们是资产。任何被判定为‘欺诈性资产’的回忆，都将被依法没收。”

The silence that followed was total. The crowd, once fueled by the anger of being cheated, was now struck by the paralyzing fear of being erased.

广场上一片死寂。刚才还因被骗而愤怒的人群，此刻被一种面临“抹除”的恐惧死死钉在原地。

[Learning Point]

Crystallized: 结晶/凝结（形容情绪）。

Defiled: 被玷污的。

Retroactive: 追溯的。

Compliant: 合规的/顺从的。

Fraudulent: 欺诈的/虚假的。
