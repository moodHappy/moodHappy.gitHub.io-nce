Chapter 20: The Council of Jackals

第二十章：豺狼议会

They didn't hide in the shadows; they invited the survivors to the table.

他们没有躲在阴影里，而是邀请幸存者们坐到了桌前。

Four of the region's most powerful brokers—smugglers, fixers, and bankers of blood money—arrived at the villa under a white flag.

该地区四位最有权势的中间人——走私犯、掮客和血钱银行家——打着白旗来到了别墅。

They expected a monster, but they found a woman who moved like incense smoke.

他们预想会见到一个怪物，却发现了一个行动如檀香烟雾般优雅的女人。

Miyetsuko presided over a dinner of local delicacies, her presence a silent command that stilled the air.

美悦子主持了一场当地佳肴的晚宴，她的存在本身就是一种让空气凝结的无声号令。

The gold silk of her dress clung to her curves, a constant reminder of the physical cost of her rise.

她身上金色的丝绸勾勒着曲线，时刻提醒着人们她上位所付出的肉体代价。

"You killed two kings in three days," Elder Khun, the oldest smuggler on the river, said through a cloud of tobacco.

“你在三天内杀了两个国王，”河上最资深的走私大鳄坤老，穿过烟草的雾气说道。

"Now the river is screaming for order. Who are you to give it?"

“现在整条河都在渴求秩序。你凭什么来给？”

Miyetsuko didn't flinch; she leaned forward, the neckline of her dress dipping low enough to disturb the old man's focus.

美悦子没有退缩，她倾身向前，领口低垂得足以扰乱老人的注意力。

"I am the one who survived Tokyo and the jungle," she whispered, her voice a silk cord tightening.

“我是那个在东京和丛林中都活下来的人，”她低声说道，声音宛如一根正在收紧的丝弦。

"I don't want your titles, and I don't want your blood. I want your loyalty, paid in a 30% tax on every kilo that moves through the water."

“我不要你们的头衔，也不要你们的血。我要你们的忠诚，以每公斤水路货物抽取30%的税收来偿还。”

Chen Mo stood behind her, his hand resting visibly on the grip of his customized pistol, a silent promise of the alternative.

陈默站在她身后，手明显地搭在定制手枪的握把上，这是一个无声的替代方案承诺。

The brokers looked at each other, weighing the beauty of the woman against the lethality of her shadow.

中间人们面面相觑，在女人的美貌与其影子的致命性之间权衡。

"And if we refuse?" another broker asked, his hand trembling slightly.

“如果我们拒绝呢？”另一位中间人问道，手微微颤抖。

"Then you can join the General in the mud," Miyetsuko smiled, a slow, beautiful threat.

“那么你们可以去泥潭里陪将军，”美悦子笑了，这是一个缓慢而华丽的威胁。

"I am a widow, gentlemen. I have nothing to lose, and everything to gain."

“先生们，我是个寡妇。我没有什么可以失去的，只有一切可以赢得。”
