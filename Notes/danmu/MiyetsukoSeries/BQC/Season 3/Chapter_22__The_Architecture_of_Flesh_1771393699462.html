第二十二章：血肉建筑

Phnom Penh. The Royal Palace Hotel.

金边。皇宫酒店。

The air in Phnom Penh was thick with the scent of river mud and incense, a heavy blanket that smothered the city's French colonial elegance.

金边的空气中弥漫着河泥与香火的味道，如同一块沉重的毯子，笼罩着这座城市法式殖民时期的优雅。

Miyetsuko stood on the balcony of their suite, looking out at the confluence of the Mekong and the Tonle Sap.

美悦子站在套房的阳台上，俯瞰着湄公河与洞里萨河的交汇处。

She wore a gown of silver silk that shimmered like moonlight on oil, the fabric clinging to her curves with predatory precision.

她穿着一件银色丝绸长裙，闪烁着如油面月光般的光泽，布料以猎食者般的精准贴合着她的曲线。

"They call it 'Labor Export' in the papers, Chen," she said, her voice a low, lethal vibration.

“报纸上管这叫‘劳务输出’，陈，”她说道，声音带着低沉而致命的颤动。

"But we both know the price of 'Golden Silk'. It’s the most expensive commodity in the world because it has a soul."

“但我们都清楚‘金丝’的价格。它是世界上最昂贵的商品，因为它拥有灵魂。”

Chen Mo sat at a mahogany desk, his fingers dancing across a keyboard as he encrypted the flight manifests.

陈默坐在红木桌旁，手指在键盘上跳动，对飞行舱单进行加密。

The dark business of human trafficking required a cleaner touch than opium; it required the silence of a grave.

比起鸦片，人口贩运这种阴暗生意需要更干净的手段；它需要墓地般的寂静。

"The buyers from the Gulf are arriving tomorrow," Chen Mo reported without looking up.

“海湾地区的买家明天到达，”陈默头也不回地报告道。

"They don't want soldiers. They want 'entertainers'. And they are paying in diamonds."

“他们不要士兵。他们要的是‘艺人’。而且他们支付的是钻石。”

Miyetsuko ran a finger along the line of her jaw, the Golden Derringer a familiar weight against her thigh beneath the silver silk.

美悦子的手指勾勒着自己的下颚线，银色丝绸下的大腿侧，金色德林加手枪是她熟悉的重量。

"In Tokyo, I was the one being sold," she whispered, a cold light flickering in her eyes.

“在东京，我是那个被贩卖的人，”她低声呢喃，眼中闪烁着冷光。

"In Phnom Penh, I am the one who holds the ledger. The transition is... intoxicating."

“在金边，我是握着账本的人。这种转变……令人陶醉。”
