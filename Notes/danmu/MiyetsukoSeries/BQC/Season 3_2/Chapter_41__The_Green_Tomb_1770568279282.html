Chapter 41: The Green Tomb

第四十一章：绿色坟墓

The Land Rover smashed through a wall of vines and came to a halt. Steam hissed from the radiator, mixing with the heavy mist of the Atlantic Forest.

路虎撞穿一堵藤蔓墙，停了下来。散热器发出嘶嘶声，蒸汽喷涌而出，混合在亚马逊大西洋雨林的浓雾中。

"We are here," Chen Mo killed the engine.

“到了，”陈默熄火。

The "safehouse" was barely a house. It was a concrete bunker from the 70s military dictatorship era, half-buried in the mud and roots of a massive fig tree. No lights. No water. Just the sound of insects and the dripping of condensation.

这个“安全屋”几乎算不上房子。那是70年代军政府时期留下的混凝土掩体，半埋在一棵巨大无花果树的泥土和树根里。没有灯。没有水。只有昆虫的鸣叫和冷凝水滴落的声音。

Chen Mo kicked the rusted iron door open. He lit a kerosene lantern. The yellow light flickered, casting long, dancing shadows on the damp walls.

陈默踢开生锈的铁门。点亮了一盏煤油灯。黄色的火光摇曳，在潮湿的墙壁上投下长长的、跳舞的影子。

Miyetsuko walked in. She limped slightly. The adrenaline crash was hitting her hard now. Her body felt heavy, like it was made of lead.

美悦子走了进来。她走得有点跛。肾上腺素的消退正在狠狠反噬她。她的身体感觉很沉，像灌了铅。

She sat on a rotting wooden crate, the only furniture in the room.

她坐在一只腐烂的木箱上，那是房间里唯一的家具。

"It smells like a grave," she whispered, looking at the moss growing on the ceiling.

“这儿闻起来像坟墓，”她低声说，看着天花板上生长的苔藓。

"It's better than a prison cell," Chen Mo placed the lantern on the floor and locked the heavy door. "And it's invisible to thermal satellites. The canopy covers us."

“总比牢房好，”陈默把灯放在地上，锁上了厚重的门。“而且热成像卫星看不到这儿。树冠遮住了我们。”
