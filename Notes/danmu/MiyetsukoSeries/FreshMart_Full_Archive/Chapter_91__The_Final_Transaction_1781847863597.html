第九十一章：最后的契约

The bell above the "Fresh Mart" door chimed with a melancholy, brassy note. An elderly man shuffled in, his shoulders hunched, skin paper-thin. He moved with a fragile, rhythmic gait toward the pharmacy aisle. Miyetsuko, manning the counter, looked up from her ledger—and froze.

“新鲜马特”门上的铜铃发出了一声忧郁的脆响。一位老人驼着背，皮肤薄如蝉翼，颤颤巍巍地挪进店内，步履蹒跚地走向药妆货架。美悦子正低头看着账本，抬头一瞬，整个人僵住了。

It was Mr. Tanaka. Her high school literature teacher. The man who had once taught her how to dissect Shakespeare now stood before her, clutching a single condom like a holy relic.

那是田中先生。她高中的文学老师。那个曾经教她如何解析莎士比亚的男人，此刻正站在她面前，手中紧紧捏着一只避孕套，仿佛那是什么神圣的遗物。

"Mr. Tanaka?" she breathed.

“田中老师？”她轻声唤道。

The old man looked up. His eyes were milky, tired, and deeply resigned. "Miyetsuko-san. It’s been a long time." He placed the item on the counter with a trembling hand. "I’m sorry for the trouble. I’ve been given a deadline by my doctor. Terminal. I’ve decided... I’ve decided I’d rather go out feeling something, rather than just fading away in a hospital bed."

老人抬起头，那双浑浊的眼睛里透着深不见底的疲惫与坦然。“美悦子同学。好久不见了。”他将那件东西颤颤巍巍地放在柜台上。“抱歉打扰了。医生给我下了最后通牒。晚期了。我决定……我决定与其在病床上静静枯萎，不如在最后感受一下什么。”

Miyetsuko stared at the condom, then at the man who had once shaped her mind. She saw the quiet, terrifying dignity in his choice. "You’re worried about the ethics of it? About 'disrupting business'?" she asked, her voice uncharacteristically soft.

美悦子盯着那只避孕套，又看向这位曾启蒙过她的男人。她看到了他选择中那种安静而惊人的尊严。“你是在担心伦理问题？担心‘影响生意’？”她问，语气罕见地温柔。

"I don't want to leave a mess," he whispered. "But I have so little time, and so much... need."

“我不想留下什么麻烦，”他低语，“但我剩下的时间不多了，而我心中的……渴望，却还这么多。”

Miyetsuko didn't laugh. She didn't offer pity. She saw a problem, and she looked for the most efficient solution. She thought of Aya—the girl who turned pleasure into a high-stakes, 30-million-yen art form. She knew Aya wouldn't care about the morbidity of the situation; she would only care about the complexity of the execution.

美悦子没有嘲笑，也没有表现出怜悯。她看到了一个问题，并寻求着最有效的解决方案。她想到了绫——那个将快感变成三千万日元艺术品的女孩。她知道绫不会在意这种沉重的话题，她只在乎执行的复杂程度。

"I'll handle it," Miyetsuko declared. She picked up the phone, dialed, and spoke in short, crisp sentences. Within an hour, a black car pulled up to the back alley. Aya stepped out, looking efficient and sterile.

“我会处理的，”美悦子果断地说。她拿起手机拨通了电话，简短地交代了几句。一小时内，一辆黑车停在了后巷。绫走了下来，看起来既高效又冷峻。

Miyetsuko didn't explain the price tag to the teacher. She covered the cost herself, treating it as a final, private scholarship for a man who had taught her everything about the complexity of the human spirit.

美悦子没有向老师解释那昂贵的账单。她自掏腰包支付了费用，将其视为给这位曾教会她人性复杂之处的男人的最后一笔私人奖学金。

[The Final Act / 终场演出]

Aya led Mr. Tanaka to the quiet room upstairs, her demeanor not that of a sex worker, but of a high-end hospice nurse for the soul. She was professional, gentle, and utterly committed.

绫将田中先生带到楼上的静室，她的态度不像是援交女，倒像是一位服务灵魂的高级临终关怀护士。她专业、温柔，且无比敬业。

Inside, the atmosphere was a stark contrast to the harsh world outside. Aya didn't rush. She treated his fragile, aging body with a reverence that made him weep. She used the condom, not as a barrier, but as a seal of a sacred pact.

屋内，气氛与残酷的外界截然不同。绫没有催促。她怀着一种近乎虔诚的态度对待他那副脆弱、衰老的身体，令老者泪流满面。她使用了那只避孕套，不是作为屏障，而是作为这份神圣契约的封印。

She guided him through the motions, her technique so flawless, so attentive to his every failing nerve, that the pain of his terminal illness seemed to dissolve into the background. She was the anchor he needed. She gave him the intensity he craved, a final, blinding spark of life before the darkness took over.

她引导着他完成每一个动作，技术完美无瑕，细致入微地呵护着他每一根衰竭的神经，让那种晚期的病痛仿佛消融在了背景之中。她是他在这一刻最需要的锚点。她给了他渴望已久的狂野，在黑暗彻底吞噬前，给予了他最后那抹耀眼的生命之光。

When it ended, he was gone. He died not in agony, but in a state of suspended, quiet ecstasy, his face etched with a look of profound, blissful relief.

当一切结束，他也走了。他没有在痛苦中挣扎，而是在一种悬浮的、寂静的极乐中离世，脸上定格着那种深沉而安详的解脱。

Aya walked out, her face calm, and handed the room key to Miyetsuko.

绫走了出来，神色平静，将房门钥匙递还给美悦子。

"He paid in full," Aya said quietly. "He found exactly what he came for."

“他付清了余款，”绫轻声说，“他找到了他想要的。”

Miyetsuko looked at the key, then back at her sister. In the silence of the "Fresh Mart," she felt a strange, chilling respect for the way they had managed the final exit of a man who had only ever wanted to be treated like a human being until the very last second.

美悦子看着钥匙，又看向妹妹。在“新鲜马特”的寂静中，她对她们处理这位老人最后告别的方式，感到了一种既奇怪又冷冽的敬意——毕竟，他仅仅是希望在最后一秒前，还能被当作一个有血有肉的人来对待。
