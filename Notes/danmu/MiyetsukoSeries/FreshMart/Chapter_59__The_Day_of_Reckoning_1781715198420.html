Chapter 59: The Day of Reckoning

发布时间：2026/6/18 00:53:17

第五十九章：挂牌歇业

The smell of browned butter and crispy bacon still lingered in the air as they sat around the kitchen table. Fujiwara wiped a smear of egg yolk from Sayuri’s chin, his thumb lingering for a second too long.

厨房里还弥漫着焦黄黄油和培根的余香。藤原伸手擦掉小百合下巴上的一抹蛋黄，大拇指在她皮肤上停留了片刻。

"The shop is a zoo today," Fujiwara remarked, sipping his coffee. "And frankly, my mind is nowhere near the inventory."

“今天超市里简直是乱象丛生，”藤原抿了口咖啡，评论道，“说实话，我现在的思绪离那些库存可是十万八千里。”

Miyetsuko, leaning back with her eyes closed, feet propped up on the table, let out a slow, contented exhale. "I don't think I can handle a single customer today. I don't want to hear 'where's the milk' or 'do you have this discount' just one more time."

美悦子靠在椅子上闭着眼，脚随意地搭在桌边，长长地呼出一口舒心的气。“我今天实在受不了任何一个顾客了。我不想再听到什么‘牛奶在哪里’或者‘还有没有折扣’这种鬼话。”

Sayuri looked at them, a mischievous spark in her eyes. "So, don't open. Put the sign up. It’s our store, isn't it?"

小百合看着他们，眼中闪过一丝俏皮的火花。“那就别开了。挂上牌子就是了。这超市是我们的，不是吗？”

The decision was made with a strange, exhilarating ease. They walked to the front of the shop. Fujiwara grabbed the "OPEN" sign, flipped it to "CLOSED," and locked the glass door from the inside, drawing the blinds for good measure.

这个决定下得既随意又痛快。他们走到店门口。藤原抓起那块“营业中”的牌子，翻到“休息中”那一面，反锁了玻璃门，甚至还谨慎地拉下了卷帘门。

The silence that followed was total. No ringing bell, no customer footsteps, no hum of the barcode scanner.

随后，店内陷入了彻底的寂静。没有了门铃声，没有了顾客的脚步声，也没有了扫码枪的嗡嗡声。

"Now what?" Fujiwara asked, turning to face them.

“现在干什么？”藤原转过身问。

"Something we’ve never done," Miyetsuko suggested, walking to the shelves. She began to clear a space on the middle rack, pushing aside the mundane cans of tuna and soup. "Let’s play 'what if.' What if we didn't have to be the owners of this place? What if we just... treated this whole floor like our living room for the next twelve hours?"

“做点我们从来没做过的事，”美悦子提议道，走到货架前。她开始清理中间那层货架，把那些乏味的罐头和汤包推到一边。“来玩个‘如果’游戏吧。如果我们要是不当这儿的老板呢？如果我们在接下来的十二个小时里，把这整个楼面当成我们的客厅呢？”

Sayuri clapped her hands, her face lighting up. "Let's turn the music up and rearrange the aisles. We can build a fort out of the cereal boxes."

小百合拍手称快，一脸兴奋。“把音乐开大，重新布置货架。我们用麦片盒搭个堡垒吧！”

They spent the morning in a blur of laughter and chaotic redecoration. They turned the aisles into a labyrinth, moved the inventory to create cozy little nooks, and turned the speakers up so loud the floor vibrated with jazz. They were three people playing house in a grocery store, stripped of the burden of the "National Icon," the "Failed Tycoon," and the "Depressed Survivor."

整个上午，他们都在笑声和混乱的装修中度过。他们把过道改成迷宫，移动存货搭出舒适的小角落，音响开得震天响，爵士乐让地板都在震动。他们只是三个在超市里玩过家家的普通人，卸下了“国民偶像”、“失败大亨”和“抑郁幸存者”的沉重包袱。

They sprawled out on the floor in the beverage aisle, surrounded by rows of colorful bottles, sharing a bottle of the store's finest wine.

他们摊在饮料区的地板上，周围簇拥着一排排五颜六色的瓶子，分享着店里最好的那瓶酒。

"You know," Fujiwara said, looking at the two women who had become his entire universe, "I spent years building things that felt like monuments. But this? This ridiculous, lazy, unproductive day? This is the only thing that's ever felt real."

“你知道吗，”藤原看着这两个成了他全部宇宙的女人，轻声说，“我花了好几年去建立那些看似丰碑的东西。但这个呢？这个荒唐、懒散、毫无建树的一天？这才是唯一让我觉得真实的东西。”

Miyetsuko smiled, a soft, genuine look that had been missing for so long. "Real is overrated, Fujiwara. Happy is better."

美悦子笑了，那种失踪了很久的、温柔且真诚的神情回到她脸上。“真实这词被高估了，藤原。开心才是最重要的。”

[Learning Point]

Reckoning: 清算/结算。

Mundane: 乏味的。

Exhilarating: 令人振奋的。

Inventory: 库存。

Labyrinth: 迷宫。
