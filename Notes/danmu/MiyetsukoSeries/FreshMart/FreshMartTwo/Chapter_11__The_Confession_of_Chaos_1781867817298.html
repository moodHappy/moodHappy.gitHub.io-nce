第十一章：谎言的告解室

Valentine’s Day at "Fresh Mart" wasn't about chocolate hearts or cheap roses. It was about the truth—the kind of truth that burns buildings down.

情人节的“新鲜马特”没有巧克力红心，也没有廉价玫瑰。这里只剩真话——那种能把房子烧成灰烬的真相。

Fujiwara and Miyetsuko had converted the register area into a makeshift "Confessional Booth." Hidden under the counter was a high-gain microphone, wired directly into the massive, playground-grade speakers mounted on the roof. They weren't looking for romance; they were looking for the spectacle of human unraveling.

藤原和美悦子把收银台改造成了简陋的“告解室”。柜台下藏着高灵敏度麦克风，直连屋顶上那套操场级的扩音器。他们不图浪漫，只想要一场人性崩塌的奇观。

[The Parade of Sins / 罪恶的游行]

The community stepped up, oblivious to the fact that their "whispered" sins were booming across the neighborhood.

社区居民们走进来，浑然不知他们“低声”告解的罪行正响彻整个街区。

The Pre-Wedding Exhaustion: A woman, draped in lace, leaned into the booth. "I didn't 'catch the flu' the day before my wedding," she whispered. "I spent the entire weekend being drained by my ex-boyfriend until I couldn't walk. I postponed the wedding for a month just so I could heal." The speakers shrieked her secret to every house within a mile.

婚前的“透支”： 一位披着蕾丝婚纱的女人靠向话筒。“婚礼前一天，我根本不是‘得了流感’，”她低语，“我整个周末都被前男友搞到彻底透支，连路都走不动。我推迟婚期整整一个月，就是为了休养。”扩音器将她的秘密向方圆一英里内轰炸而去。

The Secret Line: A man in a tailored suit sighed. "My wife thinks I’m working late. I’m actually in the back of my car, sexting my ex-wife until dawn." The street outside stopped dead as the confession echoed off the asphalt.

秘密连线： 一个穿西装的男人叹气，“我老婆以为我在加班，其实我一直躲在车后座，跟前妻短信调情到天亮。”街道上的人停下了脚步，告解声在柏油路上回荡。

The Kindergarten Scandal: A father, looking perfectly respectable, muttered, "My kid loves his kindergarten teacher. I love her too—in the bed, during lunch breaks, while my wife picks up the kids."

幼儿园丑闻： 一个看起来体面的父亲嘟囔：“孩子喜欢他的幼儿园老师。我也喜欢——在午休时间的床上，趁老婆去接孩子的时候。”

The Domestic Repulsion: A woman wept, "I’m married to a man who demands 'family time' in the bedroom, but he’s so boring, so clinical... I lie there every night, faking it, dreaming of being anywhere else."

家庭反胃： 一个女人哭泣：“我嫁给了一个要求‘家庭时刻’的男人，但他太无聊、太机械了……我每天晚上都躺在那儿，装模作样，心里只想着逃离。”

The Twisted Obsession: Finally, an old man stepped up, his voice raspy. "I didn't marry my wife for her. I’m just waiting for the hag to kick the bucket so I can finally have her daughter—my stepdaughter—all to myself. I’ve been grooming her for years."

扭曲的执念： 最后，一个老头走上前，声音嘶哑。“我娶我老婆根本不是为了她。我只是在等那个老太婆早点死，好把她女儿——也就是我的继女——占为己有。我暗中调教她已经好多年了。”

[The Battlefield / 情人节战场]

The reaction was instantaneous. A woman in the back of the store, who had been holding her husband’s hand, dropped his grip as if it were burning coal. She let out a scream that rivaled the sirens. A chair flew across the store, smashing into a display of expensive wine.

反应是瞬间爆发的。店后方，一个原本正牵着丈夫手的女人像触电般甩开了对方。她发出一声堪比警笛的尖叫。一把椅子飞向空中，狠狠砸烂了名贵葡萄酒展示架。

"You lying piece of shit!"

“你这个撒谎的畜生！”

The "Fresh Mart" erupted. It wasn't just a breakup; it was a riot. Spouses turned into warriors. The "Confessional" itself was the first casualty—a flying punch from a betrayed husband shattered the soundproofing, followed by a flurry of kicks that turned the booth into splinters.

“新鲜马特”炸锅了。这不仅仅是分手，这是一场暴乱。配偶们化身为战士。“告解室”成了第一牺牲品——一个被背叛的丈夫一记重拳砸烂了隔音板，紧接着是一阵乱踢，把告解室拆成了碎片。

Fujiwara and Miyetsuko retreated to the back, watching the carnage with glasses of champagne.

藤原和美悦子撤到后方，端着香槟看着这满地狼藉。

"Well," Fujiwara remarked, dodging a flying handbag. "At least we don't have to worry about selling Valentine’s Day gifts anymore."

“好吧，”藤原躲开一只飞来的手袋，点评道，“至少咱们不用发愁情人节礼物卖不出去了。”

"True," Miyetsuko laughed, watching a couple wrestle over a box of chocolates. "The truth really is the most expensive thing you can buy at a store."

“也是，”美悦子看着一对夫妻正为了盒巧克力扭打在一起，大笑起来，“真话果然是这家店里最昂贵的商品。”

[Learning Point]

Confessional Booth: 告解室。

Spectacle: 奇观/场面。

Unraveling: 崩溃/解体。

Casualty: 牺牲品/伤亡者。

Carnage: 大屠杀/大乱子（这里指破坏场面）。
