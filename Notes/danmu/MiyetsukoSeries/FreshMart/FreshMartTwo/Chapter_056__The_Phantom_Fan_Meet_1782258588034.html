Chapter 56: The Phantom Fan Meet

发布时间：2026/6/24 07:49:33

第五十六章：不存在的见面会

The "National Phenom" fan meet was billed as the event of the century. Thousands of souls—men in suits, students in uniforms, and weary laborers—had braved the transit grid and paid exorbitant ticket prices to catch a glimpse of Sayuri, the screen goddess who had defined the collective fantasies of a generation.

这场国民级影迷见面会被宣传为世纪盛事。成千上万的人——穿着西装的上班族、穿着制服的学生，以及劳累的工人——不惜挤破交通网，支付了高昂的票价，只为一睹小百合的芳容，这位曾经定义了一代人集体幻想的银幕女神。

The venue was a cavernous, desolate warehouse on the edge of the industrial zone. When the doors finally groaned open, the surge of fans rushed inside, their cameras ready, their voices chanting her name like a secular prayer.

场馆是工业区边缘的一座空旷、荒凉的仓库。当大门终于发出令人牙酸的吱呀声，粉丝们涌入现场，相机严阵以待，呼喊声如同某种世俗的祈祷。

They didn't find Sayuri. They found Fujiwara, Miyetsuko, and Rin.

但他们没看见小百合。他们只看到了藤原、美悦子和铃。

The trio sat behind a flimsy card table that wobbled under the weight of an abandoned box of expired rice crackers. Fujiwara wore a "Sayuri Fan Club" sash that was stained with machine grease; Miyetsuko was filing her nails with a piece of sandpaper; and Rin was simply staring at a blank wall, twitching occasionally.

三人坐在一张破旧的折叠桌后，那桌子在几盒过期仙贝的重压下摇摇欲坠。藤原戴着一条沾满机油的“小百合粉丝会”绶带；美悦子正用砂纸磨指甲；铃则面无表情地对着白墙，偶尔抽搐一下。

The silence in the warehouse was deafening. A lone fan, a man in his fifties clutching a vintage DVD, stepped forward. "Where is she? Where is Sayuri?"

仓库里死一般的寂静。一个五十岁左右、攥着一张绝版DVD的粉丝颤抖着走上前：“她在哪？小百合在哪？”

Fujiwara looked up, his face devoid of any customer-service warmth. "She had a schedule conflict," he said flatly. "The 'Toilet Saint' had a sudden urge to visit the local amusement park. It was a divine, maternal imperative."

藤原抬起头，脸上没有半点服务业的温情。“她有档期冲突，”他淡然回应，“‘马桶圣子’突然想去游乐场了。这是神圣的、母性的迫切指令。”

Somewhere across the city, the "goddess" was actually in the middle of a ferris wheel car. She was holding the emaciated, silent infant, staring out at the smog-choked horizon of the neon metropolis. She wasn't thinking about her fans, or the thousands of people currently rioting in a warehouse. She was feeding the child a lukewarm corn dog she’d stolen from a carnival stall.

此刻在城市另一头，这位“女神”正坐在摩天轮的轿厢里。她抱着那个瘦弱、沉默的婴儿，凝视着霓虹都市那被雾霾笼罩的尽头。她没想粉丝，也没想仓库里正在暴动的几千人。她正往那孩子嘴里塞着一根从嘉年华摊位上偷来的温热玉米热狗。

"Look, Saint," she cooed, her voice stripped of the performative lust she was famous for. "Look at the lights. They think they’re watching you, but they’re just watching the ghost of a girl they used to touch in their sleep. Let them wait."

“看，圣子，”她轻柔地说，嗓音里褪去了那曾让她闻名遐迩的表演性色情，“看那些灯。他们以为他们在看着你，其实他们只是在看着一个他们在睡梦中抚摸过的女孩的幽灵。让他们等着吧。”

Back at the warehouse, the riot began. People were throwing their memorabilia at the card table. The fan with the DVD had collapsed into a sobbing heap, clutching his chest.

仓库这边，暴动开始了。人们将纪念品扔向那张卡片桌。那个拿着DVD的粉丝瘫倒在地，捂着胸口嚎啕大哭。

Miyetsuko didn't even flinch when a flying autograph book bounced off her forehead. She simply looked at the enraged crowd and sighed, "You really are pathetic. You paid to see a woman who sold you a dream, and now you're angry because she's out living a reality you aren't part of? Go home. The merchandise is for sale if you have the credits. Otherwise, stop breathing my air."

面对飞来的签名册，美悦子连眼皮都没眨一下。她冷眼扫过愤怒的人群，叹道：“真可悲。你们花钱买了一场梦，现在却因为她正在过着你们参与不了的生活而愤怒？回家去吧。想要周边，拿积分买。否则，别在这浪费我的空气。”

[Learning Point]

Phenom: 现象级人物。

Exorbitant: 过高的/过分的。

Secular: 世俗的。

Cavernous: 空旷的/深渊般的。

Imperative: 命令/义务。
