第二十三章：小镇的“纯白”浩劫

The marketing launch for "The Sayuri Juicer" wasn't just a product drop; it was a societal inundation.

“小百合牌榨汁机”的发布会根本不是什么新品上线，简直是一场社会性的洪水灾害。

The launch event was held in the middle of the town square. With the confidence of a seasoned performance artist, Sayuri stood atop a makeshift pedestal, surrounded by velvet ropes. When she slowly unzipped her dress and let it pool at her feet, revealing the lush, untamed reality of her anatomy—the 1:1, biologically accurate mold for the product—the crowd didn't just cheer. They roared. It was a primal, collective release of tension.

发布会选在镇中心广场。小百合展现出一种资深行为艺术家般的自信，站在临时搭建的展台上。当她缓缓拉开裙子，让裙摆坠落在脚踝，露出了那片茂盛、狂野且真实的原始丛林——那个与产品一比一还原的“倒模”源头——台下的人群不仅是欢呼，简直是在咆哮。那是一种原始的、集体的压抑释放。

The comparison was surgical. The silicone replica was held up against the real thing, and the match was flawless. Within seconds, the "limited edition" tag became a cruel joke. The production line in the back warehouse shifted into overdrive, churning out units by the thousands, then tens of thousands.

那场面极其硬核。硅胶复制品与本体比对，毫无瑕疵。几秒钟后，“限量版”的招牌就成了一个彻头彻尾的笑话。后仓的生产线全速运转，几千个、几万个成品如流水般涌出。

[The White Plague / 白色瘟疫]

By the next week, the town of Oakhaven had changed. It was no longer a place of suburbia; it was a landscape of obsessive, public indulgence.

一周后，奥克黑文镇变天了。它不再是那个宁静的郊区，而是一片充斥着偏执与公开沉溺的景象。

Men were everywhere, and they were, frankly, busy.

大街小巷，男人无处不在，而且，坦白说，他们忙得很。

You couldn't take the bus without hearing the rhythmic schlick-schlick of a fellow passenger intensely focused on his lap. You couldn't walk into a bistro without seeing a diner ignoring his spaghetti in favor of his silicone companion under the table. Even the local library—once a bastion of quiet—was now a sanctuary of rapid, frantic friction.

你坐公交车，耳边全是邻座那充满节奏感的“滋溜”声；走进餐馆，食客们连意面都顾不上吃，桌子底下的手动作娴熟；就连曾经神圣安静的图书馆，现在也成了某种狂热、快速摩擦的避难所。

The town was being painted. From the park benches to the bus stops, a sticky, pearlescent film began to coat the urban furniture. It was as if the town had been caught in a localized blizzard of pure, biological excess.

整座小镇都在被涂抹。从公园长椅到候车亭，一层粘稠、半透明的乳白色薄膜开始覆盖所有公共设施。就好像这座小镇遭逢了一场局部的、生物性的、充满狂欢气息的暴风雪。

[The Management’s View / 管理层的“视角”]

Fujiwara and Miyetsuko watched the chaos from the "Fresh Mart" window, sipping coffee. The shop was essentially a temple of commerce, with people lining up not for groceries, but for spare lubricants and replacement sleeves.

藤原和美悦子端着咖啡，透过“新鲜马特”的玻璃窗看着这一切。便利店现在俨然成了商业圣殿，人们排队不是为了买杂货，而是为了囤积润滑油和替换装。

"They’re painting the town," Miyetsuko remarked, pointing to a man on a bicycle who was steering with his knees while holding his "Juicer" with both hands. "Literally."

“他们在给这座小镇刷漆呢，”美悦子指着一个正用膝盖骑车、双手却忙着侍奉他那“榨汁机”的男人评价道，“字面意义上的。”

Fujiwara sighed, wiping a spot of white splatter off the storefront window. "It’s a hygiene nightmare. But look at the balance sheet, Miyetsuko. We’ve reached peak economic efficiency. We’re basically the sole provider of serotonin for the entire male population."

藤原叹了口气，擦掉店门玻璃上的一点白色飞溅物。“这简直是卫生的噩梦。但你看看账单，美悦子。咱们达到了经济效益的巅峰。我们成了整个小镇男性人口血清素的唯一供应商。”

Sayuri walked up behind them, looking at the street below. She felt a strange sense of pride. She had gone from a "fallen" idol to the patron saint of the town’s collective climax.

小百合走到他们身后，俯瞰着街道。她心中竟产生了一种奇妙的自豪感。她从一个“堕落”的偶像，一跃成了这座小镇集体高潮的守护圣女。

"Should we clean the streets?" Sayuri asked, genuinely curious.

“我们要清理下街道吗？”小百合单纯地问。

Fujiwara looked at the bustling, productive, and utterly manic town, then back at the massive stack of cash behind the counter.

藤原看着这座熙熙攘攘、高效运转且彻底疯狂的小镇，又看了看收银台后那堆积如山的钞票。

"No," he said, turning the sign to 'Closed' and locking the door. "Let them paint. It’s the only way they’ll ever leave a mark on history."

“不，”他说着，把门上的牌子翻到“已打烊”，锁上了门，“让他们涂吧。这是他们能在历史上留下痕迹的唯一方式了。”

[Learning Point]

Inundation: 洪水/泛滥（形容市场饱和）。

Pedestal: 基座/展台。

Squelch / Schlick: 湿润的摩擦声。

Pearlescent: 珍珠色的（此处委婉描述那令人作呕的白色薄膜）。

Serotonin: 血清素（快乐荷尔蒙）。
