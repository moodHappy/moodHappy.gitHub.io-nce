Chapter 42: The Vault of Eternity

发布时间：2026/6/17 19:06:49

第四十二章：下一站：喜马拉雅，云端之上的禁忌

Venice was a drowned grave of vanity; they were ready for something closer to the heavens. Fujiwara had become obsessed with the idea that Sayuri needed a "temple" that wasn't built by human hands. He pointed to the highest, most inaccessible point of the Himalayas in Tibet, a region so thin on air that locals believed it to be the border between the physical world and the afterlife.

威尼斯是一座沉入水中的虚荣坟墓，他们渴望去往更接近天际的地方。藤原产生了一个执念：小百合需要一座非人力所能及的“圣殿”。他指向了西藏喜马拉雅山脉中海拔最高、最无法触及的区域——那里的空气稀薄得让当地人相信它是生者与死者世界的边界。

They didn't arrive by plane. They bought a private fleet of custom-modified, pressurized helicopters that looked like black, predatory dragonflies cutting through the clouds.

他们没有乘飞机，而是购置了一支私人机队。那些经过特殊定制的增压直升机，像是一群黑色的、贪婪的蜻蜓，在云层中穿梭。

"Why here?" Sayuri asked, wrapped in layers of furs that cost a fortune, standing on a ledge where the wind screamed like a trapped soul.

“为什么要选这里？”小百合问。她裹着价值连城的皮草，站在悬崖边，耳边是如困兽般尖啸的狂风。

Fujiwara didn't answer with words. He gestured to the mountains—jagged, uncaring, and older than time itself. "Because down there," he said, gesturing to the distant, blurred globe below them, "people talk about your 'comeback.' Up here, you are simply the cold, thin air that everyone else is gasping for."

藤原没有言语，只是指向那些参差嶙峋、漠视一切、比时间更古老的山脉。“因为在下面，”他指着下方那个模糊的星球，“人们还在讨论你的‘复出’。而在这里，你就是那道所有人都在渴望、却又因太冷而无法呼吸的寒风。”

He commissioned a hermitage to be carved directly into the sheer face of the rock. It was a brutal, minimalist construction of black glass and heated slate, perched on a precipice so narrow that only a helicopter could access it.

他命人直接在这绝壁之上凿出了一座隐修院。那是一座由黑色玻璃和地热板岩构成的粗犷极简建筑，悬挂在陡峭的悬崖边缘，窄得只有直升机能抵达。

The locals in the valley below lived in terror and awe. They watched the black dragonflies descend to the peak every evening, convinced that a trio of vengeful demons had occupied the summit. They left offerings at the base of the mountain: yak butter, stones etched with mantras, and bowls of grain.

谷底的当地人陷入了恐惧与敬畏之中。每当傍晚时分看着那些黑色“蜻蜓”降落在那座孤峰，他们便认定那是三尊复仇的恶魔占据了山巅。他们会在山脚留下牦牛酥油、刻着咒语的石头和盛满谷物的碗作为祭品。

Fujiwara loved it. He would take Sayuri out onto the glass-floored balcony that jutted out over a ten-thousand-foot drop.

藤原对此乐此不疲。他会将小百合带到那个悬挑在万丈深渊之上的全玻璃阳台上。

"They leave offerings," Fujiwara said, drinking a high-altitude tea that cost more than a worker's yearly salary. "They see you as a spirit now. You’re not an idol to them; you’re a force of nature. Do you feel the difference?"

“他们留下了祭品，”藤原饮下一杯价值不菲的高山茶，说道，“他们现在把你当成灵体。你对他们来说已经不再是偶像，而是一股自然力量。感受到这种差别了吗？”

Sayuri, dressed in the heavy, ceremonial robes of a local oracle he had "purchased" for her, stood at the edge. She didn't look back at him. She looked out at the rolling sea of clouds, feeling the vibration of the mountain in her bones.

小百合披着他为她“购置”的厚重神谕法袍，站在边缘。她没有回头看他，而是眺望着翻涌的云海，感受着山脉在骨骼中传来的震颤。

"I feel... empty," she said, her voice swallowed by the mountain air. "And for the first time, it’s not because I’m being consumed. It’s because I’m finally high enough that I don't need to be filled by anyone."

“我感到……空灵，”她的声音被山间的空气吞没，“而这是第一次，这种空不是因为我正在被消费，而是因为我终于高到了不再需要被任何人填补的地步。”

Miyetsuko, standing nearby, began to laugh—a sharp, clear sound that echoed off the granite peaks. "She's evolving, Fujiwara. We didn't come here to start a cult; we came here to watch her turn into the god she was only pretending to be."

美悦子在一旁低笑，那声音在花岗岩山峰间激荡，清脆且冷冽。“她在进化，藤原。我们来这里不是为了搞什么邪教；我们是来看着她，从扮演神，变成真正的神。”

They lived there for weeks, above the clouds, while the world below speculated about where the "missing icon" had vanished. They became a myth, a ghost story told in bars and boardrooms, while they sat on their frozen balcony, drinking the air of the world's ceiling and waiting for the next outrage.

他们在云端住了数周，而山下的世界则在疯狂猜疑“失踪的偶像”究竟去了哪里。他们成了神话，成了酒馆和董事会里的都市传说，而他们却坐在那冰封的阳台上，饮着世界之巅的空气，静候下一次惊世骇俗的暴动。
