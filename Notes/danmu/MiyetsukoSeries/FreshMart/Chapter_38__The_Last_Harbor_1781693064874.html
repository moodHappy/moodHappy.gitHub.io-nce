Chapter 38: The Last Harbor

发布时间：2026/6/17 18:44:23

第三十八章：最后的港湾

The "Fresh Mart" stood in the afternoon sun, its glass door dusty and the "Open" sign hanging crookedly. The shelves inside were mostly empty, the vibrant inventory of their early days replaced by a few scattered, long-forgotten cans and the heavy, lingering scent of a life lived in secret. It was the most pathetic, stagnant corner of their empire, yet it remained the only place where the heavy armor of their public lives could be set aside.

“新鲜马特”伫立在午后的阳光下，玻璃门上积着灰尘，那块“营业中”的挂牌歪歪扭扭地挂着。店内的货架大多空置着，早年间繁杂的货品已不见踪影，只剩下几罐被人遗忘的零星罐头，空气中沉淀着一种隐秘生活特有的厚重气息。这是他们帝国中最萧条、最停滞的角落，却也是唯一能让他们卸下公众生活那副沉重铠甲的地方。

Fujiwara stood by the register, counting the last of the daily till—a handful of coins that wouldn't cover the electricity bill. The irony wasn't lost on him. They had built a fortune in the digital ether, yet here, in the physical world, they were struggling to sell a bottle of water.

藤原站在收银台旁，清点着最后一点日结款——一把连电费都凑不够的硬币。这种讽刺感让他感到一种微妙的荒谬。他们在数字世界里构建了财富帝国，可在这里，在这个物理世界里，他们连一瓶水都卖不出去。

Miyetsuko walked in, draped in a designer coat that cost more than the entire inventory of the shop. She surveyed the barren shelves with a sharp, ironic glint in her eyes. "You know, Fujiwara," she said, her voice lilting with a mocking, affectionate humor, "I don't think closing the shop will actually inconvenience anyone. I’m fairly certain we don't get three customers in a day, even when we are open."

美悦子走了进来，身上披着一件价格抵得上整个小店库存的名牌大衣。她扫视着荒芜的货架，眼中闪烁着锐利而讽刺的光。“你知道吗，藤原，”她语调轻快，带着那种戏谑而宠溺的幽默，“我觉得关店这事儿，根本没人会觉得不方便。我敢肯定，就算我们真的开着门，一天也不见得会有三个客人。”

Fujiwara chuckled, the sound deep and resonant in the quiet shop. He looked at the shop—the site of their first conspiracies, the cage where they had played hunter and hunted, and finally, the sanctuary that had held their most intimate moments.

藤原轻笑出声，那笑声在寂静的店里显得低沉而回响。他环顾着这家店——他们最初那些阴谋的发源地，他们扮演猎人与猎物的囚笼，以及最终，承载了他们最私密时刻的避难所。

Sayuri emerged from the back room, her bags already packed, her face soft and expectant. She didn't look like a national icon; she looked like a girl ready for her first true adventure.

小百合从后仓走出，行李早已收拾妥当，面容柔和而充满期待。她看上去不像什么国民偶像，只像是一个即将踏上人生第一次真正探险的女孩。

"Everything is ready," Sayuri said, her gaze lingering on the walls as if memorizing the shadows. "The world is waiting, isn't it?"

“都准备好了，”小百合说，视线在墙面上徘徊，仿佛在铭记那些阴影，“世界就在外面等着我们，对吧？”

Fujiwara stepped forward and flipped the sign on the door. From "Open" to "Closed." It was a small, definitive gesture that felt like the closing of a chapter.

藤原走上前，翻转了门上的招牌。从“营业中”变成了“休息中”。这个细小而果断的动作，仿佛在为一段漫长的章节画上句点。

"We don't need the money," Fujiwara said, looking at the two women who had become his world. "We don't need the fame. For the first time, we have everything we ever took, and we’re going to spend it on the only thing that actually matters."

“我们不需要钱了，”藤原看着成为他全世界的两个女人，说道，“我们也不需要名声。这是第一次，我们拥有了一切我们曾经掠夺来的东西，而我们要把它花在唯一真正重要的事情上。”

"And what’s that?" Miyetsuko asked, pulling her coat tight, a genuine, unburdened smile breaking through her usual guarded expression.

“那是什么？”美悦子紧了紧大衣，一向防备的脸上终于露出了发自内心、毫无负担的微笑。

"Time," Fujiwara replied, taking their hands. "And the freedom to decide who we are when the cameras aren't looking."

“时间，”藤原答道，握住她们的手，“还有在摄像机熄灭时，决定我们到底是谁的自由。”

They turned their backs on the empty, pathetic little store. As they walked out into the bright, indifferent afternoon, the "Fresh Mart" retreated into silence, a forgotten harbor that had served its purpose. They were leaving the shadows behind, heading toward a horizon that belonged to no one but themselves.

他们背对着那家空荡而落魄的小店。当他们步入明亮且冷漠的午后，身后，“新鲜马特”退回到寂静之中，成了一座完成了使命、终将被遗忘的港湾。他们抛下了那些阴影，正向着那片只属于他们三个人的地平线走去。

[Learning Point]

Stagnant: 停滞的/萧条的。

Lilting: 轻快的/抑扬顿挫的。

Conspiracies: 阴谋。

Definitive: 决定性的。

Unburdened: 无负担的。
