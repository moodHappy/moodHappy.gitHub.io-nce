Chapter 37: The Harvest Moon

发布时间：2026/6/20 11:38:38

第三十七章：月下麦浪

Sayuri’s roots were deeper than the concrete of the city. Back home, her parents owned ten thousand acres of golden, undulating wheat, a sea of grain that stretched to the horizon. When the call came to bring in the harvest, Sayuri didn't hesitate. She packed up the "Toilet Saint," hitched a ride, and returned to the land that had raised her.

小百合的根扎得比城市的钢筋水泥深得多。家里那一万亩良田，金浪翻滚，一直延伸到天际的尽头。接到家里催收庄稼的电话，小百合没有犹豫。她抱起“马桶圣子”，一路颠簸回到故乡。

When her parents saw her walking up the path, holding a child with an unsettling, prehistoric gaze, they were overjoyed. They didn't care about the child’s origin; they were farmers, and they knew the value of life. "Sayuri!" her mother cried, embracing her. "You’ve finally come back! But tell us—when did you get married? Who’s the father?"

当父母看到她走上田埂，怀里还抱着个眼神深沉得可怕的孩子时，喜出望外。他们是地道的农民，懂得生命的珍贵，根本不在乎孩子的来历。“小百合！”母亲含泪拥抱她，“你总算回来了！可快告诉妈，你什么时候结的婚？孩子他爸是谁？”

Sayuri just smiled, a mysterious, serene look on her face. "It’s a long story, Mother. For now, let’s get the harvest in."

小百合只是笑了笑，神情宁静而神秘。“妈，说来话长。先把庄稼收了要紧。”

The news of Sayuri’s return spread through the village like wildfire. Kenji, who had pined for Sayuri since they were children, heard the rumors: she was back, and she was a mother. He didn't care. He showed up at the farmhouse gate, hat in hand, his heart hammering against his ribs.

小百合回村的消息像野火一样蔓延。健二，那个从小就把小百合视作女神的男人，听到她带了个孩子回来的消息，心里反倒没那么在乎了。他戴着草帽，手里攥着汗，敲开了她家的篱笆门。

He found her in the barn. "Sayuri," he said, his voice raw. "I know about the boy. I don't care. I’ve loved you since we were kids. If no one else will have you, I’ll take you in. I’ll be a father to him. I’ll work this land for you."

他在谷仓里找到了她。“小百合，”他声音沙哑，“那孩子的事我知道了。我不介意。从小到大我心里只有你。要是没人要你，我接盘。我把他当亲儿子养，这地，我替你种。”

Sayuri looked at him, touched by his stubborn, decade-long devotion. "Kenji, I didn't come back to settle down. I came back to help my parents with the wheat. But I appreciate your heart."

小百合看着他，被这份执拗了十几年的深情打动了。“健二，我回来不是为了定居，是为了帮我爸妈收麦子。但你的心意我领了。”

Kenji didn't say another word. He just grabbed a scythe. He spent the next three days working until his hands were blistered and his back ached, keeping pace with Sayuri, proving his worth in the dirt and the heat.

健二没再多说什么，转身抄起镰刀。接下来的三天里，他没日没夜地干活，磨出了满手的水泡，腰也直不起来，却始终紧跟在小百合身后，在烈日和尘土中默默证明着自己。

On the final night, under a moon as bright and round as a freshly minted coin, the fields were silent. The harvest was complete, the grain safely in the silo. Sayuri left the "Toilet Saint" in the care of her parents, who were too tired to ask questions, and walked out into the cool, moonlit wheat stubble.

最后一天晚上，月亮如同一枚刚铸好的银币，圆而明亮，原野一片寂静。庄稼收完了，粮仓也填满了。小百合把“马桶圣子”留给累得睡着的父母，独自走到月色如水的麦茬地里。

Kenji was waiting by the tractor. Sayuri walked up to him, the moonlight catching the softness of her eyes. She felt the weight of his years of longing, and in that moment, she wanted to grant him peace.

健二正守在拖拉机旁。小百合向他走去，月光映照出她眼底的温柔。她感受到了他多年苦候的重量，那一刻，她只想让他得偿所愿。

Without a word, she led him into the shadows of the grain stacks. She offered herself to him, a simple, honest gift born of gratitude and the smell of fresh earth. It was a night of release, a final, beautiful surrender to the boy who had never stopped loving her. When the sun began to bleed over the horizon, Kenji was gone, his heart finally quiet, and Sayuri returned to the farmhouse, ready to face the city again.

她没说一句话，带着他钻进了堆积的麦垛阴影中。她将自己毫无保留地献给了他，那是一份源于感激与泥土芬芳的纯粹馈赠。那是一个释放之夜，是对那个从未停止爱她的男孩的一场最终的、唯美的告别。当黎明的第一缕曙光洒在地平线上时，健二悄然离开，心愿已了；而小百合则转身回屋，准备面对那个混乱的都市生活。

[Learning Point]

Undulating: 起伏的（形容麦浪）。

Pined for: 深切渴望/苦候。

Scythe: 镰刀。

Silo: 粮仓。

Wheat stubble: 麦茬。
