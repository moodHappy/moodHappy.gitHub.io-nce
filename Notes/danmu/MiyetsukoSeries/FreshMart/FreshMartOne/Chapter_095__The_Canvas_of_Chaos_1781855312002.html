Chapter 95: The Canvas of Chaos

发布时间：2026/6/19 15:48:30

第九十五章：无光的画卷

Following her "documentary" epiphany, the household had shifted into a bizarre state of creative flux. Rin, inspired by the acclaim showering upon Sayuri, decided she needed an artistic outlet of her own. She chose painting.

在小百合“纪录片”爆火之后，家里陷入了一种奇异的创作狂热。铃，被小百合那如潮水般涌来的赞誉所激励，决定寻找自己的艺术出口。她选择了绘画。

She set up an easel in the center of the living room, the canvas standing like a blank, mocking judge. Sayuri, ever the patient instructor, sat beside her, acting as her eyes.

她在客厅中央支起画架，那块画布立在那里，像是一位空白而嘲弄人的法官。小百合作为那个耐心的导师，坐在她身边，充当她的眼睛。

"It’s a sunset, Rin," Sayuri explained, her voice soft. "Imagine the sky as a gradient. Start with a deep, burning orange at the top, fading into a soft, bruised purple toward the horizon. Use the crimson—it’s that little tube on the left."

“那是夕阳，铃，”小百合轻声解释，“想象天空是一个渐变色。顶部用浓烈燃烧的橙色，向地平线过渡到柔和的、带点淤青的紫色。用那个深红色——就是左边那个小管子。”

Rin reached out, her fingers fumbling with the tubes of paint. She squeezed them onto the palette with a reckless, enthusiastic force. She couldn't see the world; she had never seen a sunset, a tree, or even a face. To her, the world was a map of textures, vibrations, and heat.

铃伸出手，指尖在颜料管间摸索。她粗鲁而充满热情地将颜料挤在调色盘上。她看不见这个世界；她从未见过夕阳、树木，甚至连一张脸都没见过。对她而言，世界是一张由触感、振动和温度构成的地图。

She attacked the canvas. There was no brushwork, no gentle application of color. She used her palms, smearing the colors in wild, frantic arcs, driven by a rhythm she felt in her own pulse.

她开始向画布发起进攻。没有笔触，没有温柔的涂抹。她用手掌，在画布上疯狂地涂抹出狂乱、急促的弧线，全凭她脉搏中感受到的节奏驱动。

"Rin, that’s... that’s a lot of black," Sayuri noted, trying to keep her voice encouraging. "And the orange is mixing with the green. It’s creating a... a very murky brown."

“铃，那个……黑色用得太多了，”小百合试图让语气保持鼓励，“而且橙色和绿色混在了一起。看起来有点……像是一团浑浊的棕色。”

Rin didn't stop. She breathed heavily, her face splattered with blobs of paint, her movements becoming more violent. She was trying to paint "heat," "desire," and the chaotic "salty" laughter she heard every night at the dinner table.

铃没有停下。她粗重地喘着气，脸上溅满了颜料点，动作愈发狂暴。她试图画出“热度”、“欲望”，以及她每晚在餐桌上听到的那种混沌而“咸味”的笑声。

After an hour, she stepped back, panting.

一小时后，她后退一步，气喘吁吁。

"How is it?" Rin asked, her sightless eyes bright with hope. "Does it look like a sunset?"

“怎么样？”铃问道，失明的双眼里闪烁着希望的光。“看起来像夕阳吗？”

Sayuri looked at the canvas. It was a disaster. It looked like a swampy, visceral mess—a muddy, pulsating glob of color that defied any geometric sense. It was, quite literally, a puddle of mud captured in oil.

小百合看向画布。那简直是一场灾难。看起来像是一片泥泞、血肉模糊的乱码——那团泥泞的、跳动的颜色块，完全没有任何几何美感。从字面上讲，那简直就是一摊被困在油彩里的烂泥。

"It’s... it’s very abstract, Rin," Sayuri lied, struggling to find a compliment. "It captures the... the density of your feelings."

“它……它非常抽象，铃，”小百合撒了个谎，绞尽脑汁寻找着赞美之词，“它捕捉到了……你内心那种丰盈的密度。”

Fujiwara, who had just walked in, leaned over to look. He let out a loud, unvarnished laugh. "Abstract? It looks like you ran over a palette with a truck, Rin. It’s a literal mudslide."

刚走进来的藤原凑过来看了一眼，发出一阵毫不留情的狂笑。“抽象？看起来像是你开着卡车从调色盘上压过去了，铃。这分明是一场泥石流。”

Rin, unbothered by his critique, wiped a smudge of blue paint from her nose and grinned. "Mud is just earth that’s been 'woken up' by water, Fujiwara-san. Just like you woke me up in the kitchen. Maybe it’s not a sunset. Maybe it’s just... the truth."

铃对他的批评毫不在意，抹掉鼻尖上的一抹蓝色颜料，露出了灿烂的笑容。“泥巴不过是被水‘唤醒’的泥土罢了，藤原先生。就像你在厨房唤醒我一样。或许这根本不是夕阳。或许，这就是……真实。”

The room fell silent as they looked at the glob of brown paint. It was ugly, messy, and fundamentally confusing—just like their family.

房间安静下来，他们注视着那团棕色的颜料块。它丑陋、混乱，且根本让人摸不着头脑——就像他们这个家一样。

[Learning Point]

Easel: 画架。

Gradient: 渐变。

Murky: 浑浊的。

Visceral: 发自肺腑的（此处指粗糙而直接的触感）。

Unvarnished: 不加修饰的/直率的。
