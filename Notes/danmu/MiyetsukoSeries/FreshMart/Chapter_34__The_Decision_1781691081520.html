Chapter 34: The Decision

发布时间：2026/6/17 18:11:20

第三十四章：命运的博弈

The silence that followed their argument was heavy, saturated with the hum of the studio equipment. The blue light from the monitors played across their faces, carving deep, hollow shadows into their expressions. Fujiwara didn't back down, and Miyetsuko wouldn't yield. The deadlock was absolute, a collision of two unmoving forces.

争吵后的寂静沉重得令人窒息，直播设备的嗡嗡声在空气中游走。显示器的蓝光映照在他们脸上，勾勒出深陷而空洞的阴影。藤原不肯退让，美悦子寸步不让。这僵局如死锁般凝固，是两股不可撼动力量的剧烈碰撞。

"If we can't agree," Fujiwara finally said, his voice dropping into a cold, clinical calm, "we let fate decide. Simple. A game of rock-paper-scissors. One round. No repeats, no regrets."

“如果我们达不成共识，”藤原终于开口，声音降至一种冷酷的理智，“那就让命运来定。很简单。猜拳。一把定胜负。不重来，不后悔。”

Miyetsuko hesitated, her hand trembling slightly as she reached out. She looked at Sayuri, who had finally set the box down and was watching them with wide, unfocused eyes. "Fine," Miyetsuko whispered. "But if I win, the subject is closed forever."

美悦子迟疑了，伸手时指尖轻微颤动。她看向小百合，女孩终于放下了箱子，正用那种涣散而空洞的眼神注视着他们。“好，”美悦子低语，“但如果我赢了，这个话题永远封死。”

The atmosphere felt thin, like the air in a vacuum. They stood face to face, hands clenched into fists, the tension between them snapping like a high-tension wire.

空气稀薄得仿佛真空。他们面对面站着，手心紧紧攥成拳，两人间的张力如同一根被拉到极致的钢丝，随时可能崩断。

"One... two... three..."

“一……二……三……”

Fujiwara threw "paper." Miyetsuko, caught in a split second of hesitation, threw "rock."

藤原出了“布”。美悦子在千钧一发的犹豫中，出了“石头”。

The sound of Miyetsuko’s gasp was lost in the sudden, sharp silence. The air in the room seemed to rush out, leaving only the clinical, sterile coldness. Fujiwara didn't cheer; he didn't even look triumphant. He simply nodded, a slow, grim acceptance of what the universe had ordained.

美悦子那声惊喘被吞没在骤然而至的尖锐死寂中。房间里的空气仿佛瞬间被抽干，只剩下那种无菌室般的冷冽。藤原没有欢呼，甚至没有流露出胜利者的狂喜。他只是缓缓点头，以一种近乎肃穆的姿态，接受了命运的裁决。

Miyetsuko slumped back into her chair, her face turning ashen. She looked at Sayuri, her eyes searching for a sign of protest, a cry for mercy.

美悦子瘫软回椅子上，脸色灰败如土。她看向小百合，眼神里带着一丝渴求，那是希望女孩能反抗、能哀求的最后挣扎。

Sayuri stood in the center of the room. She was trembling, her breath hitching in her chest. She was terrified—the looming prospect of being laid bare, of her deepest secrets being consumed by the faceless masses. But beneath that terror, there was a tiny, rhythmic pulse of something else: the seductive, dangerous whisper of fame. The chance to be seen, to be adored, to be the center of a world that had always ignored her.

小百合站在屋子中央。她浑身颤栗，呼吸急促。她是恐惧的——那种即将被彻底赤裸、将自己最隐秘的灵魂供大众消费的恐惧。但在这恐惧之下，某种节奏在跳动：那是“名利”二字诱人而危险的低语。那种被注视、被狂热崇拜、成为世界焦点的诱惑，对一直生活在阴影中的她来说，有着致命的毒性。

"Sayuri?" Miyetsuko asked, her voice cracking. "Tell him no."

“小百合？”美悦子嗓音沙哑地问道，“告诉他，不行。”

Sayuri looked at her sister-in-arms, then at Fujiwara. She slowly wiped her palms on her apron, her eyes darkening with a newfound, terrifying resolution. "I..." she began, her voice gaining strength, "I want to see what happens. I want to see... if I can be something more."

小百合看了看自己的战友，又看向藤原。她缓缓擦干手心的冷汗，眼神中凝聚起一股全新的、令人战栗的决绝。“我……”她开口，声音逐渐变得坚定，“我想看看会发生什么。我想看看……我是不是能成为不一样的人。”

Fujiwara’s smile was the first thing to break the stillness—small, sharp, and terrifyingly satisfied.

藤原的嘴角微微勾起，打破了这凝固的空气——那笑容细微、尖锐，且透着一种令人毛骨悚然的满足。
