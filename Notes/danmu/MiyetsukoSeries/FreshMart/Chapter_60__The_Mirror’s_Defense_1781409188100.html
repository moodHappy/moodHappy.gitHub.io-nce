Chapter 60: The Mirror’s Defense

发布时间：2026/6/14 11:53:06

第六十章：洗手间的“反击”

The air in the office grew thin. The woman, Eri, approached with an effortless, porcelain-doll grace, holding out a delicate box of macarons. Her smile was sweet, but there was an unmistakable sharpness in her eyes—a calculated observation that made Miyetsuko feel like a specimen under a microscope.

办公室里的空气变得稀薄。那个叫绘里的女人走上前来，步履间带着瓷娃娃般的优雅，递出一盒精致的马卡龙。她的笑容很甜，但眼神中却藏着某种锐利——那是一种经过精心计算的审视，让美悦子感到自己像是一个被放在显微镜下的样本。

"I'm Eri," she repeated, tilting her head. "Fujiwara mentions his office dynamics quite often. He says you’re his... most challenging project, isn't that right?"

“我是绘里，”她重复道，微微歪着头。“藤原经常提到他的办公室人际。他说你是他……最‘具有挑战性’的项目，对吧？”

Miyetsuko looked at her. Really looked at her. Eri was polished, yes, but in a way that felt practiced—a carefully curated aesthetic that lacked the raw, dangerous edge that Miyetsuko had spent weeks battling. There was a dullness there, a predictability that Miyetsuko found almost insulting. This was the competition?

美悦子看着她，真正地打量着她。绘里确实很考究，但那种考究显得过于刻意——那是一套精心包装的审美，却缺乏美悦子这几周来一直在对抗的那种原始而危险的野性。她觉得绘里身上有一种沉闷感，一种令人失望的预见性。这就是对手？

A wave of bitter, unspoken resentment surged in Miyetsuko’s chest. It wasn't just jealousy; it was the sheer annoyance of being compared to—and losing to—something so beige.

一股苦涩的、难以言表的怨念在美悦子心中翻涌。这不仅仅是嫉妒，更有一种被拿去和如此平庸的人比较、甚至还要被迫退让的烦躁。

"I'm sure he has his own unique way of describing things," Miyetsuko said, her voice cool and steady, ignoring the offered macarons. She didn't wait for a reply. She turned on her heel and strode toward the restroom, the rhythmic clack of her heels sounding like a retreat she didn't want to admit to.

“我想他描述事物的方式一定很‘独特’，”美悦子冷冷地说道，声音平稳，直接无视了递到面前的马卡龙。她没有等对方回应，转过身大步走向洗手间。高跟鞋撞击地面的声音听起来像是撤退的鼓点，尽管她一点也不想承认那是撤退。

Once inside the sterile sanctuary of the restroom, she locked the door and leaned against the cold tiles, her breath hitching. She strode to the floor-to-ceiling mirror.

走进这间冰冷的洗手间避难所，她反锁上门，背靠着冰冷的瓷砖，呼吸急促。她走向那面落地镜。

She peeled off her blazer, then her blouse, letting them pool on the floor. In the harsh fluorescent light, her body reflected back—not the "office supervisor," not the "patient," but a woman who was taut, strong, and undeniably alive. She ran her hands over her curves, observing the way her skin glowed, the strength in her shoulders, and the soft, yet firm resilience of her form.

她脱掉西装外套，接着脱下衬衫，让衣服堆在地板上。在惨白的荧光灯下，镜中映照出的——不再是那个“办公室主管”，也不再是那个“病人”，而是一个紧致、充满力量、生机勃勃的女人。她双手抚过自己的曲线，审视着皮肤的光泽，肩膀的力量感，以及那副既柔软又富有韧性的身体。

She touched the faint, lingering redness on her inner thighs—the phantom souvenir of last night’s encounter. A small, defiant smile crept across her lips.

她指尖触碰到大腿内侧那抹淡淡的、未褪去的红痕——那是昨夜那场邂逅的隐秘纪念品。一抹挑衅的、傲慢的笑容在嘴角蔓延。

So, he likes "safe" and "polished" on his lunch break? she thought, her eyes sparkling with a new, dangerous resolve. Fine. Let him have his macaron. I’ll make sure he never forgets the taste of the real thing.

所以，他午休时喜欢这种“安全”又“优雅”的货色？ 她心中暗想，眼底闪烁着一种危险的决心。行。随他去吃那块马卡龙吧。我会让他这辈子都忘不掉那真正的滋味。

Porcelain-doll grace: 瓷娃娃般的优雅。

Specimen: 样本 / 标本。

Resilience: 韧性 / 恢复力。
