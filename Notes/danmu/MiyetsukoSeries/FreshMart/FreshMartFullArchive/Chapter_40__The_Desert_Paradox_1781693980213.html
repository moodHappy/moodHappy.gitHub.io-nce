Chapter 40: The Desert Paradox

发布时间：2026/6/17 18:59:38

第四十章：下一站：沙漠里的幻梦之都

From the eternal white of the Arctic, they craved a heat that could strip them down to their core. Fujiwara didn't choose a luxury hotel in Dubai. Instead, he dropped them into the middle of the Empty Quarter (Rub' al Khali) in Oman, the largest continuous sand desert on the planet.

从极地的永恒雪白中抽身，他们渴望一种能将一切褪去、直抵灵魂的燥热。藤原没有选择迪拜的奢华酒店，而是直接将他们空投到了阿曼的鲁卜哈利沙漠（Empty Quarter）——那是地球上最大的连续沙海。

They arrived in a vintage, open-top Mercedes G-Wagon, customized with gold-plated rims that looked utterly ridiculous against the shifting orange dunes.

他们开着一辆复古的敞篷奔驰G级越野车抵达，那镀金的轮毂在起伏的橘色沙丘中显得荒谬至极。

"It’s not enough to just walk through the sand, Miyetsuko," Fujiwara said, stepping out into the 45°C furnace, his suit perfectly pressed as if he were attending a gala. "We need to own the horizon."

“仅仅走过这片沙地是不够的，美悦子，”藤原踏入45摄氏度的炼狱中，那身西装依旧熨帖得像是要出席盛大的晚宴，“我们需要掌控这道地平线。”

He didn't just buy a camp; he commissioned an entire "mirage" to be built overnight. Using local Bedouin expertise and an absurd amount of money, they constructed a temporary, high-fashion pop-up club right in the middle of nowhere. It was a structure made of reflective mirrors and silk tents, designed to catch the sunset and scatter it like diamonds across the dunes.

他不仅是盖了个营地，而是在一夜之间制造了一个“海市蜃楼”。他雇佣了当地贝都因人的力量，挥金如土，在荒无人烟的沙漠中央建起了一个前卫的快闪俱乐部。那是一座由镜面和丝绸帐篷组成的建筑，旨在捕捉每一缕落日的光线，并将其如钻石般洒落在这片广袤的沙海之上。

Miyetsuko, draped in flowing, translucent fabrics, spent her time acting as the "Queen of the Sands." She sat on a throne carved from a single block of imported Italian marble, hauled across the desert by a caravan of camels. She spent her days sipping iced vintage champagne while local guides, bewildered and handsomely paid, performed traditional dances that she turned into background fodder for her next "aesthetic" post.

美悦子身披飘逸的半透明长袍，扮演着这片沙海的“女王”。她坐在从意大利进口的一整块大理石雕琢而成的王座上，那王座是由一支骆驼商队历经艰辛运入沙漠中心的。她整日在那儿啜饮着冰镇古董香槟，而那些拿着巨额报酬、一脸茫然的当地向导们，则被迫为她跳着传统的民族舞蹈，只为给她做下一条“艺术美学”帖子的背景素材。

But the true spectacle was Sayuri.

但真正的奇观是小百合。

Fujiwara had decided that the desert needed a "deity." He arranged for a massive, silk-draped platform to be set up on the highest dune. At night, while the desert temperature plummeted, he had specialized lighting rigs flown in to illuminate her against the star-drenched sky. She stood there, a vision of pale, dark-haired perfection, wearing nothing but sheer layers and the desert wind.

藤原认为这片沙漠需要一位“神祇”。他在最高的沙丘上架起了一座巨大的丝绸平台。夜晚，当沙漠温度骤降，他动用货机空运来专业的灯光设备，在璀璨星空下为她打光。她站在那里，像是一个肤色苍白、长发漆黑的完美幻影，身上只披着轻薄的丝绸，任由大漠的风穿透她的衣物。

He wasn't filming a movie; he was creating a hallucination for the nomadic tribes miles away. They began to tell stories of a "Ghost of the Sands," a beautiful spirit who appeared only when the moon was at its peak.

他不是在拍戏，而是在制造一场给方圆百里外游牧部落看的“集体幻觉”。当地人开始流传起“沙之幽灵”的传说——一个只有在月亮升到中天时才会现身的绝美精灵。

"Do you hear them?" Fujiwara asked one night, pulling Sayuri off the platform as the sand began to swirl around them in a gale. "They’re whispering about you in the villages. They think you're a miracle."

“你听见了吗？”一晚，狂风卷起沙砾，藤原将小百合从平台上拉下来，“他们在村子里低声议论你。他们觉得你是一个神迹。”

Sayuri, her skin coated in a fine layer of golden sand, leaned into him, her gaze reflecting the vast, uncaring indifference of the desert. "Miracle or monster, Fujiwara? Does it matter, as long as they never forget me?"

小百合浑身裹着一层细细的金沙，靠向他，眼中映照出沙漠那广袤而冷漠的虚无。“神迹还是怪物，藤原？只要他们永远忘不了我，这重要吗？”

Miyetsuko watched them from her marble throne, a glass of gold-flecked liqueur in her hand, laughing as the desert wind whipped her hair into a frenzy. She was the one who had survived the illness; she was the one who had learned that life was just a series of performances before the final curtain.

美悦子坐在大理石王座上看着他们，手中端着一杯金箔酒液，任由沙漠狂风将她的头发吹得凌乱，肆意大笑。她是那个从癌症中活下来的人；她早已看透，生命不过是谢幕前的一场又一场表演。

The club was abandoned the next morning, buried by the shifting sands, as if it had never existed. They drove off in the gold-rimmed G-Wagon, leaving behind nothing but a legend and a very confused group of local guides who were now richer than their entire lineage combined.

第二天清晨，那个俱乐部被风沙掩埋，仿佛从未存在过。他们开着那辆镀金轮毂的G级越野车绝尘而去，只留下了一个传说，以及一群虽然极度困惑，但如今已富可敌国的当地向导。

[Learning Point]

Continuous: 连续的/不间断的。

Hallucination: 幻觉。

Nomadic: 游牧的。

Frenzy: 狂暴/混乱。

Lineage: 血统/家族。
