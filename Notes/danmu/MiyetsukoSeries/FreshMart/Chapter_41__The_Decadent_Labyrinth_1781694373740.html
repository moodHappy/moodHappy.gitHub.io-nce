Chapter 41: The Decadent Labyrinth

发布时间：2026/6/17 19:06:12

第四十一章：下一站：威尼斯，淹没的奢靡

They left the arid silence of the desert for the drowning elegance of Venice, Italy. But they didn't come for the gondolas or the art museums. Fujiwara arrived with a singular, chaotic ambition: to treat the sinking city as his own private, aquatic parlor.

他们抛下了沙漠那干燥的死寂，奔向了正在沉没的优雅之都——意大利威尼斯。但他们不是为了贡多拉，也不是为了艺术博物馆而来。藤原带着一种单一而疯狂的野心：把这座正在下沉的城市，当成他私人的水上客厅。

He didn't just rent a palazzo; he bought an entire 15th-century residence on the Grand Canal, gutted the furniture, and replaced it with a custom-built, floor-to-ceiling aquarium that spanned the entire main floor.

他不仅是租了一座宫殿，而是直接买下了大运河边的一座15世纪府邸，拆空了所有的古董家具，取而代之的是一个定制的、贯穿整个主楼层的巨型落地水族馆。

"I want to live in the throat of the city," Fujiwara announced as the construction crew scrambled to install the reinforced glass.

“我要住在这座城市的咽喉里，”当施工队争分夺秒地安装强化玻璃时，藤原宣布道。

Miyetsuko, ever the queen of the spectacle, found the decadence amusing. She spent her days lounging on a velvet chaise longue inside the glass-walled room, watching the murky, green water of the canal press against the walls, while colorful, exotic fish—imported from the most remote corners of the globe—swam around her.

美悦子作为这种浮华盛宴的女王，觉得这种堕落无比有趣。她整日躺在玻璃室内的天鹅绒躺椅上，看着运河那浑浊的绿水挤压着墙壁，而那些从全球最偏远角落空运来的奇异鱼类，则围绕着她自在游弋。

"We’re literally living in a fishbowl, Fujiwara," she laughed, sipping a cocktail as a tourist boat drifted past, its passengers pressing their faces against the glass, unaware that the "attraction" inside was staring back at them. "I feel like a mermaid in a museum."

“藤原，我们简直是活在鱼缸里，”她大笑着啜饮鸡尾酒。一艘游客船正好漂过，游客们把脸贴在玻璃上向内张望，却不知道里头的人正冷冷地打量着他们。“我觉得自己像个被放在博物馆里展览的美人鱼。”

But the true insanity happened at night.

但真正的疯狂，是在夜里。

Fujiwara, obsessed with the idea that Sayuri needed to be "purified by the tides," transformed their private dock into a stage. He had local divers drag up ancient, discarded statues from the city's murky lagoon bottom. He then arranged them in the water surrounding the palazzo to create an underwater gallery.

藤原执着于“小百合需要被潮汐净化”的念头，把私人码头改造成了一个舞台。他雇佣当地潜水员，从威尼斯浑浊的泻湖底捞出那些被遗弃的古代雕塑，布置在府邸四周的水中，打造出了一座水下画廊。

Every night, he had Sayuri perform a "sinking ritual." Dressed in heavy, weighted silk robes that would drag her beneath the surface, she would walk out onto the submerged steps of the palazzo as the moon hit the water. She wasn't drowning—Fujiwara had underwater oxygen lines hidden in the architecture—but to the passing boats, it looked as if the "National Icon" was sacrificing herself to the lagoon.

每晚，他都让小百合进行一场“沉没仪式”。她穿着会被重量拖入水下的沉重真丝长袍，趁着月光洒在水面时，步入府邸没入水中的台阶。她并没有淹死——藤原在建筑结构里隐藏了水下供氧管道——但在经过的船只看来，这位“国民偶像”仿佛正在向泻湖献祭自己。

"They cry, you know," Sayuri whispered one night, emerging from the dark water, her skin glistening, her dark hair slicked back. She climbed back onto the marble pier, her wet clothes clinging to her body, highlighting every curve. "The people on the boats. They hold their breath until I disappear. They think I'm a tragic beauty."

“他们都在哭，”一晚，她从黑暗的水中浮出，肌肤闪烁着光泽，湿漉漉的深色长发向后梳去，低声呢喃。她爬回大理石码头，湿透的衣物紧贴着躯体，勾勒出每一道曲线。“那些船上的人。他们屏住呼吸直到我消失。他们以为我是一个悲剧美人。”

"Let them believe it," Fujiwara replied, wiping her face with a warm, silk towel. "Tragedy sells better than fame. By tomorrow, your 'suicide' footage will be the most searched video on the planet."

“让他们信吧，”藤原回答，用温热的丝绸毛巾为她擦拭脸庞，“悲剧比名声更卖座。到明天，你这段‘投水自尽’的视频，就会成为地球上搜索量最高的内容。”

Miyetsuko watched from her throne inside the aquarium, surrounded by glowing tropical fish, feeling the house tremble under the rising tide. They were in the most beautiful, dying city on Earth, turning its decay into their own personal, opulent performance art.

美悦子在水族馆内的王座上注视着他们，被游动的热带鱼群包围，感受着这座房子在潮汐中微微颤抖。他们置身于地球上最美也最易逝的城市，将它的腐朽变成了专属于他们的、奢靡的表演艺术。

[Learning Point]

Decadent: 颓废的/堕落的。

Labyrinth: 迷宫。

Palazzo: 宫殿/府邸。

Opulent: 豪华的/富裕的。

Submerged: 没入水中的。
