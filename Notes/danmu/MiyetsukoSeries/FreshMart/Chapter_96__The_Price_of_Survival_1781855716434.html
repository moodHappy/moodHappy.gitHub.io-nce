Chapter 96: The Price of Survival

发布时间：2026/6/19 15:55:14

第九十六章：赔本赚吆喝的“战争”

The peace of the "Fresh Mart" was shattered on a Tuesday. Across the street, a neon-bright, sterile, and aggressively efficient "QuickPick" opened its doors. It wasn't just a store; it was a military-grade retail machine.

周二，“新鲜马特”的安宁被打破了。马路对面，一家霓虹闪烁、冰冷而高效的“快取便利店”开业了。那不仅是一家店，简直是一台军事化的零售机器。

Miyetsuko stood by the window, her eyes narrowing as she watched the sea of customers swarm the QuickPick entrance. They were giving away free energy drinks and had a promotion that defied logic: "Buy one condom, get a lifetime supply of instant noodles!"

美悦子站在窗边，双眼微眯，注视着那群蜂拥而入“快取”的顾客。他们居然在派发免费能量饮料，还搞出了一个违背逻辑的促销：“买一个避孕套，送一辈子吃不完的泡面！”

"They're playing dirty," Miyetsuko hissed. "They're targeting our niche. They know our customer base is comprised of pregnant influencers, blind painters, and men who flush bricks. Fujiwara, mobilize the defensive pricing!"

“他们玩阴的，”美悦子嘶声道。“他们在针对我们的客户群。他们知道我们的顾客组成是：怀孕的网红、画泥巴的盲人，还有那个每天早上冲砖头的男人。藤原，立刻启动防御性定价！”

Fujiwara leaped into action, pulling out a whiteboard. "We’ll slash our prices! We’ll sell milk at the cost of water! We’ll offer 'buy-one-get-everything-else-free'!"

藤原立刻行动起来，拖出一块白板。“我们要大甩卖！牛奶卖出矿泉水的价格！我们要搞‘买一送所有’！”

[The Promotion War / 毁灭性的促销]

By noon, the "Fresh Mart" was a disaster zone. To compete, they lowered the price of eggs to one yen. Then, in a panic, they decided to pay people two yen to take the eggs away.

到了中午，“新鲜马特”已经成了重灾区。为了竞争，他们把鸡蛋降价到一元。然后，在恐慌之下，他们干脆决定每送出一盒鸡蛋，就反向补贴顾客两元。

The line stretched for three blocks. The problem was, the customers were entirely comprised of local freeloaders and the QuickPick employees themselves, who were coming over, taking the "subsidized eggs," and then heading back to QuickPick to buy their actual groceries.

排队的队伍绵延了三个街区。问题是，顾客全都是当地的占便宜专业户，甚至是“快取”的员工——他们跑过来领走“补贴鸡蛋”，然后转身回“快取”去买真正的生活用品。

Rin was at the register, trying to process the "transaction." "Fujiwara-san, the balance sheet is screaming. We just sold our entire stock of imported brie for three yen, and I’m pretty sure the guy who bought it was just a raccoon in a trench coat."

铃坐在收银台前，试图处理这些“交易”。“藤原先生，资产负债表在尖叫。我们刚把所有进口布里奶酪以三元的价格卖掉了，而且我敢肯定，刚才买奶酪的那个人其实是披着风衣的浣熊。”

Miyetsuko was in the aisle, screaming at a customer who was trying to fill a grocery cart with free samples of shampoo. "It's a sample, not a swimming pool! Put the soap back, you scavenger!"

美悦子在货架区咆哮，对着一个试图把免费洗发水样品装满购物车的顾客大吼：“那是样品，不是游泳池！把肥皂放回去，你这个拾荒者！”

By 4:00 PM, they were bankrupt. Or at least, they were deep in the red.

下午4点，他们破产了。或者至少，已经亏得底裤都不剩了。

The QuickPick store across the street had a massive sign: “We appreciate the Fresh Mart’s donation to the neighborhood. Everything is 50% off for the next hour!”

对面“快取”便利店挂出了一块巨大的招牌：“感谢‘新鲜马特’对社区的捐助。接下来一小时，全场五折！”

[The Aftermath / 彻夜长谈]

They sat on the floor of the empty back office, surrounded by stale bread and a pile of unsold, one-yen eggs.

他们瘫坐在空荡荡的后仓地板上，四周堆满了陈面包和一堆卖不出去的一元鸡蛋。

"Well," Sayuri said, patting her belly while she ate a bowl of lukewarm instant ramen—stolen, ironically, from their own shelf. "On the bright side, at least we don't have to worry about inventory management anymore. Because we have no inventory."

“嗯，”小百合一边拍着肚皮，一边吃着一碗微温的泡面——讽刺的是，这还是从他们自己货架上拿的。“从好的一面看，我们不用担心库存管理了。因为我们已经没货了。”

Miyetsuko lit a cigarette, her face smeared with mascara from the stress of the day. "I fought the QuickPick and the QuickPick won."

美悦子点燃一支烟，因为压力过大，睫毛膏晕成了熊猫眼。“我挑战了‘快取’，然后‘快取’把我的底裤都赢走了。”

Fujiwara leaned his head back against the wall, laughing until he wheezed. "We tried to fight a corporation with a blind artist, a pregnant ex-idol, and a woman who causes toilet blockages. It was never going to end any other way."

藤原把头靠在墙上，笑得直喘气。“我们居然想用一个盲人画家、一个怀孕的前偶像，还有一个会堵塞马桶的女人去对抗一家跨国企业。这结局早就注定好了。”

"So, what do we do tomorrow?" Rin asked, dipping a finger into a puddle of spilled egg white.

“那我们明天干什么？”铃问道，手指伸进一滩洒出来的蛋清里蘸了蘸。

"Tomorrow?" Fujiwara grinned. "Tomorrow, we apply for jobs at QuickPick. I heard their manager needs a new assistant."

“明天？”藤原咧嘴一笑。“明天？明天我们就去‘快取’应聘。我听说他们经理正缺个新助理。”

The room echoed with their chaotic, defeatist laughter. They had lost the store, but they were still, predictably, the most dysfunctional family on the block.

房间里回荡着他们那充满失败感的混沌笑声。店没了，但他们依然是这条街上最无可救药、最荒诞不羁的一家子。

[Learning Point]

Military-grade: 军事级别的（形容高效）。

Subsidized: 补贴的。

Freeloaders: 占便宜的人。

In the red: 亏损状态。

Defeatist: 失败主义的。
