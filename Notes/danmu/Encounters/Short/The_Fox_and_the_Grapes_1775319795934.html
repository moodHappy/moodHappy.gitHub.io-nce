The Fox and the Grapes

狐狸与葡萄

One hot summer day, a fox was wandering through a vineyard, feeling thirsty and tired.

在一个炎热的夏日，一只狐狸在葡萄园里徘徊，感到又渴又累。

As he walked along, he suddenly spotted a bunch of juicy grapes hanging high on a vine.

当他走着走着，突然看见一串多汁的葡萄高高地挂在藤上。

The grapes looked ripe and delicious, and the fox’s mouth began to water.

这些葡萄看起来又熟又美味，狐狸不禁流起了口水。

“I must have those grapes,” he said to himself.

“我一定要吃到那些葡萄。”他对自己说道。

So the fox stepped back, took a run, and jumped as high as he could, but he missed them.

于是狐狸后退几步，助跑后用力一跳，但没有够到。

He tried again and again, leaping higher each time, but the grapes were just out of reach.

他一次又一次地尝试，每次都跳得更高，但葡萄始终够不着。

After many attempts, the fox grew tired and gave up.

尝试了很多次之后，狐狸变得筋疲力尽，只好放弃。

As he walked away, he said with a shrug, “Those grapes are probably sour anyway.”

当他离开时，他耸了耸肩说道：“那些葡萄肯定是酸的。”

And so the fox left, pretending he did not care.

于是狐狸离开了，假装自己并不在意。

---

Moral: It is easy to despise what you cannot have.

寓意：得不到的东西，人们往往会说它不好。
