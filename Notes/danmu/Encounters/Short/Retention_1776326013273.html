Retention

留存

The number had been stuck for weeks.

这个数字已经卡了好几个星期。

“User retention is dropping again,” Mia said, staring at her laptop.

“用户留存又在下降，”米娅盯着电脑说道。

She didn’t sound surprised—just tired.

她的语气里没有惊讶，只有疲惫。

“Did we try the new onboarding flow?” her manager asked.

“我们不是试了新的新手引导吗？”她的经理问。

“Yeah. People sign up… and then they leave.”

“试了。用户注册了……然后就走了。”

“So we’re good at attracting people,” Mia said, “just not keeping them.”

“看来我们很会吸引人，”米娅说，“就是留不住。”

That word lingered in her mind—retention.

那个词在她脑子里挥之不去——留存。

Later that night, Mia sat alone in her apartment, scrolling through old photos.

那天晚上，米娅独自坐在公寓里，翻看旧照片。

Trips, birthdays, random dinners. Moments she thought she would never forget.

旅行、生日、随便的一顿晚餐。那些她曾以为永远不会忘记的时刻。

But she struggled to recall the details.

可她已经记不起细节了。

“My memory retention is getting worse,” she murmured.

“我的记忆留存越来越差了，”她喃喃道。

Her phone buzzed. A message from Alex.

手机震动了一下，是Alex发来的消息。

“Hey… you free this weekend?”

“嘿……这周末有空吗？”

Mia stared at the screen for a long time.

米娅盯着屏幕看了很久。

They hadn’t talked properly in months.

他们已经好几个月没好好说过话了。

She typed, deleted, and typed again.

她打字，又删掉，再打。

“I’ve just been busy,” she finally sent.

“我最近有点忙，”她最终发了出去。

The truth was, she didn’t know how to keep people anymore.

其实，她已经不知道该怎么“留住”一个人了。

The next day at work, Mia presented her ideas.

第二天上班，米娅汇报了她的想法。

“We’re focusing too much on features,” she said.

“我们太关注功能了，”她说。

“Retention isn’t just about what the app does.”

“留存不只是产品功能的问题。”

Her manager leaned forward. “Then what is it about?”

她的经理前倾身体：“那到底是什么？”

Mia hesitated.

米娅迟疑了一下。

“It’s about how people feel when they leave… and whether they want to come back.”

“是用户离开时的感觉……以及他们是否还想回来。”

The room went quiet.

会议室安静了下来。

That evening, she opened Alex’s chat again.

那天傍晚，她又打开了和Alex的聊天框。

This time, she didn’t overthink it.

这一次，她没有再犹豫。

“I wasn’t just busy,” she typed.

“我不只是忙，”她打字道。

“I just didn’t know how to stay.”

“我只是……不知道该怎么留下来。”

A few seconds later, Alex replied.

几秒后，Alex回复了。

“You don’t have to stay perfectly. Just don’t disappear.”

“你不需要完美地留下来。只要别消失就好。”

Mia read the message twice.

米娅把这句话看了两遍。

Maybe retention wasn’t about holding on tightly.

也许，“留存”并不是死死抓住。

Maybe it was about giving something a reason to remain.

也许，是给某些东西一个留下来的理由。

The next morning, the number on her dashboard hadn’t changed yet.

第二天早上，后台的数据还没有变化。

But for the first time, she didn’t feel stuck.

但她第一次不再觉得卡住了。

✅ 精读表达（值得你积累）

1. stuck for weeks

EN: not changing or improving for a long time

中：持续卡住，没有进展

例句：The project has been stuck for weeks.

项目已经卡了好几个星期。

2. linger in someone’s mind

EN: stay in your thoughts for a long time

中：在脑海中挥之不去

例句：That question lingered in my mind all day.

那个问题在我脑子里待了一整天。

3. struggle to recall

EN: have difficulty remembering something

中：很难回忆起

例句：I struggle to recall his name.

我很难想起他的名字。

4. overthink it

EN: think about something too much

中：想太多

例句：You’re overthinking it—it’s not that complicated.

你想太多了，没那么复杂。

5. don’t disappear

EN: don’t suddenly cut off contact

中：不要突然消失 / 断联

例句：If things get hard, just don’t disappear.

如果事情变难了，也别直接消失。
