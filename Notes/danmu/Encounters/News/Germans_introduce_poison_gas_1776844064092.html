Germans introduce poison gas

🔗 阅读原文

A Short Story Adaptation (English Original Fiction Based on Historical Facts)

短篇故事改编（基于历史事实的英文原创小说）

The afternoon sky over Ypres hung heavy and pale, as if the clouds themselves sensed something unnatural drifting toward the trenches.

伊普尔上空午后的天空显得沉重而苍白，仿佛云层本身也感觉到了某种不寻常的东西正向战壕飘去。

Private Marcel Fournier of the French colonial forces wiped the mud from his hands and leaned against the sandbags, waiting for the next German bombardment.

法国殖民地部队的二等兵马塞尔·富尼耶擦去手上的泥土，靠在沙袋上，等待着德军的下一轮炮击。

The guns had fallen strangely silent.

炮声出奇地安静了下来。

Silence on the Western Front was never a good sign.

在西线，寂静从来都不是好兆头。

A faint greenish mist began to rise from the German lines.

德军阵地开始升起一层淡淡的绿色薄雾。

At first Marcel thought it was smoke from artillery, but the wind carried it too smoothly—rolling low across the ground, curling like a living thing.

起初马塞尔以为那是大炮的硝烟，但风把它吹得太平稳了——贴着地面翻滚，像活物一样卷曲着。

Then the shouting began.

接着，呼喊声响了起来。

Soldiers up and down the trench line screamed for cloths, for water, for anything.

战壕上下，士兵们尖叫着要布，要水，要任何能用的东西。

The mist slipped over the parapet and poured into the trench like a tide.

薄雾漫过胸墙，像潮水一样涌入战壕。

Marcel inhaled once—just once—and felt his throat seize as if gripped by an invisible hand.

马塞尔吸了一口——就一口——感觉喉咙一阵痉挛，仿佛被一只无形的手紧紧掐住。

His eyes burned.

他的眼睛感到火辣辣的刺痛。

Men around him clawed at their necks, collapsing into the mud.

周围的人拼命抓挠着自己的脖子，瘫倒在泥水里。

Chlorine.

氯气。

The word flashed through his mind, something he had heard whispered in rumors but never believed.

这个词在他的脑海中闪过，他曾在谣言中听到过别人窃窃私语，但从未相信过。

War had always been brutal, but this—this was something else.

战争向来残酷，但这——这完全是另一回事。

Something engineered.

一种被精心制造出来的东西。

Marcel stumbled upward, half-blind, dragging a younger soldier by the collar.

马塞尔跌跌撞撞地向上爬，眼睛半瞎，手里拽着一个年轻士兵的衣领。

The boy’s face was already blistering.

那个男孩的脸上已经开始起水泡了。

Above them, the sky blurred into a sickly yellow haze.

在他们上方，天空模糊成了一片病态的黄色雾霾。

Across no-man’s-land, German troops hesitated.

在无人区对面，德军士兵犹豫了。

Even they seemed stunned by the devastation their new weapon had unleashed.

似乎连他们自己都被这种新武器所造成的巨大破坏力惊呆了。

The Allied line had cracked open, yet the Germans advanced slowly, cautiously, as if afraid of the very cloud they had released.

协约国的防线已经被撕裂，然而德军却推进得缓慢而谨慎，仿佛在害怕他们自己释放出的这团毒云。

For two days the gas returned—first against the French and Algerian divisions, then against the Canadians.

毒气连续两天袭来——首先是针对法国和阿尔及利亚师，然后是针对加拿大人。

Each time, the defenders fell back, coughing, choking, clutching their rifles with trembling hands.

每一次，守军都被迫撤退，他们咳嗽着，窒息着，用颤抖的手紧紧抓着步枪。

By May, the Allies had retreated toward the battered town of Ypres, the air still haunted by the memory of the green cloud.

到了五月，协约国军队已经撤退到饱受摧残的伊普尔镇附近，空气中依然萦绕着那团绿色毒云的阴影。

But the line did not break.

但是，防线并没有彻底崩溃。

In the months that followed, every nation rushed to create its own chemical weapons.

在随后的几个月里，每个国家都在竞相制造自己的化学武器。

Gas masks became as essential as rifles.

防毒面具变得和步枪一样必不可少。

Mustard gas arrived later—burning skin, lungs, and eyes—and the trenches filled with shells carrying invisible poisons.

芥子气后来也出现了——它会灼伤皮肤、肺部和眼睛——战壕里落满了携带隐形毒药的炮弹。

Marcel survived the attack, though his lungs never fully healed.

马塞尔在袭击中幸存了下来，尽管他的肺部从未完全康复。

Years later, he would tell his children that the worst part was not the burning or the fear of death.

多年后，他会告诉他的孩子们，最可怕的并不是烧灼感或对死亡的恐惧。

It was the knowledge that humans had crossed a line—that the air itself could be turned into a weapon.

而是意识到人类已经跨过了一条底线——空气本身也能被变成武器。

And once that line was crossed, it could never be uncrossed.

而一旦跨过了这条底线，就再也无法回头了。
