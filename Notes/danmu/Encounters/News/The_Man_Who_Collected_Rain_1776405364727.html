The Man Who Collected Rain

收集雨的人

---

The first time it rained after he moved in, Liang put a bowl on the balcony.

梁搬进来后的第一场雨，他在阳台上放了一只碗。

Not to catch water—at least, not in the usual sense.

不是为了接水——至少，不是通常意义上的那种。

He said rain sounded different depending on where it landed.

他说，雨落在不同的地方，声音是不一样的。

Metal sang.

金属会唱歌。

Leaves whispered.

树叶会低语。

Concrete… complained.

而水泥……像是在抱怨。

---

His neighbor, Mrs. Sato, watched him from behind her curtain.

他的邻居佐藤太太躲在窗帘后面看着他。

“That young man is strange,” she told her cat.

“那个年轻人有点奇怪，”她对猫说道。

The cat, uninterested, continued sleeping.

猫毫无兴趣，继续睡觉。

---

Every time it rained, Liang added something new.

每次下雨，梁都会多放一样东西。

A glass bottle.

一个玻璃瓶。

An old umbrella turned upside down.

一把倒过来的旧雨伞。

A cracked teacup he never used.

一只他从不用的裂纹茶杯。

---

At night, he would sit by the balcony door and listen.

夜晚，他会坐在阳台门边，静静地听。

Not music. Not quite.

不是音乐，但又有点像。

Something between memory and weather.

更像是介于记忆与天气之间的东西。

---

One evening, Mrs. Sato knocked on his door.

一天傍晚，佐藤太太敲响了他的门。

“I heard…” she hesitated,

“我听说……”她有些犹豫，

“…you are collecting rain?”

“……你在收集雨？”

---

Liang smiled, as if that was the most natural thing in the world.

梁笑了，好像那是再正常不过的事。

“I’m collecting moments,” he said.

“我是在收集片刻，”他说。

---

She frowned.

她皱起眉头。

“Moments don’t stay.”

“片刻是留不住的。”

---

Liang picked up the cracked teacup and handed it to her.

梁拿起那只裂纹茶杯，递给她。

“Listen.”

“你听。”

---

At first, she heard nothing but the soft tapping of rain.

一开始，她只听见细碎的雨声。

Then—

然后——

A train passing somewhere far away.

远处驶过的一列火车。

Children laughing under a shared umbrella.

一把伞下孩子们的笑声。

A door closing gently, as if someone didn’t want to leave.

一扇轻轻关上的门，像是不舍离开的人。

---

She pulled the cup away, startled.

她猛地把茶杯移开，吃了一惊。

“That’s impossible.”

“这不可能。”

---

Liang shrugged.

梁耸了耸肩。

“Rain remembers where it has been.”

“雨记得它去过哪里。”

---

After that, Mrs. Sato began leaving things on her own balcony.

从那以后，佐藤太太也开始在自己的阳台上放东西。

A spoon.

一把勺子。

A flower pot.

一个花盆。

Once, even her cat—though it ran away before the rain started.

有一次甚至是她的猫——不过在下雨前就跑掉了。

---

One afternoon, when the sky was clear, she knocked again.

某个晴朗的下午，她再次敲门。

“I listened,” she said.

“我听了，”她说。

---

“And?”

“然后呢？”

---

“I heard my husband.”

“我听见了我丈夫。”

---

Liang didn’t ask more.

梁没有再问。

Some things, like rain, should not be held too tightly.

有些东西，就像雨，不该被握得太紧。

---

That night, no one put anything on the balcony.

那天晚上，两人都没有在阳台上放东西。

---

But when the rain came—

但当雨落下的时候——

They both listened.

他们都在听。
