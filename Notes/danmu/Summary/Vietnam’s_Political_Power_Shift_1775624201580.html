Vietnam’s Political Power Shift

🔗 阅读原文

越南的权力转变

---

Paragraph 1

Vietnam’s top leader To Lam has consolidated his authority after being unanimously elected as both president and head of the Communist Party, giving him an unusually strong concentration of power.

越南最高领导人苏林在全票当选国家主席和共产党总书记后进一步巩固了权力，使他在国家政治体系中拥有罕见的高度集中权力。

---

Paragraph 2

His rise follows years of influence as the minister overseeing national security and leading a sweeping anti‑corruption campaign that removed many political rivals.

他的上升源于多年担任公安部长期间主导全国反腐行动，这场行动清除了许多潜在的政治竞争者。

---

Paragraph 3

To Lam temporarily assumed both top roles in 2024 after the previous president resigned and the party chief passed away, but some factions—especially within the military—were initially opposed to him keeping both positions.

在前任国家主席辞职、总书记去世后，苏林于 2024 年暂时接任两项最高职务，但党内部分派系，尤其是军方，最初反对他长期兼任这两个职位。

---

Paragraph 4

Vietnam traditionally prefers collective leadership among several senior positions, yet To Lam has now secured enough support to lead for the next five years, drawing comparisons to China’s model of centralized authority.

越南传统上倾向于由多名高层共同领导，但苏林如今获得足够支持，将在未来五年继续掌权，这也引发了外界对其权力集中程度与中国模式的比较。

---

Paragraph 5

Despite historical anti‑China sentiment among the public, the ruling parties of Vietnam and China maintain close ties, and Chinese leader Xi Jinping has publicly expressed support for To Lam.

尽管越南民间长期存在反华情绪，但两国执政党关系密切，中国领导人习近平也公开表达了对苏林的支持。

---

Paragraph 6

Analysts note that Vietnam’s central committee still acts as a check on the top leader, meaning To Lam must continue building alliances within the party to maintain stability.

分析人士指出，越南中央委员会仍对最高领导人形成制衡，这意味着苏林必须继续在党内建立联盟以维持稳定。

---

Paragraph 7

The biggest challenge ahead is whether he can deliver the ambitious economic goals he has set, including rapid growth and continued anti‑corruption efforts aimed at transforming Vietnam into an upper‑income country.

未来最大的挑战在于他能否实现自己设定的宏大经济目标，包括保持高速增长、持续反腐，并推动越南在未来几十年迈入高收入国家行列。
