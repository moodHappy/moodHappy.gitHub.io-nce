India’s economy faces a Middle East oil shock

🔗 阅读原文

印度经济正面临来自中东的石油冲击

---

India’s recent high‑growth momentum has been disrupted as the Middle East conflict pushes up energy risks and weakens the rupee.

随着中东冲突加剧能源风险并导致卢比走弱，印度近期的高速增长势头受到冲击。

---

The currency has fallen sharply, and analysts warn that prolonged conflict could trigger even steeper declines and broader economic stress.

印度货币大幅贬值，分析人士警告若冲突持续，可能出现更深度下跌并引发更广泛的经济压力。

---

Rising import costs, potential drops in remittances, and higher logistics expenses are beginning to weigh on inflation and growth expectations.

进口成本上升、侨汇可能减少以及物流费用增加，正开始压低通胀与增长预期。

---

India’s growth forecast may fall by up to one percentage point, delaying its ambition to become the world’s fourth‑largest economy.

印度的增长预测可能下调最多一个百分点，使其成为世界第四大经济体的目标进一步推迟。

---

Although fuel prices at the pump remain stable due to government intervention, shortages of natural gas and LPG are already disrupting restaurants, factories, and households.

尽管政府干预使零售燃油价格保持稳定，但天然气和液化气短缺已开始影响餐馆、工厂和家庭。

---

Economists warn of a possible stagflation shock, as rising prices combine with slowing activity and early signs of migrant workers returning home.

经济学家警告可能出现滞胀冲击，因为物价上涨与经济活动放缓并存，且出现外来务工人员提前返乡的迹象。

---

The government has announced a stabilisation fund and increased subsidies, but experts say the measures remain modest compared with the scale of the challenge.

政府宣布设立稳定基金并增加补贴，但专家认为这些措施与挑战规模相比仍显不足。

---

While a weaker rupee could help exports, the crisis highlights India’s urgent need to diversify energy sources and accelerate its transition to renewables.

尽管较弱的卢比可能有利出口，但这场危机凸显印度迫切需要多元化能源来源并加速向可再生能源转型。
